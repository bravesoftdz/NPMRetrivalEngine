        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">Google video: Safe Search for Images</h1>
        <p class="entry-meta"><time class="entry-time" itemprop="datePublished" datetime="2012-02-10T16:08:31+00:00">February 10, 2012</time> modified <time class="entry-modified-time" itemprop="dateModified" datetime="2012-03-09T17:22:19+00:00">March 9, 2012</time> <br><span class="entry-categories">Filed Under: <a href="http://www.bewebsmart.com/category/safe-search/" rel="category tag">Safe Search</a></span></p>

       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>I just stumbled upon a video that shows you the Safe Search settings for Google that Ive mentioned in <a title="Kids Safe Searching: Bing and Yahoo beat Google" href="http://www.bewebsmart.com/2011/11/08/safe-search-yahoo-bing-and-google/">previous posts</a>. &nbsp;This video is straight from the source, produced by the Google team. &nbsp;While this video is specific to images, the safe search settings will apply to all your Google searches. &nbsp;(But dont forget to LOCK the strict setting if you have smart kiddos who share your computer).</p>
        <p>I recently used the <a title="How to Report an offensive image in Google image search results" href="http://support.google.com/images/bin/answer.py?hl=en&amp;answer=112750" target="_blank">Report Offensive Images</a> feature to let Google know that their strict setting wasnt strict enough for my liking! Pretty easy to do.</p>
