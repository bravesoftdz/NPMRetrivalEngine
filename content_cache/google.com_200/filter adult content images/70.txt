        <h2 id="description-header">Description</h2> 
        <p>A plugin that provides a warning box with a ton more options completely re-written from the ground up.</p> 
        <blockquote> 
         <p>This plugin is on a maintenance hold, if you’re interested in taking over maintenance on this plugin with 4,000+ active installs, please let me know directly. Via my website, or email in the plugin description. Support may resume at a later date.</p> 
        </blockquote> 
        <h4>Support Requests</h4> 
        <p>All support requests should be on the <a href="https://github.com/JayWood/content-warning-v3" rel="nofollow">Github Repository</a>.<br> <strong>SUPPORT IS ON HOLD</strong></p> 
        <h4>Want to Contribute?</h4> 
        <p>Make a <a href="https://github.com/JayWood/content-warning-v3" rel="nofollow">Pull Request</a> to the Github’s project development branch. Don’t forget to read the <a href="https://github.com/JayWood/content-warning-v3/wiki/Dev-Documentation" rel="nofollow">Development Documentation</a> for nifty hooks that may help.</p> 
        <p>A WordPress Plugin to allow site owners to display an acceptance dialog to their users and have that follow them throughout the site. This plugin allows you to do redirect users if they decline,<br> and show popups only on single posts, pages, or categories. ie. if a user accepts on <code>Post A</code>, but no <code>Post B</code>, you can force them to accept on a per-post/page/category basis. You can also set<br> the popup to site-wide, meaning the first time they accept, they will not see it again.</p> 
        <p>This plugin allows you to do the following:</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li>Gate Individual Posts</li> 
         <li>Gate Individual Pages</li> 
         <li>Gate Individual Categories</li> 
         <li>Specify cookie time ( in days ) – or just for the browser session</li> 
         <li>Block or redirect users who previously denied your terms.</li> 
         <li>Customize enter &amp; exit links</li> 
         <li>Customize the message, enter, and exit text</li> 
         <li>Customize the CSS in the settings page</li> 
         <li>Customize the background color via a simple color selector, along with it’s opacity</li> 
         <li>Blanket protect misc. pages like search and archives</li> 
        </ul> 
        <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;"><iframe class="youtube-player" type="text/html" width="640" height="390" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/0_ZNojpYuwk?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;autohide=2&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" allowfullscreen="true" style="border:0;"></iframe></span> 
        <p><em>NOTE:</em></p> 
        <blockquote> 
         <p>If the exit link is left empty, users will be redirected to google.</p>

        <h2 id="installation-header">Installation</h2> 
        <h4>Default Method</h4> 
        <ol> 
         <li>Go to Settings &gt; Plugins in your administrator panel.</li> 
         <li>Click <code>Add New</code></li> 
         <li>Search for Content Warning v2</li> 
         <li>Click install.</li> 
        </ol> 
        <h4>Easy Method</h4> 
        <ol> 
         <li>Download the zip file.</li> 
         <li>Login to your <code>Dashboard</code></li> 
         <li>Open your plugins bar and click <code>Add New</code></li> 
         <li>Click the <code>upload tab</code></li> 
         <li>Choose <code>content-warning-v2</code> from your downloads folder</li> 
         <li>Click <code>Install Now</code></li> 
         <li>All done, now just activate the plugin</li> 
         <li>Go to CWv3 menu and configure</li> 
         <li>Save, and you’re all good.</li> 
        </ol> 
        <h4>Old Method</h4> 
        <ol> 
         <li>Upload <code>content-warning-v2</code> to the <code>/wp-content/plugins/</code> directory</li> 
         <li>Aktiver utvidelsen via «Utvidelser»-menyen i WordPress</li> 
        </ol>

          <p>All support questions should be directed to the github issues section, just <a href="https://github.com/JayWood/content-warning-v3/issues" rel="nofollow">open a ticket</a>.</p> 
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          How to get support
         </dt> 
         <dd> 
          <ul> 
           <li>Provide a detailed explination of what the issue is, <code>it's broken</code> tickets will be closed.</li> 
           <li>Detail what you have done to try and fix the problem.</li> 
           <li>Provide a detailed list of options that are set.</li> 
           <li>Give a link to your site or the affected page.</li> 
          </ul> 
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          The plugin is broken, fix it.
         </dt> 
         <dd> 
          <p>Please do not contact me with questions like this. If you cannot be descriptive with your problem I cannot help you.</p> 
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          I’m still seeing the dialog even after I clicked enter/exit
         </dt> 
         <dd> 
          <ul> 
           <li>If your browser does not have cookies enabled, the plugin will not store your response.</li> 
           <li>Also, if you’re using google chrome, this is a bug that unfortunately I have been unable to squash. Hitting F5 or refresh will fix it.</li> 
          </ul> 
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          Plugin installed &amp; activated, but not showing up
         </dt> 
         <dd> 
          <ul> 
           <li>Verify your theme contains a <code>wp_header()</code> and <code>wp_footer()</code> tag, without it, this plugin will <strong>NOT</strong> function.</li> 
          </ul> 
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          Dialog mis-aligned, partially working?
         </dt> 
         <dd> 
          <ul> 
           <li>Could be that your theme’s css has broken the dialog</li> 
           <li>Another plugin may be breaking the javascript, I suggest you <a href="https://github.com/JayWood/content-warning-v3/issues" rel="nofollow">open an issue</a> and provide a screen shot of your debug console.</li> 
          </ul> 
         </dd> 
        </dl>
