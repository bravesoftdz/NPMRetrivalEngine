         <h2><a id="user-content-intro" class="anchor" href="#intro" aria-hidden="true">
           <svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewbox="0 0 16 16" width="16">
            <path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path>
           </svg></a>Intro</h2> 
         <p>Through our City SDK, we are aiming to provide a user-friendly "toolbox" for civic hackers to connect local and national public data. The creation of the SDK came out of the desire to make it easier to use the Census API for common tasks that our developer community asked for. We have been engaging developers around the country for the past two years and have observed how they use the API. Here we have packaged the most commonly needed functionalities typically built on top of our API, saving the developer from having to do it herself.</p> 
         <h2><a id="user-content-features" class="anchor" href="#features" aria-hidden="true">
           <svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewbox="0 0 16 16" width="16">
            <path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path>
           </svg></a>Features</h2> 
         <ul> 
          <li>Never have to figure out what your FIPS code is again! Just pass in lat/longs, we handle the translation. Only have ZIP codes? No problem, we translate those too.</li> 
          <li>Get values and Census geographic boundaries (currently GeoJSON only: down to ‘block-group’ level) with a SINGLE CALL (whoohoo)!</li> 
          <li>A <a href="http://uscensusbureau.github.io/citysdk/guides.html">modular architecture</a> which makes mashing Census data up with <a href="http://uscensusbureau.github.io/citysdk/gallery.html">third-party data</a> a snap.</li> 
          <li>Pull down Census Bureau geographic boundaries by sending your own custom geography in the request (currently GeoJSON support only, <a href="http://terraformer.io/">Terraformer.io</a>)</li> 
          <li>A <a href="https://uscensusbureau.github.io/citysdk/examples/geoRequest/">showcase of examples</a> to help you get started.</li> 
          <li>We’ve provided a starting list of aliases for some of our most popular variables (most aliases are in the American Community Survey [ACS5])</li> 
          <li>More coming soon! (you may also add to our issues using the <a href="https://github.com/uscensusbureau/citysdk/issues?q=is%3Aopen+is%3Aissue+label%3A%22user+stories%22">#user stories</a> label to make feature requests)</li> 
         </ul>
