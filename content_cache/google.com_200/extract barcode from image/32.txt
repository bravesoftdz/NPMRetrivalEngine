        <h1 class="entry-title">Barcode Scanners: How Do They Work?</h1> 
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="date published time" title="2014-03-25T09:50:19+00:00">March 25, 2014</span> by 
         <span class="author vcard"><span class="fn"><a href="http://www.waspbarcode.com/buzz/author/ptrujillo/" title="Paul Trujillo" rel="author">Paul Trujillo</a></span></span> 
         <span class="post-comments"><a href="http://www.waspbarcode.com/buzz/how-barcode-scanners-work/#comments"><span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="5664 http://www.waspbarcode.com/buzz/?p=5664">7 Comments</span></a></span> 
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p class="entry-tagline"></p>
         <p><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-5662" alt="how-scanners-work-banner" src="http://buzz.waspbarcode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/how-scanners-work-banner.jpg" width="940" height="330" srcset="http://buzz.waspbarcode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/how-scanners-work-banner-560x196.jpg 560w, http://buzz.waspbarcode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/how-scanners-work-banner.jpg 940w" sizes="(max-width: 940px) 100vw, 940px"></p> 
         <p>As consumers, we see <a href="http://www.waspbarcode.com/ " target="_blank">barcodes</a> used all the time: purchasing from any retail store, renting a car, attending major events, flying, and even going to the doctor. &nbsp;Barcodes arent just lines appearing on tickets or inventory items, barcode scanning systems help businesses track an amazing amount of information which, in turn, increases productivity and efficiency. You will improve your business process (saving time and money) by understanding how barcodes work and by knowing how to use them effectively in partnership with a quality <a href="http://www.waspbarcode.com/barcode-scanners" target="_blank">barcode scanner</a>.</p> 
         <h2>The Barcode</h2>

           <iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/IU7edcc8qEk" height="315" width="560" allowfullscreen frameborder="0"></iframe>
           <p></p> 
           <address>2D Barcode Scanner (USB) for Inventory  WDI4600</address> 
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <p>In June of 1974, the first barcode appeared on a pack of Wrigley Company chewing gum.&nbsp; Since then, barcodes can be found on almost every item for purchase within a store. A barcode is used to encode information in a visual pattern readable by a machine. Barcodes are used for a variety of reasons including tracking products, prices, and stock levels for centralized recording in a computer software system.</p> 
         <p>There are two types of barcodes  linear and 2D. The most visually recognizable, the UPC (<a href="http://electronics.howstuffworks.com/gadgets/high-tech-gadgets/upc.htm" target="_blank">Universal Product Code</a>), is a linear barcode made up of two parts: the barcode and the 12-digit UPC number. The first six numbers of the barcode is the manufacturers identification number. The next five digits represent the items number. The last number is called a check digit which enables the scanner to determine if the barcode was scanned correctly or not.</p> 
         <p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5666" alt="barcode" src="http://buzz.waspbarcode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/barcode.jpg" width="126" height="92"></p> 
         <p>A linear barcode typically holds any type of text information.&nbsp; In contrast, a 2D barcode is more complex and can include more information in the code: price, quantity, web address or image. A linear barcode scanner cant read a 2D barcode; requiring the use of an image scanner for reading the information embedded in a 2D barcode.</p> 
         <p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5667" alt="qr" src="http://buzz.waspbarcode.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/qr.jpg" width="135" height="135"></p> 
         <p>Check out Wasps <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MXCiGNSvqdw" target="_blank">What is a Barcode, Anyway</a>? video&nbsp;to learn the basics of barcodes in under a minute.<br> <iframe src="//www.youtube.com/v/MXCiGNSvqdw" height="450" width="800" allowfullscreen frameborder="0"></iframe></p> 
         <h2>The Barcode Scanner</h2> 
         <p>Most barcode scanners consist of three different parts including the illumination system, the sensor, and the decoder. In general, a barcode scanner scans the black and white elements of a barcode by illuminating the code with a red light, which is then converted into matching text. More specifically, the sensor in the barcode scanner detects the reflected light from the illumination system (the red light) and generates an analog signal that is sent to the decoder. The decoder interprets that signal, validates the barcode using the check digit, and coverts it into text.</p> 
         <p>This converted text is delivered by the scanner to a computer software system holding a database of the maker, cost, and quantity of all products sold. This video is a quick <a href="http://www.waspbarcode.com/barcode-scanners/videos/barcode-scanner-comparison " target="_blank">lesson in barcode scanners</a> and highlights the basic differences between a Contact Scanner, Laser Scanner, and an Imager.</p> 
         <p>Because barcode scanners are variable and include diverse capabilities, some are better suited for certain industries due to reading distance and to work volume capacity.</p> 
         <div> 
          <hr> 
          <p>Outlined below are a few of the available barcode scanners with a little insight into how each works.</p> 
          <ul> 
           <li><b>Pen-type Reader:</b> consists of a light source and a photodiode on the tip of the pen.</li> 
           <li><b>Laser Scanner:</b> works similarly to a Pen-type Reader but uses a laser beam.</li> 
           <li><b>Camera-based Reader: </b>installed with camera and image processing techniques in the reading of barcodes.</li> 
           <li><b>CCD Reader:</b> has several light sensors to scan barcodes.</li> 
           <li><b>Omni-Directional Barcode Scanner:</b> highly advanced and very efficient in decoding badly printed, crumpled, and even torn barcodes on products.</li> 
          </ul>
