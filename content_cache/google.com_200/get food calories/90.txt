             <div class="swmidtitle" style="font-size:1.3em">
              About WIC 
             </div> 
             <div class="swcontent" style="font-size:1em;margin-top:5px">
              WIC is a federally funded program that provides healthy supplemental foods and nutrition counseling for pregnant women, new mothers, infants and children under age five. The program has an extraordinary 35-year record of preventing children's health problems and improving their long-term health, growth and development. WIC serves over 9.1 million women, infants and children through over 10,000 clinics nationwide. In 2013, Maryland WIC served over 143,000 women, infants and children each month.
             </div> 
             <div class="swmidtitle" style="font-size:1.3em">
              Effects of WIC Participation on Pregnant Women 
              <img align="right" class="ms-rtePosition-2" alt="mom with a girl" src="/wic/img/mdwic_vs945646_mom-children.png" style="width:280px"> 
             </div> 
             <div class="swcontent">
              Numerous studies have shown that pregnant women who participate in WIC have longer pregnancies leading to fewer premature births; have fewer low&nbsp;birth-weight babies; exp erience fewer fetal and infant deaths; seek prenatal care earlier in pregnancy and consume more of such key nutrients as iron, protein, calcium and vitamin C.
             </div> 
             <div class="swmidtitle" style="font-size:1.3em">
              Effects of WIC Participation on Children
             </div> 
             <div class="swcontent" style="font-size:1em;margin-top:5px">
              Low-income children enrolled in the WIC Program have a lower&nbsp;prevalence of anemia than those who are not enrolled.&nbsp; One study found that the anemia rate among children at the&nbsp;six-month WIC recertification visit was lower than the rate at the initial WIC screening, indicating the positive effect of WIC participation.&nbsp; Other studies found that four and five-year-olds whose mothers participated in WIC during pregnancy had better vocabulary test scores than children whose mothers had not received WIC benefits.
             </div> 
             <div class="swmidtitle" style="font-size:1.3em">
              WIC's Guiding Principles
             </div> 
             <div class="swcontent" style="font-size:1em;margin-top:5px">
              <ul type="disc">
               <li>We will treat all people with understanding, dignity and respect.</li> 
               <li>We will continuously evaluate and improve the program.</li> 
               <li>We will assure maximum program integrity, efficiency and accountability.</li> 
               <li>We will promote the importance of the program within the community and assure equal accessibility.</li> 
               <li>We will provide information and encouragement that will empower families to make positive decisions about nutrition.</li>
              </ul>
             </div> 
             <div class="swmidtitle" style="font-size:1.3em">
              WIC's Vision
             </div> 
             <div class="swcontent" style="font-size:1em;margin-top:5px">
              We envision a future Maryland WIC program that...
             </div> 
             <ul class="swcontent" type="disc" style="font-size:1em;margin-top:5px">
              <li>Promotes the optimal health and nutritional status of future generations and operates as an integral component of a comprehensive, efficient and interactive health care system; and</li> 
              <li>Insures the health and well being of women, infants and children in need through the provision of preventative services, including nutrition education, selected supplemental foods and health referrals.</li>
             </ul>
