         <h1>Calories and Kilocalories (kcal)</h1> 
         <div class="social-media"> 
          <div class="fb-like" data-layout="button_count" data-action="like" data-show-faces="false" data-share="false"></div>

         <p class="intro">It's easy to get confused about calories and kilocalories since, in a nutrition context, values are actually given for the number of kilocalories in a food, but referred to simply as calories.</p> 
         <h2>In scientific terms:</h2> 
         <p>1000 calories = 1 kilocalorie = 1 kcal = the energy it takes to raise the temperature of 1kg of water by 1캜.</p> 
         <h2>In nutrition terms:</h2> 
         <p>ie. What you'll find on food packaging, calories = kilocalories and are used interchangeably.</p> 
         <p>In some instances food energy is measured in kilojoules - mostly by the scientific community - though some food packaging also gives kilojoule (kJ) values. 1 kilocalorie = 4.2 kilojoules.</p> 
         <h2>Counting Calories</h2> 
         <p>Most medical professionals agree that making sure that calories consumed are less than calories expended is the best way to lose weight - find out more about <a href="calories/calorie_counting.htm"> calorie counting</a>.</p> 
         <h2>How Many Calories Do You Need?</h2> 
         <p>Daily calorie requirements vary from person to person - find out more about <a href="logout/calorie_intake.htm">calorie intake</a></p>
