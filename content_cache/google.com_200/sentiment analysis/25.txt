      <h1>afinn 0.1</h1> 
      <div id="download-button"> 
       <a class="button green" style="float:right;" href="https://pypi.python.org/packages/86/e5/ffbb7ee3cca21ac6d310ac01944fb163c20030b45bda25421d725d8a859a/afinn-0.1.tar.gz">Download<br> <span style="font-size: 75%">afinn-0.1.tar.gz</span> </a> 
      </div> 
      <p style="font-style: italic">AFINN sentiment analysis</p> 
      <p>AFINN sentiment analysis in Python: Wordlist-based approach for sentiment analysis.</p> 
      <div id="examples"> 
       <h2>Examples</h2> 
       <blockquote> 
        <pre>&gt;&gt;&gt; from afinn import Afinn
&gt;&gt;&gt; afinn = Afinn()
&gt;&gt;&gt; afinn.score('This is utterly excellent!')
3.0
</pre> 
       </blockquote> 
       <p>In Danish:</p> 
       <blockquote> 
        <pre>&gt;&gt;&gt; afinn = Afinn(language='da')
&gt;&gt;&gt; afinn.score('Hvis ikke det er det mest afskyelige flueknepperi...')
-6.0
</pre> 
       </blockquote> 
       <p>With emoticons:</p> 
       <blockquote> 
        <pre>&gt;&gt;&gt; afinn = Afinn(emoticons=True)
&gt;&gt;&gt; afinn.score('I saw that yesterday :)')
2.0
</pre> 
       </blockquote> 
       <p>With multiple sentences (here with data from an Austen novel available in Gutenberg):</p> 
       <blockquote> 
        <pre>&gt;&gt;&gt; from afinn import Afinn
&gt;&gt;&gt; from nltk.corpus import gutenberg
&gt;&gt;&gt; import textwrap
&gt;&gt;&gt; afinn = Afinn()
&gt;&gt;&gt; sentences = (" ".join(wordlist) for wordlist in gutenberg.sents('austen-sense.txt'))
&gt;&gt;&gt; scored_sentences = ((afinn.score(sent), sent) for sent in sentences)
&gt;&gt;&gt; sorted_sentences = sorted(scored_sentences)
&gt;&gt;&gt; print("\n".join(textwrap.wrap(sorted_sentences[0][1], 70)))
To attach myself to your sister , therefore , was not a thing to be
thought of ;-- and with a meanness , selfishness , cruelty -- which no
indignant , no contemptuous look , even of yours , Miss Dashwood , can
ever reprobate too much -- I was acting in this manner , trying to
engage her regard , without a thought of returning it .-- But one
thing may be said for me : even in that horrid state of selfish vanity
, I did not know the extent of the injury I meditated , because I did
not THEN know what it was to love .
</pre> 
       </blockquote>
