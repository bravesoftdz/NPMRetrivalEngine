           <h1 name="4971" id="4971" class="graf graf--h3 graf--leading graf--title"><strong class="markup--strong markup--h3-strong">Tracking my movements on the football pitch with&nbsp;Fitbit</strong></h1>
           <p name="3402" id="3402" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">I recently celebrated my birthday, and my wonderful girlfriend offered me a smartwatch in order to track my fitness activity. I mostly run and play football, and when it comes to track your movements while jogging, my brand new Fitbit Surge does the job almost perfectly.</p>
           <figure name="5ab8" id="5ab8" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p">

           <p name="b303" id="b303" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">I decided to test its effectiveness on the football field, so I wore it during a game in Paris. Fitbit allows you to export your data in a&nbsp;.TCX format. I did it, and then imported it in Google Earth to check whether the GPS was accurate or not.</p>
           <p name="26ca" id="26ca" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">You can see the result on the screenshot below. I think this is pretty neat, and reflects pretty much my style. I play defending midfielder, and you can see I try to cover as much area as possible&nbsp;!</p>

           <p name="6a52" id="6a52" class="graf graf--p graf--leading">Anyway, I wanted to do something else with those data, so I didn’t stopped here. Indeed, I wanted to learn <a href="https://d3js.org/" data-href="https://d3js.org/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">D3.js</a> for several months, and it was the perfect opportunity to do just that&nbsp;!</p>
           <p name="0ade" id="0ade" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I thought that it wouldn’t take long, but as I was pretty new to javascript, it took me <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">waaayyy</strong> more time than I expected. The language is really different from Python or R, and I spent several hours making those transitions work.</p>
           <p name="0a1d" id="0a1d" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Basically, I wanted to represent my movements on the pitch every second, to see myself moving dynamically, and being able to press play and pause. Also, I wanted to change the color of the circle (representing my position every second) with the value of my heart rate. It starts as light green, and becomes really red after a sprint or a long period running. You can check the clock of the game at the top right of the field.</p>

          <div class="section-inner sectionLayout--insetColumn">
           <p name="8538" id="8538" class="graf graf--p graf--leading">While Fitbit does a pretty good job to track the position, the GPS is not always working perfectly, and sometimes its behaviour is weird. Indeed, it is not “fit” to football, which require a specific type of tracking.</p>
           <p name="0221" id="0221" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I think (or I hope) that new (cheap) connected objects will emerge to allow both professional &amp; amateur football players to gather exhaustive statistics on their performance. Imagine a world where everybody would wore a connected object and you could “replay” the game afterward.</p>
           <p name="652d" id="652d" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Those objets should be small and light enough that we could wear it while playing. I wore my watch to gather those data, but I think it is not allowed, as it could hurt other players. It should be almost invisible&nbsp;!</p>
           <p name="0172" id="0172" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">This would be a first step toward “Football science”, where we would better track our progress. Moreover, we would be able to attribute the outcome of a game to the performance of every players. Currently, the striker takes all the merit when he scores goals. While he is arguably one of the most important player in a team, we should be able to explain <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">quantitatively</strong> how other players play a determinant role in a game. But this is a topic for another post.</p>
