         <h2></h2> 
         <p>This a step by step guide on how to setup your own crypto-currency mining pool.</p> 
         <p><strong><span style="color: #ff0000;">This guide is out-dated, it has now been replaced by <a href="https://blockgen.net/blog/setup-your-own-mining-pool/">Setup Your Own Mining Pool</a>.&nbsp;Please refer to the new guide if you want to setup your own mining pool. This guide will still exist and serve as a legacy guide for reference. All things in this guide still work, however MPOS and NOMP are out-dated. We highly suggest you use the <a href="https://blockgen.net/blog/setup-your-own-mining-pool/" target="_blank">New Guide.&nbsp;</a></span></strong></p> 
         <p>I have ran large mining pool operations, and have helped out at several other operations. I am creating this guide because I feel like more people should have the ability to run their own pool, whether it be for their own miners or just out of curiosity to understand how it all works. This is by no means meant to be a guide so you can setup your own professional mining<span id="more-29"></span> pool operation. Running your own mining pool that other miners other then yourself will use is not an easy undertaking, and requires extensive systems administration experience as well as a large budget, patience, troubleshooting skills, and a solid knowledge of how crypto-currencies work. I will only be going over basic security, such as firewall setup.</p> 
         <p>This guide is going over how to setup an MPOS (Mining Portal Open Source) Pool using NOMP as stratum (Node Open Mining Portal). This is meant to setup a mining pool for a SINGLE CRYPTO CURRENCY. This is not a guide for a Multipool!!!!</p> 
         <p>If you want to see what it looks like before you set it all up, head to the&nbsp;<a href="http://miningpool.website/index.php" target="_blank">Example Pool</a>&nbsp;that was built completely off of this guide and associated guides.</p> 
         <p>&nbsp;</p> 
         <h2><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Guide Requirements</strong></span></h2> 
         <p>-VPS with at least 1GB of Ram, I just deployed an instance on <a href="http://vultr.com/" target="_blank">http://vultr.com</a> for this guide since I had a free $5 coupon and it’s got a cheap hourly rate, just google “Vultr Coupons” and you’ll probably find something. You can also host a virtual machine on your own PC if you want to at no cost, just google it.<br> -Ubuntu 14.04 x64<br> -Putty (<a href="http://www.chiark.greenend.org.uk/~sgtatham/putty/download.html" target="_blank">http://www.chiark.greenend.org.uk/~sgtatham/putty/download.html</a>)<br> -WinSCP (<a href="http://winscp.net/eng/download.php" target="_blank">http://winscp.net/eng/download.php</a>)<br> -Chrome Web Browser (it’s what I am using to view web pages, can’t guarantee it’ll work on other browsers).<br> -Very basic knowledge of Linux</p> 
         <p>This guide will probably take you a long time, especially if you are new to Linux. I highly suggest you be patient, and take it one step at a time.</p> 
         <p>I will be using litecoin (scrypt), I will not be going into specific of how to host something like darkcoin (x11) or other algorithms. It is not very hard though to change algorithms, maybe I will add to the guide at a later date.</p> 
         <p>This guide is meant for novices, things like webmin and phpmyadmin are not required and only useful to novices. If you are an experienced sys admin you should be able to adapt and figure out ways around that.</p> 
         <p>If you have the ability to snapshot your VPS, then I suggest you do that everytime you complete a step. It will save you a lot of time if you make a mistake.</p> 
         <p>All shell commands will be surrounded with a code box like this:</p> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>shell command</code>
          </div> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr"></div> 
         </div> 
         <p>Information I want you to insert into a file, or somewhere else will be surrounded by quote tags (Ignore the Quote: !) :</p> 
         <blockquote>
          <p><cite>Quote:&nbsp;</cite></p> 
          <p>info for a file</p> 
          <p>&nbsp;</p>
         </blockquote> 
         <h2><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>VPS Setup</strong></span></h2> 
         <p>At this point you should have your VPS started, putty up and running and your logged in as root.</p> 
         <p>Let’s go ahead and setup the VPS before we get into the meat and potatoes:</p> 
         <h3><strong>Update Ubuntu</strong></h3> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>apt-get update</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>apt-get dist-upgrade</code>
          </div> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr"></div> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr"></div> 
         </div> 
         <p>&nbsp;</p> 
         <h3><strong>Setup SWAP</strong></h3> 
         <p>By default there is no swap setup on my Vultr vps, it is required especially on a system with limited memory. I am setting up a 4GB swap, which may be overkill but it’s the most common swap sized used in most guides so ill keep it at that.</p> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>dd if=/dev/zero of=/mnt/myswap.swap bs=1M count=4000</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>mkswap /mnt/myswap.swap</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>swapon /mnt/myswap.swap</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <p>Now let’s add it into fstab so it’ll activate at boot.</p> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>nano /etc/fstab</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <p>Add the following line at the end of the file.</p> 
         <blockquote>
          <p><cite>Quote:</cite></p> 
          <p><cite>&nbsp;</cite>/mnt/myswap.swap none swap sw 0 0</p>
         </blockquote> 
         <p>Should look like this:<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/jujuBxG.png" alt="[Image: jujuBxG.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>Ctrl+O to save, and Ctrl+X to exit the nano editor.</p> 
         <p>Now your swap is setup, you can modify the size in the future if you need more or less.</p> 
         <p>-Install Required Packages</p> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>apt-get install git</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>apt-get install build-essential libboost-all-dev libcurl4-openssl-dev libdb5.1-dev libdb5.1++-dev mysql-server</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <p>Set a MySQL Root Password (different from your Ubuntu root pass!)<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/4W8Uv6K.png" alt="[Image: 4W8Uv6K.png]" border="0"><br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/VJk0oka.png" alt="[Image: VJk0oka.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>&nbsp;</p> 
         <h3><strong>Install Required Packages for Mining Portal Open Source (MPOS)</strong></h3> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>apt-get install memcached php5-memcached php5-mysqlnd php5-curl php5-json libapache2-mod-php5</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <p>Let’s get Apache Web Server Going:</p> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>apache2ctl -k stop; sleep 2; sudo apache2ctl -k start</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <p>You will get a message stating it could not reliably determine your servers domain name, don’t worry about this right now. The web server is still running, just go to your server’s ip (http://youserverip)&nbsp;in chrome and you should see this:<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/fS6sluI.png" alt="[Image: fS6sluI.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>If you want to setup a DNS server so you can utilize a domain name, then go to google. There is literally 100’s or even 1000’s of guides on that topic. For this guide we will just be working off the servers ip. After you finish this guide, you can checkout <a href="https://blockgen.net/blog/utilize-cloudflare-as-a-dns-server-2/">Utilize CloudFlare as a DNS Server</a>&nbsp;for a quick and easy way to have a registered domain point at your pool.</p> 
         <p>&nbsp;</p> 
         <h3><strong>Install Required Packages for NOMP Stratum</strong></h3> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>curl –sL https://deb.nodesource.com/setup | bash -</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>apt-get install -y nodejs</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>npm install –g npm</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>npm install forever -g</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>apt-add-repository ppa:chris-lea/redis-server</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>apt-get update</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>apt-get install redis-server</code>
          </div> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr"></div> 
         </div> 
         <p>&nbsp;</p> 
         <h3><strong>Setup Webmin</strong></h3> 
         <p>Webmin is a GUI interface for managing your server. It’s very powerful and feature packed, and it’s free. It’s great for linux beginners as well. Keep in mind that Webmin is not officially supported under Ubuntu, the things we do in this guide will work but I can’t guarantee that everything in Webmin will work if you use it for other things.</p> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>nano /etc/apt/sources.list</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <p>Press the Down Arrow on your keyboard to reach the end, and add these two lines below:</p> 
         <blockquote>
          <p><cite>Quote:</cite></p> 
          <p>deb <a href="http://download.webmin.com/download/repository" target="_blank">http://download.webmin.com/download/repository</a> sarge contrib<br> deb <a href="http://webmin.mirror.somersettechsolutions.co.uk/repository" target="_blank">http://webmin.mirror.somersettechsolutions.co.uk/repository</a> sarge contrib</p>
         </blockquote> 
         <p>Should look like this:<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/vs6Jc57.png" alt="[Image: vs6Jc57.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>Press Ctrl+O to save,&nbsp;and exit the nano editor&nbsp;with CTRL+X</p> 
         <p>Let’s get Webmin’s GPG Key</p> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>cd /root</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>wget http://www.webmin.com/jcameron-key.asc</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>apt-key add jcameron-key.asc</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <p>Install Webmin</p> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>apt-get update</code>
          </div> 
         </div>

         <p>Now we need to update the Apache configuration a bit.</p> 
         <p>-Click on “Global Configuration” on the top center left, screenshot:<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/h1j7cOq.png" alt="[Image: h1j7cOq.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>-Click on “Edit Config Files”<br> -Scroll all the way to the bottom of the config file<br> -Add this to the bottom, and update it according to your username obviously.</p> 
         <blockquote>
          <p><cite>Quote:</cite>&lt;Directory /home/username/mpos/public&gt;<br> Options Indexes FollowSymLinks<br> AllowOverride None<br> Require all granted<br> &lt;/Directory&gt;</p>
         </blockquote> 
         <p>Should look like this:<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/pIVjVxt.png" alt="[Image: pIVjVxt.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>-Click Save<br> -Click on “Apply Changes” in the type right of Webmin.<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/BgzZMDp.png" alt="[Image: BgzZMDp.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>Now let’s see if the MPOS frontend is working, go to http://yourserverip<br> You should see a page that looks like this:<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/mf3zaJv.png" alt="[Image: mf3zaJv.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>If you see the MPOS frontpage, then go ahead and skip to the “MPOS Frontend Initial” step.</p> 
         <p>If you get a blank page, don’t worry it may be a permissions issue.<br> Login to root on ssh.</p> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>cd /home/username/mpos</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>chown -R www-data templates/compile templates/cache logs</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <p>Now head back to http://yourserverip and see if MPOS shows up. If it does not at this point, you should go back in the guide and make sure you did everything right. If that does not work, then boot up WinSCP and go to /var/log/apache2/error.log and read the bottom line (it should mention your ip), see if there is a permissions issue or some other error. You can google most apache errors easily, and find a fix somewhere.</p> 
         <p>&nbsp;</p> 
         <h3><strong>MPOS Frontend Familiarization</strong></h3> 
         <p>Before we get into the cronjobs, let’s go ahead and get familiar with MPOS.</p> 
         <p>Click on “Other” and then click “Sign up”</p> 
         <p>The first account on MPOS is the admin account and does not require email activation. Fill in the info and then click “Register” on the bottom. Much like this:<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/RXoAwIU.png" alt="[Image: RXoAwIU.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>The coin address is just your wallet, that you want your mined coins to go to. By default MPOS won’t let you register without one.</p> 
         <p>Also, if you are getting “token expired” messages don’t worry, it’s just MPOS being finicky. Try again.</p> 
         <p>You should get a “Please check your mailbox to activate” message, don’t worry you do not need to activate anything as the first account is the admin account.</p> 
         <p>Go ahead and login.</p> 
         <p>-Go to Admin Panel &gt; System &gt; Settings<br> -You should be at a page that looks like this:<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/6AzgViT.png" alt="[Image: 6AzgViT.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>-Click on “System” on that settings page:<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/LvHmdHc.png" alt="[Image: LvHmdHc.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>-Find “Disable e-mail confirmations” and select yes.<br> -Scroll to the bottom and click save.</p> 
         <p>Now you just set it so users can register without email confirmations, which is important if you don’t have a mail server setup. The other settings you can toy with later on after this guide is over, such as your pool name.</p> 
         <p>Now we need to setup a worker, so you can test your mining pool towards the end of this guide. You can either register another account, or use your admin account (which is not advised in a public environment).</p> 
         <p>Go to My Account &gt; My Workers &gt; Add New Worker<br> Input your worker details, I like to keep it simple and put “1” for the name and “x” for the password. Click “Add New Worker” and you should see your new worker, which will be “mposusername.1” with a password “x”. It will look like this:<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/uhJsz3M.png" alt="[Image: uhJsz3M.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>Remember your worker and worker password for later on.</p> 
         <p>Last thing, go to Admin Panel &gt; System &gt; Monitoring<br> This page is not important now, but it will be when we setup the cronjobs. Please keep it open in a tab on your browser for later. I will refer to it as the “Monitoring Page” in the cronjob section of this guide.</p> 
         <p>Do not worry if there is issues like “we can’t poke your Stratum server”, because the Stratum is not started yet. We will get into that here shortly.</p> 
         <p>&nbsp;</p> 
         <h3><strong>MPOS Cronjob Setup</strong></h3> 
         <p>MPOS work’s off of 3 different main cronjobs. Payout, statistics, and maintenance. There are others, but this is what we will be setting up. We will run them every minute, it is very important that you run these crons every minute otherwise MPOS likes to break.</p> 
         <p>Login to Webmin, http://yourserverip:10000/</p> 
         <p>Go to System &gt; Scheduled Cronjobs</p> 
         <p>-Select “Create a new scheduled cronjob” on the bottom.<br> -“Execute cron job as”, select your user.<br> -“Active” will be yes.<br> -Command will be “/home/username/mpos/cronjobs/run-statistics.sh”<br> -Go down to “Minutes” and click on the “selected” and then highlight all the numbers.<br> -Click “Create”</p> 
         <p>Here’s a picture for reference:<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/Q13PVtx.png" alt="[Image: Q13PVtx.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>Now you will create 2 more cronjobs using the exact process we just did above, for “/home/username/mpos/cronjobs/run-maintenance.sh” and “/home/username/mpos/cronjobs/run-payout.sh”</p> 
         <p>I won’t repeat the process, it’s really straight forward and I am sure you can do it if you have gotten this far in the guide.</p> 
         <p>However, here’s the screenshots for the last 2 cronjobs in case you get confused.<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/WbEhvd7.png" alt="[Image: WbEhvd7.png]" border="0"><br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/vHhGlQC.png" alt="[Image: vHhGlQC.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>Alright, remember that “Monitoring Page” I had you leave open? Go back to it, and hit refresh.</p> 
         <p>This is what you should see:<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/jm8PsOS.png" alt="[Image: jm8PsOS.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>Your monitoring page should get new results pushed to it every minute, if not it’ll turn yellow and eventually red.</p> 
         <p>Congraulations, your cronjobs are setup.</p> 
         <p>&nbsp;</p> 
         <h3><strong>Turning Stratum On</strong></h3> 
         <p>Remember NOMP? We are going to turn it on now.</p> 
         <p>Log back onto ssh (putty) as your user.</p> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>cd</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>cd nomp</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <p>Before we set NOMP to stay on “forever” we are going to make sure it works first while we can still interact with it.<br> Let’s start the stratum server in shell.</p> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>node init.js</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <p>You should see something that looks like this:<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/NFBw62T.png" alt="[Image: NFBw62T.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>If you have no errors, then you are good your stratum is running. If you have issues, please go back to the NOMP setup part of the guide. If that does not work, then I suggest you google your error. More then likely a github issue will pop up with a solution.</p> 
         <p>Now fire up your miner, I am not going to go into specifics about setting up cgminer or what have you. If you don’t know how to setup a miner, you probably should of researched that long before you attempted to setup your own pool.</p> 
         <p>Details for your miner:</p> 
         <blockquote>
          <p><cite>Quote:</cite></p> 
          <p>stratum+tcp://yourserverip:3333<br> -u mposusername.1<br> -p x</p>
         </blockquote> 
         <p>Now, connect your miner.</p> 
         <p>Open up your ssh terminal (putty) that you used the command “node init.js” in. You should see that your worker was authorized, and share(s) have been submitted. Should look something like this:<br> <img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/U2i06ZW.png" alt="[Image: U2i06ZW.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>Congratulations, you are officially mining on your new pool.</p> 
         <p>Now, close your miner.</p> 
         <p>Open up the ssh terminal (putty) window you ran the command “node init.js” on. Press Ctrl+C to get out of init.js.</p> 
         <p>Now type this to keep init.js running indefinitely (until you restart your server or nomp crashes).</p> 
         <div class="codeblock"> 
          <div class="body" dir="ltr">
           <code>forever start init.js</code>
          </div> 
         </div> 
         <p>Your Stratum is now running, and will stay running even if you close your ssh terminal.</p> 
         <p>If you want to know what’s happening with your stratum server, you should run some logs and read up more on forever. Check out the NOMP github page: <a href="https://github.com/zone117x/node-open-mining-portal" target="_blank">https://github.com/zone117x/node-open-mining-portal</a> Also check out the forever github page: <a href="https://github.com/foreverjs/forever" target="_blank">https://github.com/foreverjs/forever</a></p> 
         <p>This guide I am writing is just enough to get your pool started, not manage it. You will want to see who is accessing your stratum, and what’s causing crashes and what not. Logs are very important and something you should look into.</p> 
         <p>&nbsp;</p> 
         <h2><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Conclusion</strong></span></h2> 
         <p><img src="https://blockgen.net/screenshot/congrats.png" alt="[Image: congrats.png]" border="0"></p> 
         <p>Now you can head over to http://yourserverip and check out MPOS some more. I suggest playing around with all the settings and making it how you want it. If you connect your miner, you will see the graphs updating etc…</p> 
         <p>If you have any issues, please review the guide again and make sure you have not missed anything before you start asking questions. It is a huge guide and you can easily miss a step. Also, google is your friend.</p> 
         <p>I get asked a lot about what sort of server power is required to run a mining pool. Based off of the scrypt algorithm, you want about at least 1 CPU Core and 1GB of Memory per 1 GH/s to be on the safe side. This is not including at least 1 CPU Core and 1GB minimum to run your frontend (website), which will also fluctuate depending on the amount of traffic. Internet connection wise, you want at least a 10Mbps port. Most VPS or Servers that you can rent usually have at least a 100Mbps port now-a-days. I would highly recommend SSD drives, especially if you plan on going over 1 GH/s on your pool. MySQL and MPOS likes to eat up those IOPs when your pool gets busy.</p> 
         <p>Congratulations if you completed this guide successfully, it is not an easy task especially if you are a novice with mining pools and linux. Remember that the best way to learn something is just to mess with it, and see what you can do. Push your mining pool to it’s limits, edit the software, mess with the database, etc… These things will make you more knowledgeable. I did not get to where I am at from simply following guides.</p> 
         <p>I may come up with more guides in the future, or additions to this guide. I will use this one as a basis a lot, and I like to cater heavily towards novices. I know they’ll be some purists out there that will hate on me for using things such as Webmin or WinSCP but this guide was never meant for them.</p> 
         <p>I would like to thank the developers that made MPOS and NOMP. I had to cross-reference their guides multiple times to make this one. Please check them out on Github, and donate to them if you are using their software.</p> 
         <p>MPOS: <a href="https://github.com/MPOS/php-mpos" target="_blank">https://github.com/MPOS/php-mpos</a><br> NOMP: <a href="https://github.com/zone117x/node-open-mining-portal" target="_blank">https://github.com/zone117x/node-open-mining-portal</a></p> 
         <p>You are free to use my guide whichever what you want, just please give credit to my site BlockGen.</p> 
         <p>If you liked this guide, please donate to me as well!<br> BTC: 19ejzHzBgf2Jb57nEkJA89erVRqwjnnQeq</p>
