         <h2 class="helpHead2" id="Tdxn4tBK-heading3"><span class="ph" id="topic-title"><a name="topic-title"></a>Some Small Talk on Call Center</span></h2> 
         <div class="body"> 
          <div class="p">
           As you probably recall from the 
           <a class="xref" href="https://trailhead.salesforce.com/module/service_basics" target="_blank" title="HTML (New Window)">Service Cloud Basics</a> module, a good old fashion phone is one of the many channels you can use to communicate with your customers. Even though you might have set up web, email, and social channels, some people still want to hear a helpful human voice on a phone. To some, a customer support team without a call channel is like a car without an engine. Sure, you can climb in, but your team and customers won’t go very far.
           <div class="fig fignone"> 
            <img class="image" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/hy4kyit2a/image/upload/doc/trailhead/en-us63a3c4923b9592f9c774b4f3312936be.jpg" alt="A care engine with the Salesforce no software logo."> 
           </div> 
          </div> 
          <p class="p">Enter Salesforce Call Center. Call Center is just what it sounds like: phone call capabilities integrated through Salesforce. In geek speak, computer-phone integration is referred to as Computer-Telephony Integration, or CTI. CTI might sound complicated, but it simply means that you can use your computer as a phone.</p> 
          <div class="p">
           Why not just use a phone and ditch the computer? Because when you use CTI with Salesforce, any customer data that’s associated with an incoming phone number automatically pops up on your screen. Whenever a customer calls, their information appears front and center for your support agents so that agents have everything they need to help the customer. No more time spent searching, scrolling, or clicking for information about a customer. No more, “Can you please hold while I look that up?” Useful data magically pops up on screen with each call. 
           <div class="fig fignone"> 
            <img class="image" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/hy4kyit2a/image/upload/doc/trailhead/en-us87976ee836ea004f60b213065099492f.png" alt="A screenshot of a softphone popping up caller information."> 
           </div> 
          </div> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="topic nested0" xml:lang="en-us" xmlns:xml="http://www.w3.org/XML/1998/namespace" lang="en-us" id="service_call_intro_123">

         <h2 class="helpHead2" id="Tdxn4tBK-heading4"><span class="ph" id="topic-title"><a name="topic-title"></a>The 1-2-3’s of Call Center</span></h2> 
         <div class="body"> 
          <p class="p">Before we begin, let’s define a few words that might sound strange. These are the building blocks of Salesforce Call Center, and understanding them will get you on track for dialing as many digits as you want.</p> 
          <div class="data colSort">
           <table class="featureTable sort_table" summary=""> 
            <tbody class="tbody"> 
             <tr> 
              <td class="entry">Softphone</td> 
              <td class="entry">An on-screen phone from which you can make and receive calls.</td> 
             </tr> 
             <tr> 
              <td class="entry">Call Center</td> 
              <td class="entry">A Salesforce feature that integrates Salesforce with call systems built by developers or partners.</td> 
             </tr> 
             <tr> 
              <td class="entry">Open CTI</td> 
              <td class="entry">A JavaScript API that lets developers or partners build cloud-based call systems for use with Salesforce’s Call Center.<p class="p">Awesome alert: Since Open CTI is browser and platform agnostic, you can make calls on any browser and platform of your choice. Go ahead and place a call with Microsoft Internet Explorer®, Mozilla Firefox®, Apple Safari®, or Google Chrome™ on Mac, Linux, or Windows. Use the technologies <em class="ph i">you</em> like. Woo-hoo!</p> </td> 
             </tr> 
            </tbody> 
           </table>
          </div> 
          <div class="p"> 
           <div class="fig fignone"> 
            <img class="image" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/hy4kyit2a/image/upload/doc/trailhead/en-us425e76e3dfffd580c1343785bdfb9454.png" alt="A graphic of call center building blocks."> 
           </div> 
          </div> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="topic nested0" xml:lang="en-us" xmlns:xml="http://www.w3.org/XML/1998/namespace" lang="en-us" id="service_call_intro_setup">
         <a name="service_call_intro_setup"></a> 
         <h2 class="helpHead2" id="Tdxn4tBK-heading5"><span class="ph" id="topic-title"><a name="topic-title"></a>A Speed Dial to Set Up</span></h2> 
         <div class="body"> 
          <div class="p">
           Now that you know about Salesforce Call Center’s building blocks, let’s check out how simple it is to get your call center up and running. It’s as easy as dialing 1-2-3 (see what I did there?).
           <ol class="ol enumList"> 
            <li class="li">You install a CTI package created by a developer or partner from the <a class="xref" href="https://appexchange.salesforce.com/" target="_blank" title="HTML (New Window)">AppExchange</a>—Salesforce’s online marketplace for apps.</li> 
            <li class="li">When you install the package, it creates a call center for your organization—an Open CTI system that integrates with Salesforce.</li> 
            <li class="li">You add users to the call center so that they can make and receive calls with a softphone in Salesforce.</li> 
           </ol> 
          </div> 
          <div class="p"> 
           <div class="fig fignone"> 
            <img class="image" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/hy4kyit2a/image/upload/doc/trailhead/en-usf2d5b5451cc576177bc7d6ee70c957ed.png" alt="A graphic of a rotary phone showing the three steps to set up a call center."> 
           </div> 
          </div> 
          <p class="p">That’s pretty much it. Simple, huh? In the next couple of units, we’re going to walk you through the three steps above. When you’re finished, you’ll not only understand Salesforce Call Center, but you might find yourself humming the lyrics to that classic song, “I Just Called to Say I Love You.” And if you come to Dreamforce, you might hear that song playing before one of the keynotes.</p> 
          <div class="box message info">
           <div class="inner">
            <div class="bd">
             <div class="media">
              <img class="img mtm" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/hy4kyit2a/image/upload/doc/trailhead/en-usc4d9ef0c272016519cf3740a5172d683.png" alt="Note">
              <div class="mediaBd">
               <h4 class="mbs">Note</h4>
               <p>This module focuses on Salesforce Call Center for service, but you can use the same functionality for sales. After a Call Center is set up for your org, a softphone appears in the footer of the Salesforce console or in sidebar of every Salesforce page.</p>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div> 
          <div class="box message confirm">
           <div class="inner">
            <div class="bd">
             <div class="media">
              <img class="img mtm" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/hy4kyit2a/image/upload/doc/trailhead/en-us50b669ab18cf8a5692a5d53ad05604bd.png" alt="Tip">
              <div class="mediaBd">
               <h4 class="mbs">Tip</h4>
               <p>Have you heard of Salesforce Voice? Do you wonder what the difference is between Salesforce Voice and the CTI stuff covered here? It all comes down to the telephony. Salesforce Voice provides a way to provision numbers and make calls directly from Salesforce, connecting the user and customer. If you already have a telephony system in place, Open CTI is the way to go since it integrates to that existing system.</p>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
