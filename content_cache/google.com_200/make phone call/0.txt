       <h1 class="page-title "> Make Outbound Phone Calls with Node.js </h1> 
       <div class="by-line"> 
        <div class="authors-body"> 
         <div class="author"> 
          <img src="https://www.gravatar.com/avatar/ec2bdd0cce7e0e4ba8697b7396e3387d?d=mm&amp;s=50" title="Jarod Reyes" alt="Jarod Reyes"> Jarod Reyes 
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="publication-date">
          Published on May 4, 2017 
        </div> 
       </div> 
       <div class="block-rich_text "> 
        <div class="rich-text">
         <p>In this guide, we'll show you how to use <a href="/voice">Programmable Voice</a> to make outbound phone calls from your Node.js applications. It's pretty easy - all you'll need is the <a href="/docs/libraries/node">Twilio module for Node.js</a>, a voice-capable Twilio phone number, <a href="/console">your account credentials</a>, and five minutes to have a boatload of fun at your keyboard. Let's get started!</p>
        </div> 
       </div> 
       <div class="block-content_snippet "> 
        <div class="block-rich_text"> 
         <div class="rich-text">
          <h2 id="purchase-a-voice-enabled-twilio-phone-number"><a class="toclink" href="#purchase-a-voice-enabled-twilio-phone-number">Purchase a voice-enabled Twilio phone number</a></h2>
          <p><a href="https://www.twilio.com/console/phone-numbers/incoming">In the Twilio console</a>, search for and purchase an available phone number capable of making outbound calls. You'll use this phone number as the "From" phone number when you initiate an outbound call.</p>
          <p><img alt="Search for voice capable numbers" class="richtext-image full-width" height="368" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/com.twilio.prod.twilio-docs/images/console-voice-search.width-800.png" width="800"></p>
         </div> 
        </div> 
       </div> 
       <div class="block-content_snippet "> 
        <div class="block-rich_text"> 
         <div class="rich-text">
          <h3 id="retrieve-your-twilio-account-credentials"><a class="toclink" href="#retrieve-your-twilio-account-credentials">Retrieve your Twilio account credentials</a></h3>
          <p>First, you'll need to get your Twilio account credentials. They consist of your AccountSid and your Auth Token.&nbsp;<a href="https://www.twilio.com/console">They can be found on the home page of the console</a>.</p>
          <p><img alt="Retrieve Your Twilio Credentials" class="richtext-image full-width" height="419" src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/com.twilio.prod.twilio-docs/images/Selection_273.width-800.png" width="775"><br></p>
         </div> 
        </div> 
       </div> 
       <div class="block-rich_text "> 
        <div class="rich-text">
         <h2 id="make-an-outbound-phone-call"><a class="toclink" href="#make-an-outbound-phone-call">Make an outbound phone call.</a></h2> 
         <p>Now that we have everything we need, let's make an outbound phone call using the Twilio node.js module.</p>
        </div> 
       </div>

         <div class="button-mode"> 
          <h5 class="title"> <i class="fa fa-fw fa-code"></i> Make an outbound call </h5> 
          <div class="active-tail"></div> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
       </div> 
       <div class="block-content_snippet "> 
        <div class="block-rich_text"> 
         <div class="rich-text">
          <p>There are a few key parameters to drill into when making the outbound call.</p>
          <p></p>
          <ul>
           <li>"From" - the voice-enabled Twilio phone number you added to your account earlier</li>
           <li>"To" - the person you'd like to call</li>
           <li>"Url" - A URL that returns&nbsp;<a href="/docs/api/twiml">TwiML</a>&nbsp;with instructions on what should happen when the other party picks up the phone</li>
          </ul>
         </div> 
        </div> 
       </div> 
       <div class="block-content_snippet "> 
        <div class="block-rich_text"> 
         <div class="rich-text">
          <h3 id="what-is-twiml"><a class="toclink" href="#what-is-twiml">What is TwiML?</a></h3> 
          <p><a href="/docs/api/twiml">TwiML</a> is the Twilio Markup Language, which is just to say that it's an <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/XML">XML</a> document with special tags defined by Twilio to help you build your SMS and voice applications. TwiML is easier shown than explained:</p>
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="block-twiml_sample"> 
         <div> 
          <div class="codehilite">
           <pre><span class="code-line"><span class="cp">&lt;?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?&gt;</span>
</span><span class="code-line"><span class="nt">&lt;Response&gt;</span>
</span><span class="code-line">    <span class="nt">&lt;Say&gt;</span>Thanks for calling!<span class="nt">&lt;/Say&gt;</span>
</span><span class="code-line"><span class="nt">&lt;/Response&gt;</span>
</span></pre>
          </div> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="block-rich_text"> 
         <div class="rich-text">
          <p>Every TwiML document will have the root <strong>&lt;Response</strong>&gt; element and within that can contain one or more <strong>verbs</strong>. Verbs are actions you'd like Twilio to take, such as <a href="/docs/api/twiml/say">&lt;Say&gt;</a> a greeting to a caller, or send an SMS <a href="/docs/api/twiml/sms/message">&lt;Message&gt;</a> in reply to an incoming message. For a full reference on everything you can do with TwiML, refer to our <a href="/docs/api/twiml">TwiML API Reference</a>.</p>
         </div>
