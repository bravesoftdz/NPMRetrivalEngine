           <h1 name="485a" id="485a" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--figure graf--title">I Peeked Into My Node_Modules Directory And You Won’t Believe What Happened&nbsp;Next</h1>
           <p name="fb15" id="fb15" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">The left-pad fiasco shook the JavaScript community to its core when a rouge developer removed a popular module from npm, causing tens of projects to go dark.</p>
           <p name="9f19" id="9f19" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">While code bloat continues to slow down our websites, drain our batteries, and make “npm install” slow for a few seconds, many developers like myself have decided to carefully audit the dependencies we bring into our projects. It’s time we as a community stand up and say enough is enough, this community belongs to all of us, and not just a handful of JavaScript developers with great hair.</p>
           <p name="a561" id="a561" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Am I being paranoid? Maybe. Am I overestimating the hard work that goes into running an open source project? Most likely. Was I kicked off my ZogSports team because I “make sports less fun for everyone involved”? Yes</p>
           <p name="dc81" id="dc81" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I decided to document my experiences in auditing my projects’ dependencies, and I hope you find the following information useful.</p>
           <h3 name="a4f4" id="a4f4" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">Express</h3>
           <p name="5aa1" id="5aa1" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">Behind the fastest, leanest JavaScript web framework is a heaping pile of dependencies, each with their own heaping pile of dependencies. In fact, a simple “npm install express” leads to 291 installed modules.</p>
           <pre name="c1cb" id="c1cb" class="graf graf--pre graf-after--p">$ tree node_modules/ | count<br>zsh: command not found: count<br>$ tree node_modules/ | lines<br>zsh: command not found: lines<br>$ tree node_modules/ | wc -lines<br>wc: illegal option — i<br>usage: wc [-clmw] [file …]<br>$ tree node_modules/ | wc -countlines<br>wc: illegal option — o<br>usage: wc [-clmw] [file …]<br>$ man wc<br>$ tree node_modules/ | wc -l<br>  292</pre>
           <p name="5afb" id="5afb" class="graf graf--p graf-after--pre">Imagine if the apple you were eating for breakfast had 291 ingredients, or if the car you drove to work had 291 parts. You’d be worried, wouldn’t you? Yet, for some reason, we’re totally fine installing 291 individual modules just to power an enterprise-grade web server capable of handling thousands of incoming requests per second.</p>
           <p name="db2e" id="db2e" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">So, what’s in these dependencies anyway? Many are self-explanatory. “range-parser” parses ranges, “escape-html” escapes html, and “negotiator” makes great deals.</p>
           <p name="15bb" id="15bb" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">However, one dependency?—?“yummy”?—?caught my attention.</p>
           <pre name="184f" id="184f" class="graf graf--pre graf-after--p">??? yummy<br>? ??? LICENSE<br>? ??? README.md<br>? ??? like-tweet.js<br>? ??? index.js<br>? ??? package.json</pre>
           <p name="7104" id="7104" class="graf graf--p graf-after--pre">Interesting. Besides the normal “index.js” and “package.json,” we find a suspicious “like-tweet.js.” I decided to take a closer look.</p>
           <pre name="394f" id="394f" class="graf graf--pre graf-after--p">var http = require(“http”)</pre>
           <pre name="7259" id="7259" class="graf graf--pre graf-after--pre">http.request({<br>  method: “POST”,<br>  hostname: “api.twitter.com”,<br>  path: “/hotpockets/status/501511389320470528”,<br>})</pre>
           <p name="690d" id="690d" class="graf graf--p graf-after--pre">Hold on a second. “like-tweet.js,” which runs whenever your application loads up the popular express library, makes a POST request to the twitter API. Why? I went ahead and loaded up this route myself.</p>
           <figure name="3e59" id="3e59" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p">
            <div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 700px; max-height: 517px;">

           <h3 name="e06f" id="e06f" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">Ember</h3>
           <p name="9ed7" id="9ed7" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">Ember.js is a JavaScript web framework that specializes in quickly rendering todo lists. It packs quite the punch at only 112 kilobytes minified and gzipped, but what most people don’t know is how much of that 112 kilobytes is spent on nothing.</p>
           <p name="c22e" id="c22e" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">If we peek into Ember’s dependencies, we see a library called “Glimmer.” This library itself weighs in at 95KB (or 95% of Ember’s codebase), but its purpose is not immediately clear (<a href="https://github.com/emberjs/ember.js/search?utf8=%E2%9C%93&amp;q=glitter" data-href="https://github.com/emberjs/ember.js/search?utf8=%E2%9C%93&amp;q=glitter" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="nofollow noopener" target="_blank">it is not even mentioned on Ember’s internet homepage!</a>).</p>

           <p name="f1a0" id="f1a0" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">Additionally, I was unable to find <a href="http://www.bing.com/search?q=glitter&amp;qs=n&amp;form=QBRE&amp;pq=glitter&amp;sc=8-6&amp;sp=-1&amp;sk=&amp;cvid=0D14DF9331B348988DDAF04DDB346DE0" data-href="http://www.bing.com/search?q=glitter&amp;qs=n&amp;form=QBRE&amp;pq=glitter&amp;sc=8-6&amp;sp=-1&amp;sk=&amp;cvid=0D14DF9331B348988DDAF04DDB346DE0" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="nofollow noopener" target="_blank">any Google results for Glimmer that had to do with JavaScript</a>. So what’s going on here?</p>
           <p name="afcc" id="afcc" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Well, looking at <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">Glimmer’s </em>dependencies, I found something quite curious:</p>
           <pre name="fa14" id="fa14" class="graf graf--pre graf-after--p">??? glimmer@1.1.5<br>? ??? brittanica@13.0.2<br>? ? ??? brittanica-a@0.0.1<br>? ? ??? brittanica-b@0.1.2<br>? ? ??? brittanica-c@0.0.3<br>? ? ...</pre>
           <p name="3d34" id="3d34" class="graf graf--p graf--startsWithDoubleQuote graf-after--pre">“brittanica”?—?a module using up 93KB (or 93%) of Glimmer’s code size, must be doing all the heavy lifting. It itself consists of many dependencies?—?each one a module also named “brittanica”, suffixed with a letter of the english alphabet (26 in total).</p>
           <p name="0604" id="0604" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Poking further, I found that each module consists of a single <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">terms.json </strong>file. I opened a selection of these in Atom, then opened them in Sublime after Atom froze. The following gibberish appeared:</p>

           <h3 name="25f0" id="25f0" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">Babel</h3>
           <p name="e3cb" id="e3cb" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">Babel is a compiler for next-generation JavaScript that beat its competition when someone at Facebook said they liked it better.</p>
           <p name="bffb" id="bffb" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Though loved by many, Babel has had its fair share of critics. Many complain about its confusing plugin system, overly-complicated config files, and unclear error messages. Curiously, not many seem to notice the incredible amount of dependencies Babel requires. Until now.</p>
           <p name="6526" id="6526" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I started my investigation by installing the “babel-preset-es2015” package. This package allows twenty-something web developers to write a newer, worse version of JavaScript that no one else on their team knows. I then counted the number of dependencies brought in by this package, followed by the total code size, using two commands I absolutely didn’t just look up on StackOverflow.</p>
           <pre name="6ccc" id="6ccc" class="graf graf--pre graf-after--p">$ ls node_modules | wc -l<br>    90<br>$ du -sh node_modules<br>    17M node_modules</pre>
           <p name="d3f7" id="d3f7" class="graf graf--p graf-after--pre">A whopping 90 dependencies totaling 17 megabytes. Let that soak in.</p>
           <p name="6f33" id="6f33" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">If the entire recorded history of humanity could fit in a single megabyte, then Babel alone would consist of <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">SEVENTEEN</strong> times the entire recorded history of humanity. Just so that we can avoid writing JavaScript.</p>
           <p name="8258" id="8258" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">So I started wondering, what on earth is causing Babel’s code to be so large?</p>
           <p name="6b4b" id="6b4b" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">One of the biggest offenders, a package called “babel-core” was suspiciously large, coming in at 13 megabytes on its own. I opened up babel-core in vim, then turned off my computer because Ctrl-C wasn’t exiting, then opened babel-core in Sublime Text 2.</p>
           <pre name="3059" id="3059" class="graf graf--pre graf-after--p">module.exports = require("./lib/api/node.js");</pre>
           <p name="c53f" id="c53f" class="graf graf--p graf-after--pre">About 2 hours later, I successfully found the referenced “/lib/api/node.js”, and discovered the crux of the issue. A coding slip-up so unforgivable that I nearly threw out my MacBook and swore off web development forever.</p>

           <p name="cc51" id="cc51" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I have no idea if this picture was supposed to be stripped out before pushing to npm, or how this mistake passed code review. Either way, it’s there, and it’s taking up precious space on millions and millions of 15" Retina MacBook Pro hard drives across the world.</p>
           <h3 name="32c2" id="32c2" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">Conclusino</h3>
           <p name="f740" id="f740" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">To be clear, I don’t think that extra dependencies are a sign of the end of the world. I just think it’s important that we as a community start value the work of maintaining and cleaning up code as much as we value cool features and great project logos.</p>
           <p name="bcfc" id="bcfc" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">We can do better. Dead code takes up space, eats up bandwidth, and can even kill your startup.</p>
           <p name="c33a" id="c33a" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">I encourage you to take a look inside your node_modules/ directory next time you have a couple free hours and a computer with at least 16 gigabytes of RAM. The results may just surprise you.</p>
