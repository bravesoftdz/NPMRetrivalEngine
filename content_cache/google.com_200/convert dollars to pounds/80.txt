              <p>Once you’ve got MarketPress installed and activated, you’ll be presented with our Quick Setup wizard. You can skip it if you <em>really</em> want to, but we highly recommend that you follow it all the way through (as it’ll make the setup of your store easier):</p>

               <li>Your store’s currency, and tax preferences</li> 
               <li>Payment gateways (we’ve got a bunch of those!)</li>

               <li>Enable MarketPress’s alternative download method (for large downloads, like files that are several hundred megabytes in size)</li> 
               <li>Limit the amount of digital products per order</li> 
              </ul>

              <p>If you’re already using Google Analytics on your site, you’ll be pleased to know that we offer integration with that inside of MarketPress, so you can track your e-commerce activity as well (with no add ons required)!</p>

              <h3>Creating Products</h3> 
              <p>In MarketPress, there’s three kinds of products available: Physical, Digital, and External.</p> 
              <p>We’ll start off here with a physical product (a t-shirt in this example), walking through the various aspects of configuration, and then touch on the unique aspects of both Digital &amp; External products.</p> 
              <p>First on our agenda is the “Product Kind” section, where you can choose the type of product to create. We’ve already chosen the “Physical / Tangible Product” type here, for our example case:</p> 
              <div class="pic-full  ">

              </div> 
              <p>Next, we’ve got the “Price, Inventory &amp; Variants” section. Depending on the type of product chosen in the “Product Kind” section, the options displayed here will vary. Below are the options you’d see when creating a physical product:</p> 
              <div class="pic-full  ">
               <img class="attachment-735x735" src="https://premium.wpmudev.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/mp-3006-price-inventory.png" alt="MarketPress - Prices, Inventory &amp; Variants" width="735" height="555">
              </div> 
              <p>Seeing as we’re setting up a physical product at the moment, there’s a few points to note in this section:</p> 
              <p><em>Set up a Sale for this Product:</em> If you’re planning to run a sale on this product, you’ll want to check this option. Plus, you can also specify the period of time that this sale goes on for!</p> 
              <p><em>Special Taxes:</em> If your product requires a different tax rate than what’s specified in your general settings, then you’ll want to enter the desired rate here.</p> 
              <p><em>Charge Shipping:</em> If you’re charging for shipping, you’ll definitely want to check this box, as you’ll then be able to specify the weight for your product (and an additional shipping charge, if you desire).</p> 
              <p><em>Track Product Inventory:</em> Here, you can set up a quantity for your product (and you can even allow customers to purchase the product when it’s out of stock).</p>

              <div class="pic-full  ">

              <p>Here, you can specify one or more images for your product (the first image will be used as the primary image), which will then be displayed as a gallery on your product page.</p>

              <p>Ah, variations… you’ll have fun with this one. :)</p> 
              <p>With this, instead of creating several products for each type of a product (e.g. men’s shirts, women’s shirts, white shirts, etc.), you can turn them into variations inside of a single product!</p> 
              <p>Continuing our t-shirt product example here, you could have one version that’s for women, and another for men.</p> 
              <p>Or, to throw another variation type in there, you could offer men’s &amp; women’s t-shirts, along with small, medium, and large sizes.</p> 
              <p>Or to bring yet <em>another</em> variation type into the mix, you could offer men’s &amp; women’s t-shirts, in small, medium, and large sizes, <em>and</em> in an array of color options as well!</p>

              <p>Found inside of:</p>

               <img class="attachment-735x735" src="https://premium.wpmudev.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/mp-3006-invoice-addon-export-buttons.png" alt="MarketPress - Invoice Addon - Export" width="735" height="500">
              </div> 
              <p>The Invoice PDF add on also has it’s own settings page, accessible via the Add Ons page, where you can configure things like:</p> 
              <ul> 
               <li>How the PDF should be viewed</li> 
               <li>What emails should it be attached to</li> 
               <li>Whether to disable it for free products, or not</li> 
               <li>As well as template settings for the invoice itself</li> 
              </ul> 
              <h3>Using on WordPress Multisite</h3> 
              <p>On Multisite, you can choose to activate MarketPress in one of two ways:</p> 
              <p><strong>Way #1: Activating MarketPress site-by-site</strong></p> 
              <p>You would take this route if you wanted to make it a <a title="Pro Sites" href="https://premium.wpmudev.org/project/pro-sites/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Pro Sites</a> premium plugin, or you wanted to limit it to specific sites. This means your users:</p> 
              <ul> 
               <li>Can choose to activate MarketPress in their subsite dashboards via <em>Plugins -&gt; Installed Plugins</em></li> 
               <li>Can choose to activate any of the payment gateways available in MarketPress.</li> 
              </ul> 
              <p><strong>Way #2: Network activating MarketPress</strong></p> 
              <p>This is where MarketPress really gets to shine on Multisite. With this method of activation, you take MarketPress from being a shop-by-shop experience, and convert it into a network wide experience!</p> 
              <p>You can do things like:</p> 
              <ul> 
               <li>Have a single, global shopping cart, so visitors to your network can add items from any shop on the network, and check out in a single place</li> 
               <li>Charge a percentage of the profits on a sale, via the PayPal Chained Payments gateway</li> 
               <li>Control access to gateways &amp; CSS themes inside of MarketPress</li> 
              </ul> 
              <p>You’ll find all the Multisite options in the Network Dashboard by going to:</p> 
              <p><em>Settings -&gt; Store Network</em></p> 
              <div class="pic-full  ">
               <img class="attachment-735x735" src="https://premium.wpmudev.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/mp-3006-store-network-home.png" alt="MarketPress - Store Network - Home" width="735" height="503">
              </div> 
              <p>Where you’ll see the following sections:</p> 
              <ul> 
               <li>General Settings – limit the use of Global Widgets and shortcodes to the main blog and enable Global shopping cart</li> 
               <li>Product Indexer – index all of the products on your network, so that they’re visible via the global marketplace</li> 
               <li>Gateway Permissions – provide or restrict access to payment gateways for network stores (an additional Pro Sites option will be provided if the <a title="Pro Sites" href="https://premium.wpmudev.org/project/pro-sites/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Pro Sites</a> plugin is installed, more on that in a moment)</li> 
               <li>Global Marketplace Pages – customize your global marketplace, category &amp; tag pages</li> 
               <li>Theme Permissions – provide or restrict access to MarketPress CSS themes for network stores</li> 
              </ul> 
              <p><em>Important:</em> If the Site Visibility of a sub-site in your network is set to “Discourage search engines…” (under Settings -&gt; Reading), the products from that sub-site will not display in the global marketplace.</p> 
              <h3>The Global Cart and Chained Payments</h3> 
              <p>The global cart in MarketPress is designed from the ground up to allow each store to be a different merchant, accepting and processing orders independently, yet allowing for a single place on the network for customers to check out. However, it’s not designed for the same store owner to run multiple stores, you should just keep your products in one store in that case.</p> 
              <p>Two things to bear in mind about the global cart are:</p> 
              <ol> 
               <li>Due to cross-domain security concerns, the global cart is not compatible with domain mapped sub-sites.</li> 
               <li>When the global cart is active, only the PayPal Express Checkout gateway is available for use on the network.</li> 
              </ol> 
              <p>Now, regarding chained payments (also known as <a title="Parallel payments" href="https://developer.paypal.com/webapps/developer/docs/classic/express-checkout/integration-guide/ECParallelPaymens/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">parallel payments</a>)… they’re what allows a customer to checkout/pay once for an order, and have the funds automatically distributed to multiple merchants on a marketplace.</p> 
              <p>In the case of MarketPress, we provide this via the PayPal Chained Payments gateway, allowing you, the store network owner, to collect your share of the profits for a sale, while allowing your store owners to be sent the rest.</p> 
              <div class="pic-full  ">
               <img class="attachment-735x735" src="https://premium.wpmudev.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/mp-3006-network-chained-payments.png" alt="MarketPress - PayPal Chained Payments" width="735" height="605">
              </div> 
              <p><em>Important:</em> Please note, that the chained payments functionality is only available through PayPal.</p> 
              <h3>Pro Sites Integration</h3> 
              <p>With the help of our very own <a title="Pro Sites" href="https://premium.wpmudev.org/project/pro-sites/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Pro Sites</a> plugin, you can control access to specific gateways and CSS themes inside of MarketPress in an finer manner, requiring a user to first have a certain Pro Site level.</p> 
              <p>Simply install and configure Pro Sites on your network, setting up various Levels as you want them, as per our <a href="https://premium.wpmudev.org/project/pro-sites/#product-usage" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">usage page</a>.</p> 
              <p>Then, once you’ve done that, you’ll want to into the Dashboard for the main site, and head to:</p> 
              <p><em>Store Settings -&gt; Add Ons</em></p> 
              <p>And enable the Pro Sites add-on from there.</p> 
              <p>From the Store Network page inside the Network Dashboard, you’ll now see more options inside the dropdown box for access, displaying the Pro Site levels you’ve configured:</p> 
              <div class="pic-full  ">
               <img class="attachment-735x735" src="https://premium.wpmudev.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/mp-3006-pro-sites-integration.png" alt="MarketPress - Pro Sites Integration" width="735" height="587">
              </div> 
              <h3>Note on caching</h3> 
              <p>If you are using a caching plugin such as W3 Total Cache, or have another caching mechanism such as Varnish enabled on your server, you’ll need to add a couple of cookie exceptions for MarketPress to function correctly:</p> 
              <ul> 
               <li>PHPSESSID</li> 
               <li>mp_order_history_</li> 
               <li>mp_session</li> 
               <li>mp_globalcart_*</li> 
              </ul> 
              <p>If you are using W3 Total Cache you’ll want to add these to the “Rejected cookies” setting (one item per line), inside of:</p> 
              <p><em>Performance -&gt; Page Cache -&gt; Advanced</em></p> 
              <p>If your server’s using Varnish cache, please contact your hosting provider and ask them to add these exceptions.</p> 
              <h3>Conclusion</h3> 
              <p>As always, if you get stuck, or you’d like to try something not explained in the Usage guide, <a href="https://premium.wpmudev.org/forums/#question" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">come visit us on the forums</a>, we’re more than happy to help get your MarketPress store up and running.</p> 
             </div> 
            </div>
