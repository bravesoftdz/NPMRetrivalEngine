      <h1>Supported Currencies</h1> 
      <p>See what currencies you can use for making charges and for paying out to your bank account.</p> 
     </header> 
     <section> 
      <aside class="note"> 
       <h3>Supported cards</h3> 
       <p>United States users can accept <span class="supported-cards">Visa</span> <span class="supported-cards">Mastercard</span> <span class="supported-cards">American Express</span> <span class="supported-cards">Discover</span> <span class="supported-cards">JCB</span> <span class="supported-cards">Diners Club</span> credit and debit cards. </p> 
       <p>Stripe also supports a range of additional <a href="/docs/sources">payment methods</a>, depending on the <a href="/payments#worldwide">country of your Stripe account</a>.</p> 
      </aside> 
      <p>Stripe supports processing payments in <a href="#charge-currencies">135+ currencies</a>, allowing you to charge customers in their native currency while <a href="/docs/payouts#supported-accounts-and-currencies">receiving funds in yours</a>. This is especially helpful if you have a global presence, as charging in a customer’s native currency can increase sales.</p> 
      <p>The currency used comes into play in three places:</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li>The customer’s credit card currency</li> 
       <li>The currency of the charge, called the <em>presentment</em> currency</li> 
       <li>The currency accepted by your destination bank account or debit card, called the <em>settlement</em> currency</li> 
      </ul> 
      <p>If the charge currency differs from the customer’s credit card currency, the <em>customer</em> may be charged a foreign exchange fee by their credit card company. The customer may also be charged a fee by their credit card company if the credit card and your business are in different countries, regardless of the currency used.</p> 
      <p>If the charge currency differs from your settlement currency, Stripe <a href="/docs/currencies/conversions">converts the charge</a> to your settlement currency. Refer to our <a href="/docs/payouts#multiple-bank-accounts">payouts</a> documentation to learn about the different bank account currencies that we support.</p>
     </section> 
     <section id="supportedcurrencies"> 
      <h2 id="charge-currencies">Supported presentment currencies</h2> 
      <p>Any Stripe user can process charges in the following list of currencies with these exceptions:</p> 
      <ul> 
       <li>JCB, Discover, and Diners Club cards can only be charged in USD</li> 
       <li>Currencies marked with <code>*</code> are not supported by American Express</li> 
       <li>Most alternative payment methods can only be used with a single currency</li> 
       <li>Brazilian Stripe accounts (currently in Preview) can only charge in Brazilian Real</li> 
       <li>Mexican Stripe accounts (currently in Preview) can only charge in Mexican Peso</li> 
      </ul>

      <h2 id="zero-decimal">Zero-decimal currencies</h2> 
      <p>All API requests expect amounts to be provided in a currency’s smallest unit. For example, to charge $10 USD, provide an <code>amount</code> value of <strong>1000</strong> (i.e, 1000 cents).</p> 
      <p>For zero-decimal currencies, still provide amounts as an integer but without multiplying by 100. For example, to charge ¥500, simply provide an <code>amount</code> value of <strong>500</strong>.</p> 
      <p>Supported zero-decimal currencies are shown as links in the <a href="#charge-currencies">supported charge currencies</a> listed above.</p>
     </section>

      <p>If you only have one bank account in use and you create charges in the same currency as it, the minimum amount is simply what is listed for your currency. Charges that must be <a href="/docs/currencies/conversions">converted</a> into your account’s <a href="https://stripe.com/docs/payouts#multiple-bank-accounts">default settlement currency</a> must meet the equivalent minimum of the settlement currency. For example, if you have GBP and USD bank accounts, with GBP set as your default currency, any non-USD charges you create are converted to GBP. These charges must meet the minimum amount required for GBP (£0.30) after conversion.</p> 
      <p>The only limit to the maximum amount you can charge a customer is a technical one. The <code>amount</code> value supports up to eight digits (e.g., a value of <strong>99999999</strong> for a USD charge of $999,999.99).</p>
