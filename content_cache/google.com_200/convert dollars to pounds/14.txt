          <h3>Some notes about US Dollars</h3> 
          <ul class="list-vertical list-tick-circle"> 
           <li>US dollar bills come in 1, 5, 10, 20, 50 and 100 Dollar denominations. There's also a 2-dollar bill but it is fairly rare. There are also 1, 5, 10 and 25-cent US coins, as well as rarer 50 cent and 1 dollar coins.</li> 
           <li>When you order your currency ask for lower-denomination $10 and $20 dollar bills that are more widely accepted in taxis and smaller shops.</li> 
           <li><a href="/my-travel/how-much-to-tip-on-holiday">Tipping is widespread<span style="font-size: 14px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">, so take plenty of $1 bills for hotels, bars and restaurants.</span></a></li> 
          </ul> 
          <h3>How to make more of your US money on holiday</h3> 
          <p>Obviously the price of your American holiday depends to an extent on where you're staying. For example, Texas and the deep south don't cost much for dining out or buying souvenirs, compared to Boston, York or Chicago. However there are some general rules to heed, if you want your holiday spending to go further.</p> 
          <ul class="list-vertical list-tick-circle"> 
           <li>Public transport is usually cheaper with many cities offering good-value travel passes. Alternatively you might consider renting a car.</li> 
           <li>Whether you want to visit the theatre or watch a ball game ticket prices vary significantly from tour operator to tour operator and among agents so it's best to shop around.</li> 
           <li>And if you are intend to pack in a lot of sightseeing, you can money with a&nbsp;<a class="inline open-new" href="http://www.citypass.com/">CityPASS</a>, which covers admission to multiple major attractions in 10 US cities.</li> 
          </ul> 
          <h3>Tip if you think it's worth it</h3> 
          <p>While tipping is not mandatory in most of the United States, it is customary in many circumstances for service, especially at almost all sit-down restaurants which offer table service and many food servers depend on tips as an essential part of their wage. Generally, the average tip is 15% to 20% of the total meal cost.</p>
