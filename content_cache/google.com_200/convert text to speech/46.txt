         <h1>How To Make Your Raspberry Pi Speak</h1> 
        </div> 
        <div id="breadcrumbs"> 
         <span typeof="v:Breadcrumb"><span property="v:title">How To Make Your Raspberry Pi Speak</span></span>

       <div class="clearfix span8 post-9939 page type-page status-publish hentry" id="9939"> 
        <div class="page-content clearfix"> 
         <h2><b><a href="/shop/speaker-for-the-raspberry-pi/" target="_blank"><img class="alignright wp-image-115357 size-medium" src="https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/Speaker-Speaker_and_Raspberry_Pi-300x278.jpg" alt="speaker-speaker_and_raspberry_pi make the Raspberry Pi Speak " width="300" height="278" srcset="https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/Speaker-Speaker_and_Raspberry_Pi-300x278.jpg 300w, https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/Speaker-Speaker_and_Raspberry_Pi.jpg 500w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px"></a>Introduction: Make Your&nbsp;Raspberry Pi Speak!</b></h2> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Sometimes your robot just wants to talk! &nbsp;This tutorial will show you how to make your Raspberry Pi speak aloud. We will use&nbsp;a software package called Espeak to convert text (or strings) into spoken words, out loud, on your robot. We&nbsp;show you three examples:</span></p> 
         <ul> 
          <li><a href="https://github.com/DexterInd/Raspberry_Pi_Speech/blob/master/speak_count.py" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">How to create a countdown.</span></a></li> 
          <li><a href="https://github.com/DexterInd/Raspberry_Pi_Speech/blob/master/speak_text.py" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">How to modify the voice.</span></a></li> 
          <li><span style="font-weight: 400;">Modify the voice for speed, language, and gender.</span></li> 
         </ul> 
         <h2>Video</h2> 
         <p>Heres a quick video that summarizes the information below and walks you through step by step on getting the Raspberry Pi to speak aloud.</p> 
         <p><iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/bBVCpHLBctc?rel=0" width="560" height="315" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p> 
         <h2><b>Materials Youll Need</b><b><br> </b></h2> 
         <ul> 
          <li>A <a href="/shop/raspberry-pi-3/" target="_blank">Raspberry Pi</a></li> 
          <li><a href="/shop/sd-card-raspbian-wheezy-image-for-raspberry-pi/" target="_blank">Raspbian for Robots SD Card</a></li> 
          <li><a href="/shop/raspberry-pi-power-supply/" target="_blank">Power Supply</a></li> 
          <li><a href="/shop/wifi-dongle-for-raspberry-pi/" target="_blank">Wifi Dongle</a></li> 
         </ul> 
         <p><img class="alignright wp-image-115360 size-medium" src="https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/Speaker-Speaker_and_USB_Cable-300x300.jpg" alt="To make the Raspberry Pi Speak you need the speaker-speaker_and_usb_cable" width="300" height="300" srcset="https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/Speaker-Speaker_and_USB_Cable-300x300.jpg 300w, https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/Speaker-Speaker_and_USB_Cable-150x150.jpg 150w, https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/Speaker-Speaker_and_USB_Cable-180x180.jpg 180w, https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/Speaker-Speaker_and_USB_Cable.jpg 500w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px"></p> 
         <h4>Dexter Industries Speakers</h4> 
         <h4><span style="font-weight: 400;">All the instructions assume youre using&nbsp;</span><a href="http://www.dexterindustries.com/raspberry-pi-robot-software/" target="_blank"><b>Raspbian for Robots,&nbsp;</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">our custom operating system for the Raspberry Pi based on Raspbian. &nbsp;You can buy an SD Card with Raspbian for Robots on it </span><a href="/shop/sd-card-raspbian-wheezy-image-for-raspberry-pi/" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">here</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><a href="http://www.dexterindustries.com/howto/install-raspbian-for-robots-image-on-an-sd-card/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">or </span>you can download it for free and install it using our directions here.</a></h4> 
         <p><b>Software</b></p> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This tutorial uses&nbsp;</span><b><i>python3</i></b><span style="font-weight: 400;">. &nbsp;It can be adapted to earlier versions of python. &nbsp;</span></p> 
         <h4><b>Connections</b></h4> 
         <ul> 
          <li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Make sure your Raspberry Pi is powered up and connected to your network.</span></li> 
          <li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Connect the speaker&nbsp;to the audio jack as shown in the image.&nbsp;</span></li> 
         </ul> 
         <h4><b><a href="https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/image03.png" target="_blank"><img class="alignright wp-image-115613 size-medium" src="https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/image03-300x295.png" alt="image03" width="300" height="295" srcset="https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/image03-300x295.png 300w, https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/image03.png 657w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px"></a>Setting up the TTS (Text To Speech) Engine</b></h4> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">To make the Raspberry Pi speak and read some text aloud, we need a software interface to convert text to speech on the speakers. For this we need a </span><b>Text To Speech engine</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">. The TTS engine we are using in this tutorial is </span><a href="http://espeak.sourceforge.net/"><b>eSpeak</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. &nbsp;The voice may be a little robotic, however it runs offline which is an added plus.</span></p> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">First lets test if the audio is working on the Raspberry Pi. &nbsp;Run the following command:</span></p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">aplay /usr/share/sounds/alsa/*</span></pre> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you are able to hear the sounds like </span><b><i>Front Center</i></b><span style="font-weight: 400;">,</span><b><i>Front Left</i></b><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><b><i>Front Right</i></b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and so on, your sound is working! &nbsp;</span></p> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Next, install eSpeak. &nbsp;Run the following in terminal to install espeak:</span></p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">sudo apt-get install espeak</span></pre> 
         <p><b>Get the Raspberry Pi Speaking from the Command Line</b></p> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">After eSpeak has been successfully installed on the Raspberry Pi, run the following command to test eSpeak: </span></p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;">espeak "Text you wish to hear back" 2&gt;/dev/null</pre> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">For example:</span></p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;">espeak "Hello World" 2&gt;/dev/null</pre> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">You should hear </span><b>Hello World</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> from the speakers. &nbsp;Your Raspberry Pi is speaking!</span></p> 
         <h2><b></b><b>Using Python to Make Your&nbsp;Raspberry Pi Speak&nbsp;</b></h2> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Now building on top of these commands you can create Python programs that speak words. <a href="https://github.com/DexterInd/Raspberry_Pi_Speech" target="_blank">We have two examples that you can download here</a>:</span></p> 
         <ul> 
          <li style="font-weight: 400;"><a href="https://github.com/DexterInd/Raspberry_Pi_Speech/blob/master/speak_count.py" target="_blank"><b>speak_count.py</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">  This program reads aloud a countdown. &nbsp;The program prompts you for a number, and then counts off the numbers out loud until it reaches 0.</span></li> 
          <li style="font-weight: 400;"><a href="https://github.com/DexterInd/Raspberry_Pi_Speech/blob/master/speak_text.py" target="_blank"><b>speak_text.py</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">  This program reads aloud the text you enter and it also records the speech to a file named Text.wav. &nbsp;</span></li> 
         </ul> 
         <h2><b>Running the Example Programs:</b></h2> 
         <p><b>Counting Numbers</b></p> 
         <p><b></b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Connect your Pi&nbsp;to the internet. &nbsp;</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">You will need to download and install the </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">num2words</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;Python package to convert the numbers to strings. &nbsp;This will convert an integer value to a string, which are then read aloud by the Raspberry Pi. &nbsp;Use the following command:</span></p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">sudo pip3 install num2words</span></pre> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Go to the folder where you have copied the examples and run the following command:</span></p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">sudo python3 speak_count.py</span></pre> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Enter a number and you should hear a voice saying&nbsp;</span><b>Count Down Starts</b>, then you&nbsp;should<span style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;hear numbers counted down from the entered number.</span></p> 
         <p><img class="alignnone wp-image-115610 size-medium" src="https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/image04-300x111.png" alt="Your Raspberry Pi is Speaking" width="300" height="111" srcset="https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/image04-300x111.png 300w, https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/image04.png 346w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px"></p> 
         <p><b>Speaking&nbsp;Commands</b></p> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Run the program speak_text.py in the command line:</span></p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">sudo python3 speak_text.py</span></pre> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">When prompted, enter some text you wish to hear back. &nbsp;</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">You should see the text you have entered and you will hear it back through your speakers!</span></p> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Sample output is shown below.</span></p> 
         <p><img class="alignnone wp-image-115612 size-full" src="https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/image00.png" alt="Your Raspberry Pi is Speaking Twinkle Twinkle LIttle Star" width="739" height="67" srcset="https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/image00.png 739w, https://32414320wji53mwwch1u68ce-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/image00-300x27.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 739px) 100vw, 739px"></p> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">As a bonus, this example also records the voice to a file named </span><b><i>Text.wav.</i></b></p> 
         <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Run the following commands to hear back the recorded voice.</span></p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;"> aplay /home/pi/Desktop/Text.wav</span></pre> 
         <h2>A Deeper Dive on Raspberry Pi Speech: More Options</h2> 
         <p><b>Modifying the Voice</b></p> 
         <p>The Espeak package provides a few good variations to the default voice used to speak out the text. &nbsp;The software&nbsp;supports&nbsp;<a href="http://espeak.sourceforge.net/languages.html">more than 30 languages</a> and a few of them are shown below. &nbsp;The syntax is <b>-v&lt;voice filename&gt; &nbsp;For example, we can use an American accent with </b><b>en-us:</b></p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;">espeak -ven-us "Welcome to Dexter tutorial" 2&gt;/dev/null</pre> 
         <p>Or we can use a French accent with <b>fr:</b></p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;">espeak -vfr "Ce tutoriel de Dexter vous souhaite la bienvenue" 2&gt;/dev/null</pre> 
         <p>Or we can&nbsp;speak Spanish with the Raspberry Pi using the term es<b>:</b></p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;">espeak -ves "Bienvenido al tutorial de Dexter" 2&gt;/dev/null</pre> 
         <p><b>Voices</b></p> 
         <p>You can also specify a male voice, or a female voice. &nbsp; You can choose between a few different male voices :<b>+m1,+m2,+m3,+m4,+m5,+m6,+m7. &nbsp;</b>For example, we can speak in male voice five:</p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;">espeak -ven+m5 "Welcome to Dexter tutorial" 2&gt;/dev/null</pre> 
         <p>You can also use female voices: <b>+f1,+f2,+f3,+f4</b>. For example, we can speak in female voice four:</p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;">espeak -ven+f4 "Welcome to Dexter tutorial" 2&gt;/dev/null</pre> 
         <p>You can also modify a few other aspects of the voice with <b>+croak, +whisper.</b> For example, we can whisper:</p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;">espeak -ven+whisper "Welcome to Dexter tutorial" 2&gt;/dev/null</pre> 
         <p><b>Modifying Speech Speed </b></p> 
         <p>The -s parameter controls the speed of reading a single word. It sets the speed in words-per-minute. The default value is 175. I generally use a faster speed of 260. The lower limit is 80. There is no upper limit, but about 500 is probably a practical maximum. &nbsp;We can set the word speed like this:</p> 
         <p><b>Fast at 300 words per minute: &nbsp;</b></p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;">espeak -s250 "Welcome to Dexter tutorial" 2&gt;/dev/null</pre> 
         <p><b>Or slow at 80 words per minute:</b></p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;">espeak -s80 "Welcome to Dexter tutorial" 2&gt;/dev/null</pre> 
         <p><b>Pause Between Words</b></p> 
         <p>We can use the&nbsp;-g to pause between words; the g stands for word gap. This option inserts a pause between words. The value is the length of the pause, in units of 10 ms (at the default speed of 170 wpm). A fast pause:</p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;">espeak -g10 "Welcome to Dexter tutorial" 2&gt;/dev/null</pre> 
         <p>A slow pause with 100 ms between words:</p> 
         <pre style="padding-left: 30px;">espeak -g180 "Welcome to Dexter tutorial" 2&gt;/dev/null</pre> 
         <p>There are lot more options available with the eSpeak package&nbsp;you can review&nbsp;<a href="http://espeak.sourceforge.net/commands.html">here</a>.</p>

Please share any interesting projects that you build with us on the forums </span><a href="http://forum.dexterindustries.com"><span style="font-weight: 400;">here</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">!</span></p>
