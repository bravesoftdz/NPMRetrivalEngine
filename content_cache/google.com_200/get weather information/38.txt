         <td valign="top"> <h4><span style="line-height: 22px;">How do I install the WxWorx receiver?</span></h4> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           For full details, please refer to the installation guide&nbsp;included with your WxWorx receiver. Following is a summary of&nbsp;basic installation steps.
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           1. Install the XM Weather and/or audio software onto your&nbsp;display device.
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           2. Place the WxWorx receiver in the desired location.
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           3. Connect the appropriate data cable (3rd generation&nbsp;receiver) or interface module (2nd generation receiver) of&nbsp;choice.
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           4. Connect the XM antenna. Make sure it has a clear view of&nbsp;the southern sky.
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           5. Connect the WxWorx receiver to an appropriate power source.
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           6. Make the optional audio connection.
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           7. Activate your XM WX subscription by starting up the XM WX&nbsp;software on your display device. Next, find the 8-digit XM&nbsp;Radio ID printed on the label on the bottom side of the
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste"></div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           receiver. It is also printed on a label found on the original&nbsp;box.&nbsp;With your Radio ID number ready, call XM’s Listener Care at&nbsp;1-800-985-9200 to subscribe to the XM WX and/or XM Radio
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste"></div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           services. For prompt service, be sure to also have your data&nbsp;package choice and payment option ready when you call. On the&nbsp;initial startup, your receiver should begin receiving data
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste"></div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           within an hour.
          </div> 
          <div></div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste"></div> <h4>Does the WxWorx receiver and WxWorx software come with a&nbsp;manual?</h4> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           Yes. A manual is included with both WxWorx hardware and&nbsp;software.
          </div> 
          <div></div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste"></div> <h4>Will the WxWorx receiver also give me XM Satellite Radio's&nbsp;audio programming?</h4> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           The WxWorx receiver can receive both the XM WX Satellite&nbsp;Weather and XM Satellite Radio XM signals (separate&nbsp;subscriptions required). An audio output jack allows you to&nbsp;patch the audio through to a powered stereo system. If you&nbsp;have both an XM WX weather data XM WX package and XM radio&nbsp;subscription, XM will give you a $3.00/mo. discount on your&nbsp;radio subscription if activated under the same account. You&nbsp;can use the one XM antenna that is provided with your receiver&nbsp;kit to provide a signal to your XM radio receiver. Separate&nbsp;software is required to control audio of WxWorx receiver.
          </div> 
          <div>
           <strong>Please Note:</strong> The 3rd Gen Receiver can not accommodate both WxTunes and the GPS antenna. Users will have to choose between the two services.
          </div> 
          <div></div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste"></div> <h4>How does the new receiver get power?</h4> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           It depends on the connection type used.
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           • For USB connections: the USB cable itself supplies&nbsp;power to the receiver, so no external power source is&nbsp;necessary.
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           • For Bluetooth, Serial and Ethernet connections: a DC&nbsp;power adapter (supplied with an AC/DC converter in some kits&nbsp;and bundles) is used.
          </div> 
          <div></div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste"></div> <h4>What kind of connections does the receiver offer?</h4> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           • For receiver model WRT-10: USB, Serial (RS-232) and&nbsp;Ethernet are the available options. Power over Ethernet is not&nbsp;supported by the 3rd-generation receiver.
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           • For receiver model WRT-10BT: wireless Bluetooth&nbsp;functionality enables communication between the receiver and a&nbsp;display device.
          </div> 
          <div></div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste"></div> <h4>What is the range of the receiver’s Bluetooth signal?</h4> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           Approximately 30 meters.
          </div> 
          <div></div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste"></div> <h4>What kind of GPS receiver is built into the new receiver?</h4> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           The integrated GPS device is WAAS-enabled, which means it&nbsp;provides much higher positioning accuracy than previously&nbsp;available. Note that an external NMEA-compatible GPS antenna&nbsp;is required, as is software that supports the WAAS&nbsp;functionality. In practically all cases, WAAS support will be&nbsp;added via forthcoming software updates.
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           GPS Antenna Requirements:
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           • Type: Active, NMEA-compliant
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           • Connector: SMA
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           • Power Requirements: 50?, 2.5V-5V (5V tolerant), max&nbsp;50mA
          </div> 
          <div></div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste"></div> <h4>What is the operating voltage range of the WxWorx receiver?</h4> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           The Weather Data Receiver accepts 9-32V DC power input.
          </div> 
          <div></div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste"></div> <h4>Do the WxWorx receivers work with the WxTunes software?</h4> 
          <div id="_mcePaste">
           Yes. The 3rd generation WxWorx receiver supports XM Satellite&nbsp;Radio playback, so customers can use WxTunes to listen to XM&nbsp;audio programming on their PCs.
          </div> 
          <div id="_mcePaste"></div>
