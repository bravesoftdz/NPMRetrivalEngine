       <h1 id="call-a-function">Call a function</h1>
       <p>The simplest way to get started in JavaScript with your micro:bit is to call one of the micro:bit’s built-in JavaScript functions. Just like Blocks are organized into categories/drawers, the micro:bit functions are organized by namespaces, with names corresponding to the drawer names.</p> 
       <pre><code class="lang-typescript">Math.abs(-1)</code></pre> 
       <div class="ui info message"> 
        <div class="content"> 
         <p>If you want to see all functions available in the <code>Math</code> namespace, simply type <code>Math</code> followed by <code>.</code> and a list of all the functions will appear. </p> 
        </div> 
       </div> 
       <h2 id="left-and-right-parentheses-please-">Left and right parentheses, please!</h2>
       <p>Whenever you want to call a function, you give the name of the function followed by <code>(</code> and ending with <code>)</code>. Inbetween the left and right parentheses go the function arguments:</p> 
       <pre><code class="lang-typescript">Math.min(1, 2)</code></pre> 
       <p>It’s a syntax error to have a left parenthesis without the “closing” right parenthesis:</p> 
       <pre><code class="lang-typescript-ignore">Math.min(</code></pre> 
       <p>If a function has zero arguments, you still need the parentheses in order to call the function.</p>
