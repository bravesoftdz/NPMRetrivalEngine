           <p name="41ae" id="41ae" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">On the first attempt when it comes to the actual website, I wanted to take the brutalization one step further. Heavily inspired by <a href="http://n-o-d-e.net" data-href="http://n-o-d-e.net" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">N-O-D-E</a>, I set out to disrupt my CSS entirely, leaving nothing but a black and white blob of text.</p>
           <p name="0dca" id="0dca" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">It turned out to be a bit boring, so I quickly decided to find a new approach.            <p name="b6c7" id="b6c7" class="graf graf--p graf--leading">But this is not the only thing I’ve been doing.            <p name="67ab" id="67ab" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">He recommended a few different alternatives, and I decided to check out <a href="http://bulma.io/documentation/layout/container/" data-href="http://bulma.io/documentation/layout/container/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Bulma</a>.            <p name="c46c" id="c46c" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Which brings me to the next “task” I’ve been working on lately: building a web scraper.</p>

           <p name="6386" id="6386" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">That is of course the obvious choice for the developers in my team, since the publishing system we’re building is built in <a href="https://nodejs.org/en/" data-href="https://nodejs.org/en/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Node.js</a>.</p>
