           <div class="medium-editor" itemprop="text">
            <p>Doorbells are so pre-IoT, let's make a way to connect our phone to our Pinoccio with the power of&nbsp;<a href="http://www.nodejs.org" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Node.js</a><a href="http://Node.js," rel="nofollow" target="_blank">,</a>&nbsp;<a href="http://www.pinocc.io" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Pinoccio</a>, and &nbsp;<a href="http://www.twilio.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Twilio</a>.</p>
            <p>Alright to get started you're gonna need to create a Twilio accout, it's super easy, and they give you $30 worth of texting and calling for free, and their api is super easy since they have wrappers for almost any language. Get started with Twilio at&nbsp;<a href="http://www.twilio.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">http://www.twilio.com</a>.</p>
            <p>Follow their instructions to find your sid and auth token on their&nbsp;node.js&nbsp;library <a href="http://twilio.github.io/twilio-node/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">documentation</a>.</p>
            <p>Now we can move onto the&nbsp;Node.js&nbsp;app, open up a terminal create a directory for the app to live. Run:</p>
            <p><code>npm init</code></p>
            <p>to set up your app. Now run:</p>
            <p><code>npm install --save twilio pinoccio</code></p>
            <p>to install all the necessary modules needed for our app.</p>
