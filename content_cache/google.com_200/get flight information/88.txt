         <td bgcolor="#205670"><font color="#FFFFFF" class="corps"><b>1.0 Policy Perspective</b></font></td> 
        </tr> 
       </tbody>
      </table> <p class="corps">Continuous improvement of the Canadian Air Navigation Service (ANS) through the development and fielding of technologically advanced solutions, which:</p> <p class="corps">1. enhance safety management<br> 2. provide operational efficiencies<br> 3. provide clear commercial value for our clients</p> <p class="corps"><b>Purpose of the Aviation Weather Web Site </b></p> <p class="corps">The Aviation Weather Web Site "AWWS" provides a technologically advanced, high-speed means of efficiently distributing essential meteorological information to our clients throughout the country. The charts, maps, and reports currently provided over the site are viewed collectively, and are thus seen to be one of critical components in the suite of flight planning products, provided and distributed through AWWS. In the near future this suite will be expanded.</p> <p class="corps">The site is intended to be your gateway and to be used at the beginning of your flight planning process. It is a conduit through which NAV&nbsp;CANADA provides you with flight safety and operationally important information with which to safely plan your flight. Opening the web site is as easy as accessing your home or office PC or, at many FSS? you will find pilot information computer kiosks provided for your convenience. Selecting the information, using it for your en-route planning and then, discussing it with one of our highly trained and well equipped weather briefers at the FSS?/ Flight Information Centres (FIC?s) is definitely the comprehensively safest and recommended procedure.</p> <p class="corps">&nbsp;</p> <font color="#FFFFFF"><b class="corps"><a name="philo"></a></b></font> 
      <table border="0" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0" width="100%" height="32"> 
       <tbody>
        <tr> 
         <td bgcolor="#205670"><font color="#FFFFFF"><b class="corps">1.2 AWWS Philosophy</b></font></td> 
        </tr> 
       </tbody>
      </table> <p class="corps">The Aviation Weather Web Site (AWWS) is a web-based application, which means that anyone with Internet access can use it at no direct cost. It is designed to be a "living system" capable of growing with technology and user demands. The recent introduction of NOTAM data reflects this flexible approach to customer service.</p> <p class="corps"> Through periodic consultations with users, field visits, and the operational knowledge of our Flight Service Specialists, your changing requirements will be identified. These will in turn drive research and development and the creation of new products. In fact, the next generation of aviation weather briefing products should be available throughout 2004, including file-a-flight-plan capability.</p> <p class="corps"> In the long term you will also be able to obtain information from digital flight publications. In addition, consideration is being given to providing weather information in a format that can be ingested by flight planning software, providing access to colour imagery from weather cameras, and extending coverage into the United States.<br> </p> <p class="corps">&nbsp;</p> <font color="#FFFFFF" class="corps"><b><a name="usagers"></a></b></font> 
      <table border="0" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0" width="100%"> 
       <tbody>

         <td bgcolor="#205670" height="32"><font color="#FFFFFF" class="corps"><b>1.3 Users </b></font></td> 
        </tr> 
       </tbody>
      </table> <p class="corps">Anyone with Internet access can enter the site free of charge and use AWWS products. The site will also be available to Flight Service Specialists in Flight Information Centres (FIC?s) across Canada, and through the Pilot Information Kiosks (<b>PIK</b>). There are no restrictions on who can access AWWS and the site is not password-protected.</p> <p class="corps">Users have the possibility of creating their own information packages and the ability to save them under any name they choose. Generating the information required will be easily accomplished with just a few clicks of the mouse. To access this feature, the users will have to register on the web site.</p> <p class="corps">Unregistered users can still access the site and create information packages, but they will not be able to save this information or retrieve it at a later time.</p> <p>&nbsp;</p> <font color="#FFFFFF" class="corps"><b><a name="config"></a></b></font>

         <td height="32" bgcolor="#205670" class="corps"><font color="#FFFFFF"><b>1.4 System Requirements</b></font></td> 
        </tr> 
       </tbody>
      </table> <p class="corps">This web site has been optimized for a screen resolution of 800x600. This was necessary because the site will be accessible via a touch-screen at Pilot Information Kiosk (PIK), which imposes a minimum dimension on the size of objects and links displayed on AWWS. </p> <p class="corps">For maximum efficiency, <strong> Mozilla Firefox 2.0 </strong> and Internet <strong>Explorer 6.0 </strong> or <strong>higher version </strong> are recommended. The browser should be set to JavaScript enabled. </p> <p class="corps"><strong>Preferred Technical Requirements to access the AWWS </strong>: </p> 
      <ul> 
       <li class="corps"><strong>PC Type: </strong> Pentium III 450MHz PC with 64 Mbytes of RAM running Windows 98 or higher </li> 
       <li class="corps"><strong>Browser Level: </strong> Mozilla Firefox 2.0 or Internet Explorer 6.0 </li> 
       <li class="corps"><strong>Monitor Resolution: </strong> 1280x1024 True colour </li> 
       <li class="corps"><strong>Internet Access: </strong> 56 Kbps modem or High speed cable access </li> 
      </ul> <p>&nbsp; </p> <p class="corps"><strong>Tips for using Java Tools: </strong></p> 
      <ul> 
       <li class="corps">AWWS requires that your browsers preference should be set to <strong>Java Script </strong> and <strong>Java </strong> enabled. </li> 
       <li class="corps">Modem speed greater that 56 Kbps are desirable for downloading the tools. </li> 
       <li class="corps">To download the latest package, please go to the <a href="http://java.sun.com">http://java.sun.com </a> and follow the instruction to download the appropriate file for your machine.</li> 
      </ul> <p class="corps"> <span class="corps"><font color="#FFFFFF"><b class="corps"><a name="donnees"></a></b></font></span> </p>

        <tr> 
         <td height="32" bgcolor="#205670" class="corps"><font color="#FFFFFF"><b class="corps">1.5 Stale Data</b></font></td> 
        </tr> 
       </tbody>
      </table> <p class="corps">Upon submission of the request the data returned is current. The data will become stale (i.e. not the most current) if the data is not refreshed. This can be done by resubmitting the request or by pressing <b>Reload</b> (while holding the SHIFT key) in <b>Netscape</b> or <b>Refresh</b> in <b>Internet</b> <b>Explorer</b>. It is the user's responsibility to ensure that the data is the most current.</p> <p class="corps">Refresh and reload may return cached data (stale) unless the following precautions are taken:</p> 
      <ul class="corps"> 
       <li>the web browser?s cache should be set to "compare to document on network <b>every time</b>"</li> 
       <li><span class="corps">In Internet Explorer 6.0 and later, this is done by following these steps:</span> 
        <ul> 
         <li>go to <b>Tools</b> menu, select <b>Internet</b> <b>Options</b></li> 
         <li>go to <b>General</b> Index</li> 
         <li>in the Temporary Internet Files, click on <b>Settings</b></li> 
         <li>select the button "<b>every visit to the page</b>"</li> 
        </ul> </li> 
      </ul> <p class="corps"> In Netscape you can also assure that you are retrieving the most up-to-date information by holding the <b>SHIFT</b> key while clicking on the <b>Reload</b> button. Using the "<b>Back</b>" button will not override the cache, unless the cache size has been set to 0. </p> <p>&nbsp;</p> <font color="#FFFFFF" class="corps"><b><a name="civ"></a></b></font>

You can also retrieve weather information through the Pilot?s Automatic Telephone Weather Answering Service (<b>PATWAS</b>). Please consult the current Canada Flight Supplement (CFS) for the appropriate phone number(s).</p> <p></p>
