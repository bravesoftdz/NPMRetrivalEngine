        <h3><a name="gettingthere"></a>Getting There</h3> 
        <p>Nothing quite beats arriving in Mulu than from the air. Flying over this vast landscape you have the unique opportunity to a bird’s eye view of the tropical river systems that weave their way to the South China Sea.</p> 
        <p>On your decent, you’ll get your first glimpse of the Melinau Formation which rose from the sea floor around 5 million years ago and is now home to some of the largest and most famous caves in the world.</p> 
        <p><a href="http://mulupark.com/v2/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/DSC00049.jpg"><img class="alignnone wp-image-11629 " src="http://mulupark.com/v2/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/DSC00049-250x250.jpg" alt="" width="195" height="195" srcset="http://mulupark.com/v2/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/DSC00049-250x250.jpg 250w, http://mulupark.com/v2/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/DSC00049-190x190.jpg 190w, http://mulupark.com/v2/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/DSC00049-450x450.jpg 450w" sizes="(max-width: 195px) 100vw, 195px"></a>&nbsp; &nbsp;<img class="alignnone wp-image-11628" src="http://mulupark.com/v2/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/DSC00020-250x250.jpg" alt="" width="195" height="195" srcset="http://mulupark.com/v2/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/DSC00020-250x250.jpg 250w, http://mulupark.com/v2/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/DSC00020-190x190.jpg 190w, http://mulupark.com/v2/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/DSC00020-450x450.jpg 450w" sizes="(max-width: 195px) 100vw, 195px"> <img class="alignnone wp-image-11417" src="http://mulupark.com/v2/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/old-mulupark-web_map_of_mulu.jpg" alt="old mulupark web_map_of_mulu" width="273" height="195"></p> 
        <h4>BY PLANE</h4> 
        <p>Daily flights from Miri and Kuching and three times a week from Kota Kinabalu is a wholly owned subsidiary of the National Carrier- Malaysia Airlines Services.</p> 
        <p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-10843" src="http://mulupark.com/v2/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/logo-mas-wings.png" alt="logo-mas-wings" width="140" height="40"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-10844" src="http://mulupark.com/v2/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/logo-mas.png" alt="logo-mas" width="140" height="40"></p> 
        <h4>Flight schedule</h4> 
        <p>Please visit <span class="button"><a href="http://www.maswings.com.my/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">www.maswings.com.my</a></span> / <span class="button"><a href="http://www.malaysiaairlines.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">www.malaysiaairlines.com</a></span>&nbsp;for the current scheduling.</p> 
        <h4>BY FOOT</h4> 
        <p class="style6">It is possible to walk into Mulu via ‘The Headhunter’s Trail’ which takes its name from its historical role as the route taken by warring parties between the Tutoh and Medalam rivers. The trail runs between Limbang, a picturesque town on the Limbang River, entering Mulu on its northern boundary and trekking down through Camp 5 and then onto Mulu Park Head Quarters. (or visa versa).</p> 
        <p class="style6">The area is very isolated and the nearest town is many hours away, the trail is well marked and maintained but tree falls and river floods can cause confusion. If you want to travel the Headhunters Trail you must contact a licensed tour operator to make arrangements for you.</p> 
        <p class="style6">Which ever adventure you choose to get to Mulu it is just an introduction to the jungle experience waiting for you.</p> 
        <h4 class="style6">AIRPORT SHUTTLE</h4> 
        <p>Local community provides reliable shuttle services to and from the airport.<br> The walk to and from the airport can be long and hot, we encourage you to support the local community where ever possible.<br> What a better place to start on your arrival in Mulu!</p>

        <h3><a name="weather"></a>Weather</h3> 
        <p>Mulu is not strongly affected by the monsoonal seasons that most of South East Asia experiences. July – September are the driest months (it only rains about 3 days in 7) but it is also hotter without the cooling rains. March and April have a little less rain than the wettest months of October – January and May – June.</p> 
        <h3>A Typical day in Mulu</h3> 
        <p>Each Mulu morning usually dawns with a clear sky and a temperature around 23 degrees.</p> 
        <p>By early afternoon the temperature has risen to about 34 degrees and 60% humidity; around 3.00 – 5.00 pm it will start raining and the humidity increases to 90%. Showers may last 30 – 60 minutes, but after the rain the air is cool and fresh, the evenings are usually fine.</p> 
        <p>As it rarely rains in the morning, those doing the Pinnacles climb go up in dry conditions and if they are slower to return they may get wet towards the end of their walk. Cancellation of the Pinnacles climb due to wet weather would average no more than 10 days per year.</p> 
        <p>Yes we do get between 4 – 7 metres of rain each year so you will experience wonderful tropical down-pours which swell the rivers and sometimes make them flood.</p> 
        <h3><span style="color: #ff0000;"><em>VERY IMPORTANT NOTICE:</em></span></h3> 
        <p><span style="color: #ff0000;"><em>Please allow for a flexible &nbsp;itinerary and flights,to and especially from Mulu.</em></span></p> 
        <p><span style="color: #ff0000;"><em>The Mulu weather is completely unpredictable and has a major influence over flights into and out of Mulu.</em></span></p> 
        <p><span style="color: #ff0000;"><em>Allow sufficient time &nbsp;to make deadlines for important connecting flights or pre-booked activities, that are non-refundable or coupled to expiry dates.</em></span></p> 
        <h3>Peak Season</h3> 
        <p><span style="color: #008000;"><em>Bookings are essential</em></span></p> 
        <p>June, July, August and September are usually busier than other times of the year. We recommend that at all times you should make bookings for accommodation and guides in advance to avoid disappointment. Once the facilities are full and the guides booked then there is nothing we can do to help even though you have travelled a long way to visit.</p> 
        <p><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-11182" src="http://mulupark.com/v2/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Fletcher-Baylis-25339-680x451.jpg" alt="Fletcher &amp; Baylis 25339" width="680" height="451" srcset="http://mulupark.com/v2/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Fletcher-Baylis-25339-680x451.jpg 680w, http://mulupark.com/v2/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Fletcher-Baylis-25339-300x200.jpg 300w, http://mulupark.com/v2/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Fletcher-Baylis-25339.jpg 900w" sizes="(max-width: 680px) 100vw, 680px"></p> 
        <h3><a name="survivalkit"></a>Mulu Survival Kit</h3> 
        <p>Mulu is remote and isolated. Although we wouldn’t want it any other way, it does surprise some visitors that they just can’t pop down the shop and pick up something they forgot to bring along for their trek.</p> 
        <p>That means: NO banks, NO&nbsp;chemist, hardly any roads and not many options.</p> 
        <p>Things you must consider to pack:</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li>Trekking Shoes</li> 
         <li><span style="color: #ff0000;">Raincoat</span></li> 
         <li><span style="color: #ff0000;">Torchlight</span></li> 
         <li>Water bottle</li> 
         <li>Insect repellent</li> 
         <li><span style="color: #ff0000;">1<sup>st</sup> aid kit</span></li> 
         <li>Hat</li> 
         <li><span style="color: #ff0000;">Cash (RM)</span></li> 
        </ul> 
        <p><em><span style="color: #ff0000;">*Essential items.</span></em></p>
