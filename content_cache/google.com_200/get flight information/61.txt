        <div id="about-the-experience" class="faq-group scrollspy">
         <header>
          <h4>The Experience</h4>
          <p>Anything you'd like to know before your first flight?</p>
         </header>
         <div class="row">
          <div class="s12 m6 col">
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">What is a High Flight?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p>With the purchase of the high flight, guests are able to fly higher and faster with their instructor than they would fly on their own. The high flight occurs during the last 15-20 seconds of the guest’s final flight. High flights may not be available to guests that are over 260 lbs or to guest with certain restrictions</p>
            </div>
           </article>
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">Do I have to do the training session?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">All first-time flyers are required to take the training class. You’ll get a lot of important and helpful information as well as some pre-flight practice of your flying position. If you’ve flown with us once within the last three months, you don’t have to do the training session again and can <a href="http://www.iflyworld.com/book-flights/for-return-flyers/return-packages-prices/">book a flight as a Return Flyer</a>.</span></p>
            </div>
           </article>
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">Can I eat before I fly?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Yes, feel free to eat before your scheduled flight session. You won’t get any feeling of motion sickness, nausea, or dropping feeling in your stomach.</span></p>
            </div>
           </article>
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">Can I wear my glasses?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Yes. We have larger goggles that fit over eyeglasses.</span></p>
            </div>
           </article>
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">Will I need to take off my watch and jewelry?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Yes. You should remove any items that might flap around or become loose, such as earrings, necklaces, bracelets, watches, rings, and hair accessories. In addition, you should remove everything from your pockets. We do have lockers for your use, but we recommend that you leave anything valuable at home as we cannot be responsible for lost or stolen property.</span></p>
            </div>
           </article>
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">Can I do tricks and flips in the tunnel?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Our primary concern is for your safety in the flight chamber so the tricks or flips are done by experienced flyers only. Some flyers are able to do turns, move up and down, and forward and backward during their first flight. Be sure to ask your instructor how to advance your flight skills.</span></p>
            </div>
           </article>
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">Why do I need to wear a flight suit?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The instructors are trained to grasp the handles of your flight suit should you need spotting.</span></p>
            </div>
           </article>
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">What should I wear?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Please wear well-fitting, lace-up sneakers, or running shoes. Other shoes are not suitable. Wear casual clothes, preferably pants and a shirt without a collar. You won’t have to change out of these clothes—you’ll put your flight suit on over your street clothes.</span></p>
            </div>
           </article>
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">How long should I plan to be there?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The iFLY experience lasts approximately an hour and 45 minutes in total. This will cover your check-in time, the training session, your flight time, and time after your flight to remove gear and look at photos and video if interested. Save time by <a href="http://www.iflyworld.com/flight-waiver/">completing your waiver</a> ahead of time. </span></p>
            </div>
           </article>
          </div>
          <div class="s12 m6 col">
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">What time should I arrive?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Guests should arrive at their scheduled reservation time. This means that flyers need not to arrive before their scheduled time, but not later than 15 minutes after. Return Flyers should arrive no later than 15 minutes after their arrival time.</span></p>
             <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;</span></p>
            </div>
           </article>
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">Can I fly with my friends or family members?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">We generally fly several people in each session. Your friends and family will be part of the same group, however you will fly one at a time in the flight chamber one-on-one with your instructor. Once you have gained more flight experience then you will be able to fly with others of the same skill level in the tunnel. If someone chooses to come to watch, he or she is welcome to accompany you through your experience and can watch from the observation area.</span></p>
            </div>
           </article>
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">Is there a weight restriction?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p>Participants must weigh less than 300 pounds.</p>
             <p>Participants who weigh between 260 and 300 pounds must bring this to the attention of an iFLY representative as additional restrictions and instructor scheduling limitations may apply.</p>
            </div>
           </article>
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">How old do you have to be?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Flyers can be as young as 3 years of age and there is no upper age limit. Children under 18 years of age need to have a parent or guardian sign their waiver.</span></p>
            </div>
           </article>
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">Who can fly?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p>We welcome flyers&nbsp;from 3 to 103. If you have&nbsp;reasonable health and fitness you can fly. But do check the criteria below before you book. We’ll do everything we can to help you fly because our goal is to deliver the dream of flight to everyone!</p>
             <p>There are a few exceptions:</p>
             <ul>
              <li>Flyers must be over 3 years of age or older.</li>
              <li>Participants must weigh less than 300lbs.</li>
              <li>Women who are pregnant should not fly.</li>
              <li>Folks with recent&nbsp;<strong>back, neck and heart problems</strong>&nbsp;should check with a doctor before flying.</li>
              <li>iFLY recommends people with prior shoulder dislocations DO NOT FLY!</li>
              <li><strong><a href="http://www.iflyworld.com/flight-restrictions">Click here</a></strong>&nbsp;for a complete list of restrictions.</li>
             </ul>
             <p>If you have any concerns or questions about whether or not you can fly,&nbsp;<a href="http://www.iflyworld.com/find-a-tunnel/">contact your local iFLY</a>. Everyone who flys must complete a&nbsp;<a href="http://www.iflyworld.com/flight-waiver/">Flight Waiver</a>, which contains further details.</p>
            </div>
           </article>
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">Is it hard to breathe?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Not at all. It is the same as if you stick your head out a car window while driving. All you feel is the wind in your face. Just breathe normally, either through your mouth or your nose.</span></p>
            </div>

           <article class="faq">

            <div class="answer">

When jumping out of an airplane, the free fall portion lasts approx. 45 seconds. If you reserve a package that contains 2 flights, you will have 2 turns in the wind tunnel with an instructor lasting 60 seconds each. Some of our packages include double-length flights which lasts double the time at a reduced cost. The entire iFLY first-time flyer experience takes approx. 1 hour and 30 minutes from start to finish.</span></p>
            </div>

            <div class="answer">

         <header>
          <h4>The Sport of Indoor Skydiving</h4>
          <p>Ready to dive into the exciting world of indoor skydiving?</p>

         <div class="row">
          <div class="s12 m6 col">
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">Are there any flying tutorials?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Yes, there are. The International Bodyflight Association (IBA) has some excellent video tutorials. The IBA is the association that trains and accredits our instructors.</span></p>

           </article>
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">How does it work?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p>A vertical wind tunnel has fans at the top to draw air through the flight chamber and then push it back down the sides through Return Air Towers. That leads to an inlet contractor that compresses &amp; speeds up the air before it reenters the flight chamber. The result: a smooth column of air that enables you to fly. <a href="http://www.iflyworld.com/what-is-indoor-skydiving/">Watch&nbsp;our instructional video</a>.</p>
            </div>
           </article>
          </div>
          <div class="s12 m6 col">
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">What is indoor skydiving?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Competitive indoor skydiving, also known as Bodyflight, shares many of the disciplines of competitive skydiving, such as formation skydiving and freestyle skydiving. Generally, athletes compete to successfully complete a certain number of maneuvers in an allotted amount of time. Athletes can compete as teams, working to complete maneuvers and grips, or as individuals. Each performance is recorded and judged by a panel of experts. If you’re interested, our instructors can help you improve your flying skills and take you to the next level of indoor skydiving. <a href="http://www.iflyworld.com/what-is-indoor-skydiving/">More Info</a></span></p>
            </div>
           </article>
           <article class="faq">
            <a href="javascript:;" class="row"><span class="left"><i></i></span><span class="right">What is a wind tunnel?</span></a>
            <div class="answer">
             <p>Very simply, a wind tunnel is a machine that moves a lot of air. A vertical wind tunnel, which is what we use at iFLY, moves air in a vertical column at speeds high enough to keep a person safely floating.</p>
            </div>
