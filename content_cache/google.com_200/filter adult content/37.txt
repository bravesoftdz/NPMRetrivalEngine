      <div id="section-0" class="section summary">
       <p>The Firefox add-ons library features a number of extensions designed to block and filter adult websites and other inappropriate content. ProCon Latte, MetaSurf and Blocksi all use comprehensive blacklists that block URLs and unknown websites that contain blocked language. The extensions differ slightly in customization options and ease of use. ProCon Latte features automatic blacklist updates that keep the extension up to date with new websites, as well as a whitelist that allows certain websites even if they contain words in the blacklist. Blocksi relies on user input to define website categories that are unfit to visit and features detailed statistics that show which websites were blocked and which were allowed through the filter. If you don't need to muck with customizations, MetaSurf is a simple extension that lacks advanced features but offers a regularly updated blacklist that you can set and forget.</p>

       <h2>ProCon Latte Content Filter</h2> 
       <p class="highlighted">1. Search the Firefox add-ons library for ProCon Latte and click "Install," and then restart Firefox. After restart is complete, open the options window for the extension.</p> 
      </div>
      <div id="section-2" class="section nested"> 
       <p class="highlighted">2. Click "Set Password" to password protect access to the ProCon Latte options.</p>

       <p class="highlighted">3. Click the check box beside "Enable subscriptions" to receive automatic updates to the ProCon Latte blacklist.</p> 
      </div>
      <div id="section-4" class="section nested"> 
       <p class="highlighted">4. Select the "Whitelist" tab to specify websites that are safe to visit. Unlike a standard whitelist, websites in the whitelist are not the only websites that can be visited. Instead, this whitelist is used to approve websites like news websites or social networks that may normally be blocked due to the language filter.</p> 
      </div>
      <div id="section-5" class="section nested firstNested">
       <h2>Blocksi</h2> 
       <p class="highlighted">1. Search the Firefox add-ons library for Blocksi and click "Install," and then restart Firefox. After restart is complete, open the options window for the extension.</p> 
      </div>
      <div id="section-6" class="section nested"> 
       <p class="highlighted">2. Click "Kids" block access to the default set of adult websites.</p> 
      </div>
      <div id="section-7" class="section nested"> 
       <p class="highlighted">3. Click "Advanced Filter" to customize the content types you want to block in Firefox. Blocksi breaks content into dozens of categories, such as hacking, gambling and other adult materials.</p> 
      </div>
      <div id="section-8" class="section nested"> 
       <p class="highlighted">4. Click "Options" and then click the check box beside "Enable Password Protection" to disable non-administrator access to Blocksi settings.</p> 
      </div>
      <div id="section-9" class="section nested firstNested">
       <h2>MetaSurf</h2> 
       <p class="highlighted">1. Search the Firefox add-ons library for MetaSurf and click "Install," and then restart Firefox. After restart is complete, open the options window for the extension.</p> 
      </div>
      <div id="section-10" class="section nested"> 
       <p class="highlighted">2. Click the "Enable" button to turn on MetaSurf.</p> 
      </div>
      <div id="section-11" class="section nested"> 
       <p class="highlighted">3. Enter a password to protect non-administrators from adjusting the MetaSurf settings.</p>
