        <h1>Block Adult Content</h1> 
        <em>Last modified: December 16, 2013</em> 
       </header> 
       <section class="entry"> 
        <p>Site-wide blocking of pornography or other adult content can be accomplished with a single mouse click. &nbsp;Just login to you EasyTomato router and select the universal blocking rule checkbox called Block Adult Content, youre done. &nbsp;Yes, its that easy!</p> 
        <p>This rule is available on the Groups screen.&nbsp; Now all devices on the network are blocked from adult content websites. (<strong>Note:</strong> It is not possible to block adult content for only one group of computers.)</p> 
        <p>If you want more control over the access control, check out our other guides: <a title="How to Block a Website" href="http://www.easytomato.org/how-to-guides/how-to-block-a-website">How to Block a Website</a> or <a title="Block a device from the internet" href="http://www.easytomato.org/how-to-guides/block-a-device-or-group-from-the-internet">How to Block a Device From the Internet</a>.</p> 
        <p>&nbsp;</p> 
        <p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.easytomato.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/EasyTomato-Groups-Screen.png"><img class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-334" title="EasyTomato Groups Screen" alt="Block Adult Content and Pornography with EasyTomato" src="http://www.easytomato.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/EasyTomato-Groups-Screen-300x216.png" width="300" height="216" srcset="http://www.easytomato.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/EasyTomato-Groups-Screen-300x216.png 300w, http://www.easytomato.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/EasyTomato-Groups-Screen.png 1001w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px"></a></p>

        <h4>How the Block Adult Content rule works</h4> 
        <p>EasyTomato uses <a href="http://www.opendns.com/home-solutions/parental-controls/" target="_blank">FamilyShield</a>, an <a href="http://www.opendns.com" target="_blank">OpenDNS</a>&nbsp;free parental control product, to block adult content.&nbsp; When you enable the Block Adult Content rule, your router sends all website requests to an OpenDNS server.&nbsp; If the site requested is on the FamilyShield blacklist, the server will block the site, and the requesting device will receive a This site blocked page instead.</p> 
        <h4>What the rule blocks</h4> 
        <p>It blocks all sites listed on the OpenDNS FamilyShield filter list, which is updated multiple times a day.&nbsp; This should cover almost all known pornography websites in the world.&nbsp; Not only does FamilyShield block adult content, but it also blocks fraud, phishing, and identity theft websites.</p>
