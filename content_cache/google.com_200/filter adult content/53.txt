             <p>In an effort to "increase productivity" I have been asked to track employee web site usage during business hours.&nbsp; I have been using NTA to monitor and track network usage as a whole in terms of protocols and have a pretty good indication of top users, top protocols, etc.&nbsp; However, when looking into web usage I find I get a little lost.&nbsp; I am tracking top conversations, top transmitters and receivers.</p>
             <p>When I look at the lists of external hosts I see DNS info that I am having a hard time sorting out.&nbsp; I see stuff like <i>www.facebook.com</i> that are pretty easy to understand, then there are entries like <i>80.mtl-mg07.streamtheworld.net (208.80.52.80)</i> and I can assume it is streaming audio via web browser.&nbsp; But then I see entries like <i>a63-80-4-73.deploy.akamaitechnologies.com (63.80.4.74)</i> or <i>cdn-68-142-93-133.sea2.llnw.net (68.142.93.133)</i> and I'm not sure what to do with those.&nbsp; These are all http conversations, using port 80.</p>
             <p>I'm not really sure how to take that info and turn it into a web site.&nbsp; I've tried browsing to those IP's with port 80, but most of the time I get nothing.&nbsp; Any hints?</p>
             <p>Thanks in advance.</p>

               <section> 
                <!-- [DocumentBodyStart:93fb9aa7-7c96-4d46-9abc-7992279875fc] -->
                <div class="jive-rendered-content">
                 <p>You can't get accurate statistics at the HTTP level with NetFlow. This isn't a limitation of NTA: it's due to the fact that most websites are hosted on servers that have multiple sites associated with the same IP address, and are differentiated only by the host headers in the HTTP request. Flow-level data doesn't contain HTTP headers. Furthermore, many sites are hosted on content distribution networks like Akamai and LLNW, which makes flow-based tracking extremely difficult.</p>
                 <p>You need an HTTP-level inspection tool to get accurate information about websites visited.</p>

               <section> 
                <!-- [DocumentBodyStart:f49d0e13-ffa0-41b0-81cc-d7ba87cae018] -->
                <div class="jive-rendered-content">
                 <p>Akamai (akamaitechnologies.com) and LimeLight Networks (llnw.net) are two Content Delivery Networks (CDN) that host a lot of the streaming media (both video and audio) going over the Internet. There are many other CDNs but I believe these may be the most popular, based on the information I'm seeing in NTA. These CDNs have agreements/deals/partnerships with different kinds of web sites ranging from YouTube and Facebook to CNN and MSNBC. </p>
                 <p>The problem here is that not many of these sites disclose who their CDN is and it could change from year to year, it seems. Some might even use more than one CDN so you can see how it could be very difficult to map a CDN to a web site or vice versa. </p>
