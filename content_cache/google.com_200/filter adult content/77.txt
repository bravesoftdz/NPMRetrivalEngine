            <p><span><span>A new children's book in Sweden called <em>Farfar har fyra fruar</em> - "Grandpa has four wives"</span> -<span> was published in Swedish and Somali and is intended for children aged 3-6.</span></span></p> 
            <p><span><span>According to Infowars.com, the book provides an example of the "normalization of Sharia law" in Sweden, coinciding with the arrival of millions of Muslim immigrants.</span></span></p> 
            <p><span><span>According to the brief on the book's jacket, it tells of a girl named Asali, who was never in Somalia but is preparing to go there with her father, "to meet her grandfather and all her grandmothers."</span></span></p> 
            <p><span><span>The book is authored by one Oscar Trimble.</span> <span>The same writer also wrote <em>Mormor är inget spöke</em> - "Grandma isn't a ghost (spook)", intended to normalize the Muslim burka.</span> <span>"Omar greets his grandmother, who came from Somalia," explains the book's brief.</span> <span>"When Halloween comes, Omar wants to dress up as a ghost, like any other child, and he wants his grandmother to accompany him, because it can be scary."</span></span></p> 
            <p><span><span>Unlike other European countries which prohibit polygamy, Sweden recognizes polygamous marriages from other countries and allows up to four women to be registered as partners.</span></span></p> 
            <p><span><span>About 30% of Sweden's current population has a foreign background, and ethnic Swedes are expected to become a minority in their own country within 25 years at most.</span> <span>This is in a country that until a few decades ago was considered one of the most homogeneous populations in the West.</span></span></p> 
            <p><span><span>In Finland, a book entitled <em>How to marry a Finnish girl</em> was published.</span> Some have noted that<span> the book's <a target="_blank" href="http://pbs.twimg.com/media/BoQpqEPIcAAwAT-.jpg">illustration</a> appears to represent a dark-skinned man touching a fair-skinned woman in a bridal gown.</span></span></p>
