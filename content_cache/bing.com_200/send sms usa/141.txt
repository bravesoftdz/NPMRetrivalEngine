                    <!-- google_ad_section_start -->You can send email to a phone to generate SMS messages on it.

                    <br> Something as simple as:
                    <br> echo "This is a test" |mailx -s "Test from Linux" 
                    <a href="mailto:1234567890@carrier.dom">1234567890@carrier.dom</a>
                    <br> Where you replace 1234567890 with the phone number and carrier.dom with the domain of the carrier. Some of the more common ones along with more details can be found at:
                    <br> 
                    <a href="http://www.tech-recipes.com/rx/939/sms_email_cingular_nextel_sprint_tmobile_verizon_virgin/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">http://www.tech-recipes.com/rx/939/s...erizon_virgin/</a>
                    <br> 
                    <br> Of course the above requires you to setup email to allow outbound emails to be sent. How to do that would depend on your mail transport agent (MTA). Most systems have sendmail installed by default but there are others such as postfix that can be used instead.
