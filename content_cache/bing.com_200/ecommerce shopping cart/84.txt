          <div class="wrap"> 
           <h3>What&nbsp;is E-commerce software?</h3> 
           <p>E-commerce software is the store manager for your online shop. The features with each platform vary, but in general, you can expect your platform to manage inventory, collect payments, and organize delivery. You can usually integrate your store quickly with reputable payment gateways so your transactions are secure.</p> 
           <p>E-commerce software also simplifies the process of designing your website. The average person can get started with their own shop without knowing how to code. E-commerce platforms often provide customization options&nbsp;for technical users. After making edits,&nbsp;you can instantly check the site to see what your customers see.</p> 
           <p>&nbsp;</p> 
          </div> 
         </section> 
         <section class="section-3 section" id="section-3" style="<section"> 
          <div class="wrap"> 
           <h2>E-commerce Software Explained</h2> 
           <p>&nbsp;</p> 
           <p>E-commerce platforms fall into two categories:</p> 
           <ul> 
            <li>Self-hosted platforms are based on free and open source software. You get complete control over the site, along with lots of updates from the open source community. With this option, you’ll have to install the software yourself, and find your own hosting provider. Although this process isn’t all that technical, some beginners might find it intimidating.</li> 
            <li>Hosted platforms are an all-in-one product where the user pays one monthly fee for everything. The package includes templates, payment gateways, hosting, and often, transaction fees. The disadvantages are the lack of control, and the monthly payments, which can add up quickly.</li> 
           </ul> 
          </div> 
         </section> 
         <section class="section-4 section" id="section-4" style="<section"> 
          <div class="wrap"> 
           <h2>Who Should Use E-Commerce Software?</h2> 
           <p>If you want to sell products online, you need e-commerce software.</p> 
           <p>For instance, a band can use e-commerce tools to sell digital music and physical merchandise. The digital items would be delivered through an email with a link, while the physical items would be shipped to the customer.</p> 
           <p>More traditional stores can launch an e-commerce version of their existing physical store. Some online stores begin with one product, but some people start stores where they sell hundreds or thousands of different items.</p> 
           <p>You will&nbsp;need an e-commerce platform if you plan to dropship, which means that the manufacturer or supplier stores, packages and ships all products directly to the customers, and you simply take the orders.</p> 
           <p>E-commerce platforms are generally built so that both inexperienced and advanced users can get started quickly. <a href="https://digital.com/go/shopify/">Shopify</a>, for example, offers basic features for beginners, along with HTML and CSS files&nbsp;customization for advanced users.</p> 
           <p>Naturally, some platforms are more complicated than others, and it’s important to examine the level of technical skill needed to use them.</p> 
          </div> 
         </section> 
         <section class="section-5 section 
                     dark-background  " id="section-5" style="background-image: url(https://digital.com/wp-content/uploads/reviewsquirrel-darkbg.jpg);color: #ffffff;"> 
          <div class="wrap"> 
           <h2 id="what's-included">Key E-Commerce Features</h2> 
           <p>The first choice you have to make when going for an e-commerce platform is whether or not you want hosting included or if you’re going to self-host. The latter means you’ll need to purchase a separate web hosting package.</p> 
           <p>Regardless, it’s important to compare the features of the tools that you’re interested in.</p> 
           <p>All reputable systems provide shopping carts and checkout tools, allowing for your customers to place items in the cart and make a payment.&nbsp;Beyond that, look out for the following.</p> 
           <h3 id="product-pages">Product Page Customization</h3> 
           <p>You can expect to find product creation tools for uploading product lists, or creating items from scratch. The product page typically has fields for pricing, pictures, categories, descriptions, titles, and other product metadata.</p> 
           <p>Once published, the product page shows up on the front end. You can usually categorize&nbsp;it in a gallery, collection, or feature it on the store’s homepage.</p> 
           <p>Keep in mind that some ecommerce platforms have limits on the number of products you can create, but this often depends on the pricing package you choose for a hosting solution. All quality self-hosted solutions allow for unlimited products, and the majority of hosted options offer&nbsp;an unlimited plan. The cost for this may vary considerably from one hosted solution&nbsp;to another.</p> 
           <h3 id="payment-gateways">Payment Gateways</h3> 
           <p>A payment gateway allows you to take electronic payments from customers, without necessarily having to sign up for a merchant account.</p> 
           <p>The gateway should conduct&nbsp;fraud tests, collect&nbsp;money, and transfer&nbsp;it to&nbsp;your&nbsp;bank account.</p> 
           <p>Platforms like Shopify have their own payment gateways, but you can typically choose from several third party solutions. Brands like PayPal, Stripe, and Authorize.net are common.&nbsp;Fees vary from gateway to gateway, and sometimes, fees will be waived on certain hosted plans.</p> 
           <h3 id="website-blog">Static Website Pages&nbsp;and Blog</h3> 
           <p>An e-commerce system lets you build a full business website. So although you’re often working with the product pages, you should have access to page and blog publishing too.</p> 
           <p>Shopify and <a href="https://digital.com/go/bigcommerce/">Bigcommerce</a> both offer blogging tools on the backend, but they are far less powerful than the blogging tools in WordPress. <a href="https://digital.com/ecommerce-platforms/vs/bigcommerce-shopify/">Here’s a comparison of the two platforms</a>.</p> 
           <h3 id="marekting-tools">Marketing Tools</h3> 
           <p>You’re won’t&nbsp;find marketing features in all e-commerce software, but the best platforms&nbsp;offer integrated email marketing, gift cards, discount codes, and SEO tools.</p> 
           <p>Sometimes you have to integrate a third party app to achieve the functionality you need.</p> 
           <p>Keep in mind that not all of the marketing features are included with each pricing plan, so decide on how much you want to pay per month by looking at the marketing tools you think would best suit your business.</p> 
           <h3 id="apps">Expanding Your Website’s Functionality</h3> 
           <p>Many e-commerce applications have&nbsp;huge app libraries for expanding the functionality of your store. The apps and integrations should play a large role in your decision,&nbsp;since there’s huge value in automating processes&nbsp;for things like marketing, accounting, analytics, and shipping.</p> 
           <p>You can expect some sort of app integtations&nbsp;when you opt for a hosted e-commerce platform. With&nbsp;self-hosted platforms, this is more variable. Magento has extensions, but they’re not that plentiful. WordPress has an incredible number of plugins that you can add to your website, especially for WooCommerce.</p> 
           <p>As a rule of thumb, you should try to utilize free apps wherever possible. This way, you can keep your costs low and spend the&nbsp;cash on growing the business.</p> 
           <h3 id="templates">Templates and Website Design</h3> 
           <p>Every good hosted ecommerce solution has incredible templates you can use to get started with designing your website. They are generally categorized by industry, so you can go into the library and find the templates that suit&nbsp;your brand.</p> 
           <p>After that, you can customize it by moving sections around, uploading images, and creating pages.</p> 
           <p>A few ecommerce platforms have drag-and drop-website builders or visual tools that make it much easier for beginners with no coding or design experience. These are also great for more experienced designers who would like to speed up the design process.</p> 
           <h3 id="reports">Reports and Analytics</h3> 
           <p>The most basic reports and analytics are usually enough to get a small business started in the right direction. Almost all e-commerce platforms offer statistics about store performance.</p> 
           <p>For example, a standard analytics page on an e-commerce platform shows things like sales, customers, abandoned carts, bounce rate, and the time spent on the site. You can also get more advanced reporting by opting for an app or plugin.</p> 
           <p>Analytics can often be exported and broken down into all sorts of reports. So&nbsp;you can share the information with other people in the company and filter based on date, customer, and other options.</p> 
          </div> 
         </section> 
         <section class="section-6 section" id="section-6" style="<section"> 
          <div class="wrap"> 
           <h2 id="mobile">Mobile Capabilities</h2> 
           <p>Google now considers mobile-friendliness a ranking factor in its search algorithm, so it’s essential for your online store to work and look good on smaller devices like tablets and phones.</p> 
           <p>Many people like shopping online on small screens, so mobile responsiveness is a must from a usability standpoint too.</p> 
           <p>If you’re researching e-commerce platforms and notice that the design templates aren’t mobile friendly, skip that platform entirely. All reputable e-commerce platforms now offer beautifully crafted templates with mobile responsiveness.</p> 
           <p>This means that a mobile version of your site shows up when someone visits it on a smaller device. The website elements snap into place and the text is formatted for a much easier viewing and browsing experience.</p> 
           <p>With self-hosted solutions like WordPress you have to go out and find your own themes. Therefore, you should make responsiveness an essential criteria for picking a theme. Hosted platforms like Bigcommerce have their own template libraries, and you can bet that all of them are going to be responsive.</p>
