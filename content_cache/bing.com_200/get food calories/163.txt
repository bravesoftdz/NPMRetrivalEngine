         <h1 class="program-title">Women, Infants and Children (WIC)</h1> 
         <h2>WIC Food Package Regulatory Requirements</h2> 
         <div class="tabs clearfix"></div> 
         <div class="block block-system block-main block-system-main odd block-without-title" id="block-system-main"> 
          <div class="block-inner clearfix"> 
           <div class="content clearfix"> 
            <article about="/wic/wic-food-package-regulatory-requirements" typeof="foaf:Document" class="node node-page node-published node-not-promoted node-not-sticky author-dsologuren odd clearfix" id="node-page-10121"> 
             <footer class="submitted">
              <span>Last Published: </span>09/07/2017
             </footer> 
             <div class="content clearfix"> 
              <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
               <div class="field-items">
                <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
                 <p><strong>What are the Federal regulatory requirements for WIC-eligible foods?</strong></p> 
                 <p>The following list provides the Federal requirements for WIC-eligible foods. USDA requirements for WIC-eligible foods can be found in <a href="/part-246—special-supplemental-nutrition-program-women-infants-and-children">7 CFR part 246.10</a>. Click<a href="http://www.gpo.gov/fdsys/search/pagedetails.action?collectionCode=CFR&amp;searchPath=Title+7%2FSubtitle+B%2FChapter+II%2FSubchapter+A%2FPart+246&amp;granuleId=&amp;packageId=CFR-2002-title7-vol1&amp;oldPath=Title+7%2FSubtitle+B%2FChapter+II%2FSubchapter+A%2FPart+246&amp;fromPageDetails=true&amp;collapse=true&amp;ycord=690"> here</a> to view the Federal regulation on the WIC food requirements.&nbsp; WIC State agencies must use these requirements when authorizing foods on the State WIC food list. However, State agencies do not have to authorize all foods that meet WIC-eligibility requirements.</p> 
                 <p>The following WIC foods must comply with Food and Drug Administration (FDA) Standards of Identity. To view the Standards of Identity for infant formula, exempt infant formula, fruit/vegetable juices, milks, cheeses, fresh eggs, canned legumes and lentils, peanut butters, carrots and canned tuna visit the FDA web site at <a href="http://www.fda.gov/Food/default.htm" target="_blank">http://www.fda.gov/Food/default.htm</a> and click on the Federal Register and CFR link. &nbsp;</p> 
                 <p><a href="/wic/wic-food-packages">Back to WIC Food Package main page</a></p> 
                 <h2 align="left"><a href="#" name="list" id="list"><strong>WIC-Eligible Foods &nbsp;</strong></a></h2> 
                 <table border="1" height="110" width="100%">
                  <tbody>
                   <tr>
                    <td align="center" height="39"> <p><strong><a href="#ADULT CEREAL">Adult Cereal</a></strong></p> </td> 
                    <td align="center" height="39"> <p><strong><a href="#INFANT CEREAL">Infant Cereal</a></strong></p> </td> 
                    <td align="center" height="39"> <p><strong><a href="#ADULT and INFANT JUICE">Adult &amp; Infant Juice</a></strong></p> </td> 
                    <td align="center" height="39"> <p><strong><a href="#MILK">Milk</a></strong></p> </td> 
                    <td align="center" height="39"> <p><strong><a href="#CHEESE">Cheese</a></strong></p> </td>

                   </tr>

                 <h2><a name="ADULT CEREAL" id="ADULT CEREAL">ADULT CEREAL </a></h2> 
                 <p><strong>Types of Cereal &nbsp;</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>Hot or cold:&nbsp; cooked-instant or non-instant or ready-to-serve</li> 
                  <li>Adult breakfast varieties (e.g., corn flakes, bran flakes, oatmeal, grits) &nbsp;</li> 
                 </ul>
                 <p><strong>Requirements</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>Minimum of 28 milligrams of Iron per 100 grams of dry cereal (i.e., iron = 45% U.S. RDA for adults per 1 ounce dry cereal)</li> 
                  <li>Not more than 21.2 grams of sucrose and other sugars per 100 grams of dry cereal (i.e., not more than 6 grams of sucrose and other sugars per 1 ounce dry cereal) &nbsp;</li> 
                 </ul>
                 <h2><a name="INFANT CEREAL" id="INFANT CEREAL">INFANT CEREAL &nbsp;</a></h2> 
                 <p><strong>Types of Cereal &nbsp;&nbsp;</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>Any plain, dry infant cereal (e.g., rice, barley, mixed grain) &nbsp;</li> 
                 </ul>
                 <p><strong>Requirements&nbsp;</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>Minimum of 45 milligrams of Iron per 100 grams of dry cereal (i.e., Iron = 45% U.S. RDA for infants per ½ ounce dry cereal)&nbsp;</li> 
                 </ul>
                 <p><strong>Not Allowed&nbsp;</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>Wet-pack (jarred) infant cereal*</li> 
                  <li>Infant cereal with added formula (dry or wet-pack)</li> 
                  <li>Infant cereal with added fruit (dry or wet-pack)</li> 
                 </ul>
                 <blockquote>
                  <p>*Note: at the State agency’s discretion wet-pack infant cereal may be issued to homeless individuals.&nbsp; &nbsp;</p> 
                  <p><a href="#list">Back to top of WIC-Eligible Foods List</a></p> 
                 </blockquote> 
                 <h2><a name="ADULT and INFANT JUICE" id="ADULT and INFANT JUICE">ADULT and INFANT JUICE</a></h2> 
                 <p><strong>Types of Juice &nbsp;</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>Any adult or infant fruit and/or vegetable juice or juice blends (e.g., orange, grapefruit, apple, grape, pineapple, tomato, cran-apple)</li> 
                  <li>Single strength</li> 
                  <li>Concentrates &nbsp;(frozen and shelf-stable)</li> 
                 </ul>
                 <p><strong>Requirements &nbsp;</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>100% fruit and/or vegetable juice</li> 
                  <li>Minimum of 30 milligrams of Vitamin C per 100 milliliters of Juice or 72 milligrams of Vitamin C per 8-fluid ounces (i.e., Vitamin C = 90% U.S. RDA for women per 6 fluid ounces juice)</li> 
                  <li>Label should indicate that an 8-ounce serving provides 120% of the FDA’s Daily Value (DV) of Vitamin C</li> 
                  <li>For Infant Juice: minimum&nbsp; of 30 milligrams of Vitamin C per 100 milliliters of Juice or 35.4 milligrams per 4-fluid ounces of juice (i.e., Vitamin C = 100% U.S. RDA for infants per 4 fluid ounces of juice) &nbsp;</li> 
                 </ul>
                 <p><strong>Not Allowed &nbsp;</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>Fruit drinks</li> 
                  <li>Fruit-flavored ades</li> 
                  <li>Sodas</li> 
                  <li>Other beverages that are not 100% juice &nbsp;</li> 
                 </ul>
                 <h2><a name="MILK" id="MILK">MILK</a>&nbsp;&nbsp;</h2> 
                 <p><strong>Types of Milk &nbsp;</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>Fluid*:&nbsp; whole, low-fat, or nonfat</li> 
                  <li>Cultured buttermilk</li> 
                  <li>Evaporated:&nbsp; whole or skim</li> 
                  <li>Dry:&nbsp; whole, low fat, or skim</li> 
                 </ul>
                 <blockquote>
                  <p>*Note: at the State agency’s discretion, special types of fluid milk, e.g., Sweet Acidophilus, Lactaid, and calcium-fortified milk may be substituted. &nbsp;</p> 
                 </blockquote> 
                 <p><strong>Requirements</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>Cow’s milk*</li> 
                  <li>Pasteurized</li> 
                  <li>Unflavored or flavored</li> 
                  <li>400 International Units of Vitamin D per quart</li> 
                  <li>2000 International Units of Vitamin A per quart (low-fat or nonfat) &nbsp;</li> 
                 </ul>
                 <blockquote>
                  <p>*Note: At the State agency’s discretion, goat’s milk may be substituted for cow’s milk. WIC-authorized goat’s milk must meet the same requirements as for cow’s milk. &nbsp;</p> 
                  <p>&nbsp;</p> 
                 </blockquote> 
                 <h2 align="left"><a name="CHEESE" id="CHEESE">CHEESE</a></h2> 
                 <p><strong>Types of Cheese</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>Pasteurized Process American</li> 
                  <li>Brick</li> 
                  <li>Cheddar</li> 
                  <li>Colby*</li> 
                  <li>Monterey Jack*</li> 
                  <li>Mozzarella:&nbsp; part skim or whole</li> 
                  <li>Muenster</li> 
                  <li>Provolone</li> 
                  <li>Swiss &nbsp;</li> 
                 </ul>
                 <blockquote>
                  <p>*Note:&nbsp; Blends of approved cheeses, e.g., Cojack, may be substituted.</p> 
                 </blockquote> 
                 <p><strong>Requirements</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>Must be real cheese (as stated on product label)</li> 
                  <li>Must be domestic cheese</li> 
                 </ul>
                 <p><strong>Not Allowed</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>Cheese foods or spreads</li> 
                  <li>Imported cheeses &nbsp;</li> 
                 </ul>
                 <blockquote>
                  <p>&nbsp;</p> 
                 </blockquote> 
                 <h2 align="left"><a name="EGGS" id="EGGS">EGGS</a></h2> 
                 <p><strong>Types of Eggs/Requirements</strong></p> 
                 <ul>

                 <h2 align="left"><a name="DRIED BEANS/PEAS/PEANUT BUTTER" id="DRIED BEANS/PEAS/PEANUT BUTTER">DRIED BEANS/PEAS/PEANUT BUTTER</a></h2> 
                 <p><strong>Types/Requirements</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>Mature dry beans or peas (e.g., lentils, black, navy, kidney, garbanzo, soy, pinto, and mung beans; Crowder, cow, split and black-eyed peas)</li> 
                  <li>Peanut butter:&nbsp; plain - smooth or chunky, all-natural or processed without added jelly, marshmallow or other mixtures</li> 
                 </ul>
                 <p><strong>Not Allowed</strong></p> 
                 <ul>

                  <li>Peanut butter with added jelly, marshmallow or other mixtures &nbsp;</li>

                  <li>Canned and frozen carrots must contain only the mature root of the carrot packed in water &nbsp; &nbsp;</li> 
                 </ul>

                 <h2 align="left"><a name="WIC-ELIGIBLE MEDICAL FOODS" id="WIC-ELIGIBLE MEDICAL FOODS">WIC-ELIGIBLE MEDICAL FOODS</a></h2> 
                 <p><strong>Types of Medical Foods</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>Concentrated liquid</li> 
                  <li>Powdered</li> 
                  <li>Ready-to-feed (RTF) or ready-to use (RTU) liquid</li> 
                  <li>RTF or RTU solid/bar</li> 
                  <li>Semi-solid formulas having the consistency of “pudding” &nbsp;</li> 
                 </ul>
                 <p><strong>Requirements &nbsp; &nbsp;</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>Medical documentation required for issuance</li> 
                  <li>Medically necessary and prescribed by a physician when conventional foods cannot be consumed by women or children with special dietary needs.</li> 
                  <li>Intended for use as an oral feeding; formulas administered through a nasogastric tube may be substituted.</li> 
                  <li>Should serve as a major source of nutrients but may have to be supplemented by additional special nutritional products. &nbsp;</li> 
                 </ul>
                 <p><strong>Not Allowed &nbsp;</strong></p> 
                 <ul>
                  <li>Formulas used solely for the purpose of enhancing nutrient intake or managing body weight (e.g., vitamin pills, weight control products)</li> 
                  <li>Medicines or drugs</li> 
                  <li>Hyperalimentation feedings (nourishment administered through a vein)</li> 
                  <li>Enzymes</li> 
                  <li>Oral rehydration fluids or electrolyte solutions</li> 
                  <li>Flavoring agents</li> 
                  <li>Feeding utensils or devices (e.g., feeding tubes, bags, pumps) designed to administer a WIC-eligible formula</li> 
                 </ul>
