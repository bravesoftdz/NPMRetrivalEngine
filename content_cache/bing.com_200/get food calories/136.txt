        <h3>7 Steps to Lose Weight By Counting Calories</h3> 
        <div class="row-fluid">
          Calorie counting can be a great way to help you with your weight loss! For many people, it's an eye-opening experience. 
         <h5>1) Calculate Your Calorie Deficit</h5> 
         <p>Calculate your calorie deficit goal using the calculator at the top of the page. Your calorie deficit goals is the amount of food to eat each day to lose weight. It's calculated by using your <a href="/calculator/basal-metabolic-rate-bmr">BMR</a> and activity level, and then subtracting a reasonable amount of calories to lose weight. </p>
         <p><span class="label label-important">Important</span><br>As a general rule of them, women should never go lower than 1200 calories a day; for men, not lower than 1800 calories a day.</p> 
         <h5>2) Track Your Food Daily</h5> 
         <p>Track the food you eat with FitWatch's free online calorie counter. <br></p>
         <p><a href="/subscribe" class="btn btn-green">Create Your Free Calorie Counter Account</a></p>
         <br> Or search for the calorie count of the foods and record the numbers on this 
         <a href="/diary/fooddiary">printable food diary</a>. 
         <p></p> 
         <h5>3) Compare the Numbers</h5> 
         <p>Compare your total daily calories eaten against your calorie deficit goal. Check whether you're eating more or less than your calorie deficit goal.</p> 
         <h5>4) Make Changes and Adjustments</h5>
         <p>If you're eating more than the goal, <a href="/blog/7-easy-ways-to-cut-calories-without-making-big-sacrifices">cut back on high calories foods</a> that have no nutritional value (e.g.: junk food). Eat smaller portions and measure out your <a ahref="/blog/7-things-you-need-to-know-about-serving-sizes">serving sizes</a>.</p> 
         <h5>5) Exercise Daily to Burn More Calories</h5> 
         <p>Start exercising and keep track of how many <a href="/blog/how-many-calories-do-you-burn-in-a-day">calories you burn in a day</a>. You can estimate the calories burned by searching the <a href="/caloriesburned">Exercise Database</a>.</p> 
         <h5>6) Track Your Progress</h5> 
         <p><a href="/blog/4-ways-to-track-your-weight-loss-progress">Track your progress</a> and adjust when necessary, either lowering your goal or exercising more. </p> 
         <h5>7) Listen to your body.</h5>
         <p> You should feel full of energy when losing weight. You shouldn't feel light-headed or tired. These may be signs you're not eating enough or over-exercising.</p>
