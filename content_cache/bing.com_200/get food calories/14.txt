            <p>Healthy weight management requires a balance between a healthy diet and physical activity.</p>
            <p>Most of us want simpler ways to maintain a healthy weight and get the most nutrition out of what we eat. There are no magic answers... But there is one powerful tool we often overlook - the Nutrition Facts label on packaged foods.</p>
            <p>Make Your Calories Count can help you quickly make smart food choices.</p>
            <p>We'll show you how.</p>

            <p>9. Page 9</p>
            <p>It's not hard... Just a few simple steps.</p>

            <h4><a name="step1">Step 1: Size Up Your Serving &amp; Calories</a></h4>
            <p>1. (page 10.)</p>
            <p>Make Your Calories Count. Use the Nutrition Facts Label for Healthy Weight Management.</p>
            <p>Size Up Your Serving and Calories.</p>
            <p>When you select or compare foods, see what the label says about the serving size and calories. Ask yourself:</p>
            <p>What is the serving size?<br>How many servings are in the container?<br>How many calories are in a single serving?</p>
            <p>(Picture of the label character, with two thought bubbles as if it is thinking: How many servings to you expect to eat? How many Calories are in the amount you expect to eat?)</p>
            <p>(Picture of the top part of the label shows: Nutrition Facts. Serving Size 1 cup (228g); Servings Per Container 2; Amount per serving. Calories 250. Calories from Fat 110.)</p>
            <p>2. (page 11.)</p>
            <p>Size Up Your Serving and Calories (continued).<br>Serving Size.</p>

            <p>52. (page 124)</p>
            <p>It doesn't have to be difficult to judge if a food is right for you. It's only three steps:<br>1. Size up your serving.<br>What is the serving size?<br>How many servings are you eating? (one half, 1, 2 or more?).<br>How many calories are listed in a single serving? (is it high or low in calories).<br>How many calories are in the amount you actually eat?</p>
            <p>2. See what's in it for you:<br>Use the Quick Guide to %DV: 5% DV or less is low and 20% DV or more is high.<br>Is this a wise food choice in terms of the nutrients you want to get enough of or ones you want to limit?</p>
            <p>3. Judge if it's right for you.<br>Is this a wise food choice in terms of calories? (Remember that 400 calories/serving or more is high).<br>Is it a wise choice in terms of nutrients?<br>Is this a wise choice for a single food; a snack; a meal?<br>Should you look for an alternative?</p>
            <p>53. (page 125)</p>
            <p>The question of whether a food is right for you ultimately depends on many factors.</p>
            <p>These may include whether you are trying to gain, lose, or maintain your weight; how many calories you need each day based on age, gender, and level of physical activity; and any special health or dietary concerns you may have.</p>
            <p>A food you like may still be part of a healthy daily diet – if eaten in moderation and balanced by other smart choices throughout the day.</p>
            <p>Use the Nutrition Facts Label to make those choices easier ... And to make you calories count!</p>
