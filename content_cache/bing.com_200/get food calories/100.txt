     <h2 align="center" id="">&nbsp;</h2> 
     <div id="section_title">
      Diet Diary Analysis
     </div> 
     <p><strong>Objective:</strong> Recognize whether your current eating and drinking habits address your total energy and macronutrient needs via analysis of a two day diet diary. Your completed assignment will include: an analysis of your two days of food consumption and two tables listing all of the food and beverages you consumed on each day.</p> 
     <p class="light_gray_box">IMPORTANT NOTE: The information from this assignment will be used later in the course when you create a three day dietary plan.</p> 
     <p><strong>Description: </strong>You will examine two days of your eating and drinking identifying the caloric value for each food and drink item you consume excluding non-caloric beverages like water, unsweetened coffee or tea, or artificially sweetened beverages with zero calories, etc. Additionally, you will list the carbohydrate, fat (total fat, saturated fat, polyunsaturated fat, monounsaturated fat and trans fat) and proteins measured in grams. You will also include the grams of fiber for each of the food and beverage items you consume. Hopefully, the days you selected for analysis will be a typical week day and weekend day of eating and drinking for you. You must select days in which you consumed food. It is not acceptable to submit a diary that indicates you did not eat or drink or you ate or drank very little because of extenuating circumstances, e.g. too busy, ill, etc.<br> You will also identify your Body Mass Index (BMI) using one of the many Body Mass Index calculators on the Internet. </p> 
     <p>After recording all of the food and beverages you consumed and after you have identified the macronutrient values in grams of all foods you listed, you will assess whether your current diet is adequate, excessive or deficient in meeting your total energy and macronutrient needs. You will need to visit the&nbsp;<a href="http://www.mayoclinic.org/calorie-calculator/itt-20084939" target="_blank">Calorie Calculator </a>web site to determine your approximate caloric need. You will compare your caloric intake for the two days comparing it to the total energy need you identified after visiting the Calorie Calculator web site.</p>
     <br> Please review the 
     <a href="energy_macro_need.html">Energy and Macronutrient</a> web page that will explain how you will calculate your percentage of calories for energy, carbohydrate, fat and protein. 
     <p></p> 
     <p class="jump_out_spread">You will be submitting this assignment as an attachment within the Assignments area of Blackboard. The document you submit must combine all of the contents for this assignment into one .doc, .docx, or .rtf document. DO NOT send multiple documents and/or file types other than .doc, .docx, or .rtf file types.<br> You MUST transfer any data from any calorie counting software/apps to a table within the same word processed document you submit for this assignment. <strong>Sorry-- no exceptions!</strong></p> 
     <hr> 
     <p>The assignment you submit should include an analysis of your findings from the two day diary you recorded.</p> 
     <p>The analysis should include the following paragraphs:</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>First Paragraph: Identification of your current energy need and your BMI. You should identify whether you agree or disagree with your energy need and why you agree or disagree. You should also identify whether your BMI falls within normal ranges or whether you are underweight, overweight or obese. </li> 
      <li>Second Paragraph: Explain your current dietary practices and whether your current practices address your energy, macronutrient and vegetable and fruit needs. Be sure you identify your fiber intake as well.</li> 
      <li>Third Paragraph: Describe how you plan to address any excesses or deficiencies in your energy, macronutrient, vegetable, fruit and fiber intake. You need to be specific! 
       <ul> 
        <li>If your current dietary practices perfectly address your needs in these areas, please explain how you achieve this goal.</li> 
        <li>If you are underweight or overweight you should identify how you plan to increase or decrease your energy intake to get your BMI within the healthy range.</li> 
       </ul> </li> 
      <li>Fourth Paragraph: Explain any health consequences that may be of a concern if you continue your current dietary practices. Additionally, describe how the changes you propose will not only alleviate your concerns, but also describe how they will provide potential health benefits.</li> 
      <li>Fifth paragraph: This is your conclusion. Briefly summarize the major points from the previous three paragraphs and evaluate the worth of going through the process of recording a diet diary for two days. </li> 
     </ul> 
     <hr> 
     <p><strong>DIET DIARY ANALYSIS - EXAMPLE</strong></p> 
     <p>My calorie consumption is excessive for a male of my age and activity level. Based upon the Calorie Calculator web site I need 2500 calories per day to maintain my weight. I am obese according to the BMI Calculator: 32.6. My calorie consumption is excessive for a male of my age and activity level. Based upon the Calorie Calculator web site I need 2500 calories per day to maintain my weight. I am obese according to the BMI Calculator: 32.6. I know I am overweight, but the term obese hurts! I would not consider myself obese. As far as energy intake needs I am not quite sure how much energy I need to consume to maintain a healthy body weight because it has been a long time since I have been at a healthy weight. I am striving to get 60% of my calories from carbohydrates, 20% of my calories from fats and proteins. I also need approximately 30g of fiber each day.</p> 
     <p>Based upon my food consumption, I would say that I am extremely excessive in my calorie intake. I nearly tripled what I would need in a typical day. Over the course of the two days my carbohydrate, fat and protein intake were way too high. I probably eat out a couple of times every week and that really kicked me well over the edge. My fat intake is incredibly high especially saturated fat. I think I could have really improved my protein choices on this day. Percentage-wise I was much closer to meeting my goal as far as my protein intake goes. I also love fruits and vegetables, but I rarely consume them. I was close to meeting my fiber needs. I'm not sure how as I ate very few servings of vegetables and no fruit servings. Sometimes I have very little time and am in a rush (like so many people) and I just go out and buy what tastes good. </p> 
     <p>I definitely need to make some significant changes. First, I need to drastically cut down on my total energy intake. This could best be achieved by eating three much smaller meals each day with a small mid-afternoon snack. I need to add whole grains to my breakfast. I can do this by eating oatmeal and/or some whole grain toast. I could also start my day off with a piece of fresh fruit. I can add a salad to my lunch and perhaps some lettuce to a lean protein sandwich like tuna or turkey. I could consume another piece of fruit with this meal. For my afternoon snack I could choose a green smoothie with spinach, water, a banana and some blueberries. For dinner I can choose some whole grain pasta with some fresh, sautéed vegetables. My dessert could be a small Greek yogurt with some honey and fresh fruit topped with some chopped almonds.</p> 
     <p>I must make these changes soon as high fat diets lead to cardiovascular disease and my uncle who was only 53 recently had triple heart bypass surgery. My father is a type 2 diabetic and my mother is obese. The proposed changes may allow me to decrease my BMI and lower my risks for cardiovascular disease and type 2 diabetes. Additionally, by eating an energy sufficient and nutrient dense diet I may start to have more energy and begin an exercise program.</p> 
     <p>This assignment really helped me identify why I have become obese. The changes I proposed should allow me to get to a healthy body weight and decrease the risks associated with being obese.</p> 
     <p><br> </p> 
     <p>Total Energy Need: 2500 calories</p> 
     <p>Body Mass Index calculation: 32.6 (obese)</p>
