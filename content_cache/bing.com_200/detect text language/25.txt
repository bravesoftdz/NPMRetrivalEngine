           Hi,
           <br> 
           <br> 
           <em><strong>Fast answer</strong></em>: http://ntextcat.codeplex.com/
           <br> 
           <br> 
           <em><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Long answer</strong></span></em>:
           <br> 
           <br> Currently the best way seems to use classifiers trained to classify piece of text into one (or more) of languages from predefined set.
           <br> 
           <br> There is a Perl tool called TextCat. It has language models for 74 most popular languages. There is a huge number of ports of this tool into different programming languages.
           <br> 
           <br> There were no ports in .Net. So I have written one: NTextCat.codeplex.com.
           <br> 
           <br> It is pure .Net Framework dll + command line interface to it. It is fully compatible with those 69 language models mentioned, so it is capable of detecting language out of the box.
           <br> 
           <br> Any feedback is very appreciated! New ideas and feature requests are welcomed too :)
           <br> 
           <br> Alternative is to use numerous online services (e.g. one from Google mentioned, detectlanguage.com, langid.net, etc.).
           <br> 
           <br> The easiest way to determine the language of text specified is like this:
           <br> 
           <p><span style="font-family: arial; color: #000000;"><br> </span></p> 
           <p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-family: 'courier new';"><span style="color: #0000ff;">var</span> languageIdentifier = <span style="color: #0000ff;">new</span> <span style="color: #4bacc6;">LanguageIdentifier</span>(); <br> </span><span style="font-family: 'courier new';"><span style="color: #4bacc6;">IEnumerable</span>&lt;<span style="color: #0000ff;">string</span>, <span style="color: #0000ff;">double</span>&gt; languages = languageIdentifier.ClassifyText<span style="color: #c0504d;">(“Any piece of text language of which you would like to know”</span>).ToList(); <br> </span><span style="font-family: 'courier new';"><span style="color: #0000ff;">var</span> mostCertainLanguage = languages.FirstOrDefault(); <br> </span><span style="font-family: 'courier new';"><span style="color: #0000ff;">if</span> (mostCertainLanguage != <span style="color: #0000ff;">null</span>)&nbsp; <br> </span><span style="font-family: 'courier new';">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <span style="color: #4bacc6;"> Console</span>.<span style="color: #000000;">WriteLine</span>(<span style="color: #c0504d;">"Language of text is {0} with uncertainty {1}"</span>, mostCertainLanguage.Item1, mostCertainLanguage.Item2);&nbsp; <br> </span><span style="font-family: 'courier new'; color: #0000ff;">else <br> </span><span style="font-family: 'courier new';">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <span style="color: #4bacc6;"> Console</span>.<span style="color: #000000;">WriteLine</span>(<span style="color: #c0504d;">"Language couldn’t be identified with acceptable degree of certainty"</span>); </span></p>
