        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">How to Set Safe Search in Bing to filter out adult content</h1>
        <p class="entry-meta"><time class="entry-time" itemprop="datePublished" datetime="2012-09-27T06:30:52+00:00">September 27, 2012</time> modified <time class="entry-modified-time" itemprop="dateModified" datetime="2016-10-16T20:10:34+00:00">October 16, 2016</time> <br><span class="entry-categories">Filed Under: <a href="http://www.bewebsmart.com/category/safe-search/" rel="category tag">Safe Search</a></span></p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p><a href="http://www.bing.com"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1717" title="Bing" src="http://www.bewebsmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/bing-logo-sm.jpg" alt="Bing" width="160" height="120"></a>Do you use Bing? I dont think Bing has become a verb the same way that Google has.&nbsp; Ive never heard anyone say let me Bing that.&nbsp; Microsoft is trying to compete with Google for a bigger portion of the search market.&nbsp; They recently launched a site where you can compare a Google search with a Bing search side-by-side.&nbsp; Its called <a title="Bing It On" href="http://www.bingiton.com/" target="_blank">www.bingiton.com</a>&nbsp;  clever, right?</p>
        <div id="bhbadge_Featured" class="bhbadge" style="display: inline;">
         <a href="http://www.blogher.com?from=bhfbadge" target="_blank"><img class="alignright" title="Featured on BlogHer.com" src="http://www.blogher.com/files/edbadge_Featured.jpg" alt="Featured on BlogHer.com" width="120" height="100" border="0"></a>
        </div>
        <p><strong>From a safe search perspective, Bing vs. Google is a tie</strong>.&nbsp; Each has its pros and cons.&nbsp; Bing blocks out more images and video with moderate filtering, which is a plus.&nbsp; However, they do not offer a safe search lock, making it easier for your clever offspring to remove the filter.</p>
        <p>First Im going to show you how to set Bing Safe Search on the computer.&nbsp; Then Ill explain how to do the same on a mobile device.</p>
        <h2>Enable Bing Safe Search on your Computer</h2>
        <p>1. Go to Bing. &nbsp;Click the gear in the upper right of the screen for Preferences. &nbsp; Or, just use this link: <a href="http://www.bing.com/account/general">http://www.bing.com/account/general</a>.</p>
        <p>2. On the Preferences page, change the <strong>SafeSearch</strong> filtering from <strong>Moderate</strong> to <strong>Strict</strong>.</p>
        <p><img style="border: 1px solid #cccccc;" title="Change Bing SafeSearch setting from Moderate to Strict" src="http://www.bewebsmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/bingsafesearch.jpg" alt="" width="564" height="136"></p>
        <p>Note that you dont have an option to LOCK the strict setting.&nbsp; This is why Bing recommends and provides a link to <a href="https://account.microsoft.com/family/about">Windows Live Family Safety</a>.&nbsp; This is what I use on our home PC.</p>
        <p>3. Scroll down to the bottom of the page and click <strong>Save</strong>.&nbsp; This returns you to the Bing home page.</p>
        <h3>Try it!</h3>
        <p>Before you enable these settings, you may want to run a test to see how this works.&nbsp; Before I changed the Bing safe search setting to Strict, I searched for topless dancers.&nbsp; I received the message:</p>
        <blockquote>
         <p><em>Your current Bing SafeSearch setting filters out results that might return adult content. To view those results as well, change your SafeSearch setting.</em></p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>This is better than Google, where I did get results even with moderate filtering.&nbsp; However, the message included a link to Learn more, and from there you can easily change the setting.</p>
        <p>With filtering set to OFF, you (or your curious child) will see plenty of dancers strutting their stuff.&nbsp; When turning filtering off, a prompt comes up stating you must&nbsp;be 18 or older to change the setting.&nbsp; Will your child comply?&nbsp; This is why the lack of a LOCK is a problem.</p>
        <h2>Enable Bing Safe Search on a Mobile Device</h2>
        <p>As Ive mentioned, the best way to block adult-oriented content on a mobile web browser is to use a kid-safe browser.&nbsp; On iPod touch and iPad you can&nbsp;<a title="How to Restrict Safari and YouTube in iPod touch and iPad" href="http://www.bewebsmart.com/youtube/restrict-youtube-and-safari/">restrict the default Safari browser</a>, and install an alternative browser that already filters out adult content.&nbsp;&nbsp;<a title="Kid Safe Browsers for iPod Touch and iPad" href="http://www.bewebsmart.com/ipod-ipad-iphone/kid-safe-browsers/">Choose from these kid-friendly browsers for iPod touch</a>.</p>
        <p>Other mobile devices, phones, and tablets may not have the same choice&nbsp;of kid-safe browsers.&nbsp; If thats the case, follow these steps to <strong>enable Strict Search in Bing on a mobile device</strong>.</p>
        <p>These instructions and screenshots are using an iPhone, but should work about the same on an iPod touch or other mobile device.</p>
        <p>1. Go to Bing (I like the pretty background).</p>
        <p>2. Scroll down and click Settings.</p>
        <p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1709" title="Bing Mobile Settings" src="http://www.bewebsmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/bingmobilesettings.jpg" alt="Bing Mobile Settings" width="320" height="480" srcset="http://www.bewebsmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/bingmobilesettings-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.bewebsmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/bingmobilesettings.jpg 320w" sizes="(max-width: 320px) 100vw, 320px"></p>
        <p>&nbsp;</p>
        <p>3. Youll most likely see that safe search is set to <strong>moderate</strong>.&nbsp; Click once to change it to <strong>strict</strong>.</p> 
        <figure id="attachment_1710" style="width: 320px" class="wp-caption alignnone">
         <img class="size-full wp-image-1710" title="Bing Mobile moderate safe search setting" src="http://www.bewebsmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/bingmobilemod.jpg" alt="Click the word moderate to change it to Strict" width="320" height="480" srcset="http://www.bewebsmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/bingmobilemod-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.bewebsmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/bingmobilemod.jpg 320w" sizes="(max-width: 320px) 100vw, 320px">
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Click the word moderate to change it to Strict
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>&nbsp;</p>
        <p>4. Scroll down and click Done.</p>
        <p><strong>Try It</strong></p>
        <p>I ran a search for my usual test-search-term and got the following message.&nbsp; Again, notice how you are provided a link to disable SafeSearch, along with the 18-and-over warning.&nbsp; This might stop some kids in their tracks, but not all.&nbsp; Keep that in mind if Bing is the search engine of choice in your household.</p>
        <p><a href="http://www.bewebsmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/bingmobilewarning.jpg"><img class="alignnone wp-image-1711" title="Bing mobile warning" src="http://www.bewebsmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/bingmobilewarning.jpg" alt="Can't show results because SafeSearch is set to Strict" width="256" height="384" srcset="http://www.bewebsmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/bingmobilewarning-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.bewebsmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/bingmobilewarning.jpg 320w" sizes="(max-width: 256px) 100vw, 256px"></a>&nbsp;&nbsp;<a href="http://www.bewebsmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/bingmobileoff.jpg"><img class="alignnone wp-image-1712" title="Warning before turning off Bing safe search in mobile" src="http://www.bewebsmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/bingmobileoff.jpg" alt="Warning before turning off Bing safe search in mobile" width="256" height="384" srcset="http://www.bewebsmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/bingmobileoff-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.bewebsmart.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/bingmobileoff.jpg 320w" sizes="(max-width: 256px) 100vw, 256px"></a></p>
        <p>&nbsp;</p>
        <p>Give it a go  then leave a comment and let me know how this worked with your before and after searches!</p>
        <p>&nbsp;</p>
        <p><strong>More about SafeSearch in Bing:</strong></p>
