              <h2 class="uppercase color-match mar-top-24px">Description</h2>
              <div class="wysiwygContent mar-top-12px">
               <p style="MARGIN: 0px"><b><i>Exploring&nbsp;the questions of today</i></b></p> 
               <p style="MARGIN: 0px">&nbsp;</p> 
               <p style="MARGIN: 0px"><i>Sociology Now</i> reflects the discipline today and&nbsp;explores the big questions about multiculturalism and globalization that sociologists ask.</p> 
               <p style="MARGIN: 0px">&nbsp;</p> 
               <p style="MARGIN: 0px">It explains&nbsp;how the classic concerns of sociology–social order, social conflict, social interaction–have been reshaped by&nbsp;new insights about multiculturalism and globalization.&nbsp;</p> 
               <p style="MARGIN: 0px">&nbsp;</p> 
               <p style="MARGIN: 0px">Sociology has always offered a way to make sense of the complex and sometimes contradictory forces that shape our social lives in any era. As <i>Sociology Now</i>&nbsp;explains sociology as both a&nbsp;body of knowledge and a "way of seeing," it shows how the forces of globalization and multiculturalism have come to preoccupy sociologists and influence the way they look at the events and experiences of the early twenty first century. Michael Kimmel, a leading sociologist and gender researcher, and co-author Amy Aronson, a journalist and media scholar, address questions regarding these forces head-on as they make a compelling case for the importance of sociology in the contemporary world. </p> 
               <p style="MARGIN: 0px">&nbsp;</p> 
               <p style="MARGIN: 0px">The Census Update&nbsp;program incorporates 2010 Census data into a course—simply and easily. The components of the Census Update Program include an updated census edition with all charts and graphs—to reflect the results of the 2010 Census. In addition, <i>A Short Introduction to the U.S. Census </i>is available and an updated MySocLab.</p>

               <p style="MARGIN: 0px"><b>Teaching &amp; Learning Experience</b></p> 
               <ul> 
                <li> 
                 <div style="MARGIN: 0px">
                  <i>Personalize Learning — </i>MySocLab delivers proven results in helping students succeed, provides engaging experiences that personalize learning, and comes from a trusted partner with educational expertise and a deep commitment to helping students and instructors achieve their goals.
                 </div> </li>
                <li> 
                 <div style="MARGIN: 0px">
                  <i>Improve Critical Thinking</i> — Each chapter opener asks students to consider a paradox that illustrates the complexity of sociology.
                 </div> </li>
                <li> 
                 <div style="MARGIN: 0px">
                  <i>Engage Students —</i> Unique pedagogical features help students see sociology as a science and understand how sociologists use research data.
                 </div> </li>
                <li> 
                 <div style="MARGIN: 0px">
                  <i>Explore Theory — </i>Theory is discussed in context of multiculturalism and globalization.

              <br>
              <b>Michael S. Kimmel</b> is a Professor of Sociology at SUNY at Stony Brook.&nbsp; He is the author of 
              <i>Guyland: The Perilous World Where Boys Become Men</i> (HarperCollins, 2008) which was featured on the Today Show, Good Morning America and featured on over one hundred radio and newspaper and blog reviews.&nbsp; His other books include 
              <i>Changing Men</i> (1987), 
              <i>Men’s Lives</i>(8
              <sup>th</sup> edition, 2009) 
              <i>Against the Tide: Profeminist Men in the United States, 1776-1990</i> (1992), 
              <i>The Politics of Manhood</i> (1996),
              <i> Manhood: A Cultural History</i> (1996, 10
              <sup>th</sup> anniversary second edition, 2006), and 
              <i>The Gendered Society</i> (3
              <sup>rd</sup> edition, 2008).&nbsp; He co-edited 
              <i>The Encyclopedia on Men and Masculinities</i> (2004) and 
              <i>Handbook of Studies on Men and Masculinities</i> (2004).&nbsp; He is the founder and editor of 
              <u>Men and Masculinities</u>, the field’s premier scholarly journal, a book series on Gender and Sexuality at New York University Press, and edited the Sage Series on Men and Masculinities.&nbsp; He lectures extensively in corporations and on campuses in the U.S. and abroad.&nbsp; He lives in Brooklyn, New York with his wife and co-author, Amy Aronson, and their 10 year old son, Zachary. 
              <p style="MARGIN: 0px">&nbsp;&nbsp;</p> 
              <p style="MARGIN: 0px"><b>Amy Aronson</b> is Assistant Professor of Journalism and Media Studies at Fordham University. She is the author of <i>Taking Liberties: Early American Women's Magazines and Their Readers</i> and an editor of the international quarterly, <i>Media History</i>. She has co-edited several books, including a centennial edition of Charlotte Perkins Gilman's <i>Women&nbsp; and Economics</i>&nbsp; and the two-volume <i>Encyclopedia of Men and Masculinities</i>, which was honored by the New York Public Library with a Best of Reference Award in 2004. A former editor at <i>Working Woman</i> and <i>Ms.</i>, her work has also appeared in publications including <i>Business Week</i>, <i>Global Journalist</i> and the Sunday supplement of <i>The Boston Globe</i>.</p>
