       <h1 class="entry-title">2010 U.S. Census datasets are now available</h1> 
       <div class="entry-meta"> 
        <span class="meta-sep">by</span> 
        <a href="https://blogs.esri.com/esri/arcgis/author/ginger/" title="Posts by ArcGIS Content Team">ArcGIS Content Team</a> 
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">on</span> 
        <a href="https://blogs.esri.com/esri/arcgis/2012/03/21/2010-u-s-census-datasets-are-now-available/" title="9:30 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">March 21, 2012</span></a> 
       </div>
       <!-- .entry-meta --> 
       <div class="entry-content"> 
        <ul class="social"> 
         <li> 
          <!-- Start WP Socializer Plugin - Sharethis Button --> <span class="st_facebook_hcount" st_title="2010 U.S. Census datasets are now available" st_url="http%3A%2F%2Fblogs.esri.com%2Fesri%2Farcgis%2F2012%2F03%2F21%2F2010-u-s-census-datasets-are-now-available%2F" displaytext="share"></span><span class="st_twitter_hcount" st_title="2010 U.S. Census datasets are now available" st_url="http%3A%2F%2Fblogs.esri.com%2Fesri%2Farcgis%2F2012%2F03%2F21%2F2010-u-s-census-datasets-are-now-available%2F" displaytext="share"></span><span class="st_email_hcount" st_title="2010 U.S. Census datasets are now available" st_url="http%3A%2F%2Fblogs.esri.com%2Fesri%2Farcgis%2F2012%2F03%2F21%2F2010-u-s-census-datasets-are-now-available%2F" displaytext="share"></span><span class="st_sharethis_hcount" st_title="2010 U.S. Census datasets are now available" st_url="http%3A%2F%2Fblogs.esri.com%2Fesri%2Farcgis%2F2012%2F03%2F21%2F2010-u-s-census-datasets-are-now-available%2F" displaytext="share"></span> 
          <!-- End WP Socializer Plugin - Sharethis Button --> </li> 
        </ul>
        <p>We are pleased to announce that the new 2010 U.S. Census datasets with their new geometry and attributes are now available as <a title="2010 U.S. Census layer packages on ArcGIS.com" href="http://www.arcgis.com/home/group.html?q=USA%20census%20layer%20package&amp;t=group&amp;owner=esri&amp;title=ESRI%20Data%20%26%20Maps&amp;start=1" target="_blank">layer packages on ArcGIS.com</a>. Block Group, Tract, County, and State are all represented as polygons with over 40 attribute fields containing population totals by age and race, along with family and household information. Census Blocks are represented as points with total population and household information.</p> 
        <p><em>Average family size by Block Group</em></p> 
        <p><img src="http://downloads.esri.com/blogs/arcgisonline/average_family_size_by_block_group.jpg" alt="Average family size by Block Group"></p> 
        <p>Visit the <a href="http://resources.arcgis.com/content/data-maps/10.0/census" target="_blank">Esri Data &amp; Maps United States Census page</a> on the ArcGIS Content Resource Center for a list of all the census layers available for download on ArcGIS.com.</p> 
        <p><span id="more-7883"></span><em>Farm density by County</em></p> 
        <p><img src="http://downloads.esri.com/blogs/arcgisonline/farm_density_by_county.jpg" alt="Farm density by County"></p> 
        <p><br><br><br> <em>Population density by State</em></p> 
        <p><img src="http://downloads.esri.com/blogs/arcgisonline/pop_density_by_state.jpg" alt="Population density by State"></p> 
        <p><br><br><br> <em>Median age by Tract</em></p> 
        <p><img src="http://downloads.esri.com/blogs/arcgisonline/median_age_by_tract.jpg" alt="Median age by Tract"></p> 
        <p><br><br><br> <em>Tracts with FIPS ID</em></p> 
        <p><img src="http://downloads.esri.com/blogs/arcgisonline/tracts_with_fips_id.jpg" alt="Tracts with FIPS ID"><br> <br><br><br> You can <a href="http://links.esri.com/esridatamapsgroups">download</a> a collection of layer packages through the Esri Data &amp; Maps Group on ArcGIS.com.</p> 
        <p>If you have other feedback or comments, please post them to the <a title="ArcGIS Online forum" href="http://forums.arcgis.com/forums/30-ArcGIS-Online" target="_blank">ArcGIS Online forum</a>.</p> 
        <p><em>Contributed by James Shimota</em></p>
