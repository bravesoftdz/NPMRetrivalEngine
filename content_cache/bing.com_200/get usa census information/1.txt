             <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
              <p>The "Universe" for that particular variable<code>"B01003-001"</code> is TOTAL POPULATION, it is not broken down any further than that, so you are not able to get the 12+ ages from <code>"B01003-001"</code>, only the population of the whole state or county or tract you are pulling from at the time.</p> 
              <p>However, you can pull and aggregate a data frame for the tables you would like, using <code>B01001</code> and the suffixes <code>_001</code> through <code>_049</code> to pull out the populations by age and gender and then add them up.</p> 
              <p><strong>OR</strong></p> 
              <p>You could pull the entire population as you have above and subtract out the ages (both male and female NOT in your target group, which is much less work given the breakdown of age groups for children compared to the rest of life)</p> 
              <p>The one thing you will have a hard time with is getting 12+, because the highest groupings you would want to exclude are 10-14...which means you could not select under 12</p> 
              <p><strong><em>General Age by Sex for All Races Codes:</em></strong></p>
