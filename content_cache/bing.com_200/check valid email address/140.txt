          <div class="col-xs-12 editorial "> 
           <p><strong>Definition:</strong></p> 
           <p><strong>A USPS<span>®</span>&nbsp;valid address is an address that abides by United States Postal Service standards and formatting</strong>.</p> 
           <p>When addressing mail within the United States, it is important to adhere to the USPS<span>®</span>&nbsp;valid address format. While many believe that they know the appropriate way to address an envelope or package, a USPS<span>®</span>&nbsp;valid address has features that are not commonly followed by the majority of mail senders.</p> 
           <p>A USPS<span>®</span>&nbsp;valid address contains the following:</p> 
           <ul> 
            <li>All capital letters</li> 
            <li>A valid and deliverable address</li> 
            <li>Included company name, apartment number, or suite number if applicable</li> 
            <li>Valid USPS<span>®</span>&nbsp;acronyms and abbreviations</li> 
            <li>A valid ZIP+4<span>®</span></li> 
            <li>No commas or periods</li> 
           </ul> 
           <p>The example below shows a USPS<span>®</span>&nbsp;valid address:</p> 
           <p><em>JOHN SMITH&nbsp;</em><br><em>COMPANY INC</em><br><em>123 N MAIN ST STE 45&nbsp;</em><br><em>BOSTON MA 02110-1615</em></p> 
           <p>Ensuring that an address is a USPS<span>®</span>&nbsp;valid address is an important step in reducing amounts of returned mail and improving mail delivery time.</p>
