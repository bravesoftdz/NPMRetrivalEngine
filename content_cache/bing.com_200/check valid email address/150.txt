    <h1>Don’t lose customers because of typos</h1> 
   </div> 
   <section class="feature"> 
    <div class="container"> 
     <div class="group"> 
      <div class="span6"> 
       <h3>Maximize deliverability with comprehensive verification</h3> 
       <p>Mailgun performs a three-step validation check for each address we verify, including a check against the mailbox provider to determine if the address is valid.</p> 
      </div> 
      <div class="span6"> 
       <div class="validator" id="js-validator"> 
        <form> 
         <label for="login_username">Try entering an invalid email</label> 
         <input type="text" name="login_username" id="login_username"> 
         <input type="submit" name="validate_submit" id="validate_submit" value="Validate"> 
         <div id="status"></div> 
        </form> 
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
    </div> 
   </section> 
   <section class="feature"> 
    <div class="container"> 
     <div class="group"> 
      <div class="span6 aligncenter"> 
       <img src="https://www.mailgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/1-secure.png" alt="Approved validation" height="200"> 
      </div> 
      <div class="span6"> 
       <h3>Validation based on billions of sent emails</h3> 
       <p>RFC states the formal rules for email addresses. However, in the real world ESPs accept addresses that are not RFC-valid, and reject addresses that are.</p> 
       <p>When we built our validator, we did based on real-world data + RFC spec to ensure more accurate validation.</p> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
    </div> 
   </section> 
   <section class="feature"> 
    <div class="container"> 
     <h3>Custom ESP grammar</h3> 
     <div class="group"> 
      <div class="span6"> 
       <p>All major ESPs have their own set up custom grammar that impact whether an email address is valid in the real world.</p> 
       <p>We’ve integrated custom grammar checks for Gmail, Hotmail, and Yahoo into our email validator and are always adding more custom grammar checks as we discover these rules.</p> 
      </div> 
      <div class="span6"> 
       <ul class="checklist"> 
        <li><i class="icon-remove"></i> m+mailgun@gmail.com<br> <span class="subtle">Doesn’t meet Gmail’s local parts rules.</span></li> 
        <li><i class="icon-ok"></i> michael….is….awesome18@gmail.com<br> <span class="subtle">Meets local parts rules because dots are ignored.</span></li> 
       </ul> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
    </div> 
   </section> 
   <section class="feature"> 
    <div class="container"> 
     <div class="group"> 
      <div class="span6 aligncenter"> 
       <img src="https://www.mailgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/alert.png" width="450" alt="Email validation suggestions"> 
      </div> 
      <div class="span6"> 
       <h3>“Did you mean gmail.com?” and other suggestions</h3> 
       <p>People make typos all the time. Gmail.com can easily become gmal.com and when it does, you can miss out on connecting with that customer.</p> 
       <p>That’s why we built in a suggestion service into our email validation service. When we have a suggestion for a common typos, we’ll return it along with invalid status, so you can offer the suggestion to your user.</p> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
    </div> 
   </section> 
   <section class="feature"> 
    <div class="container"> 
     <div class="group"> 
      <div class="span6"> 
       <h3>Avoid high risk addresses</h3> 
       <p>Our email validation API is your first line of defense against high-risk recipients. When validating an email address, we check for role-based addresses or disposable email domains.</p> 
      </div> 
      <div class="span6 aligncenter"> 
       <img src="https://www.mailgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/1-wall.png" width="450" alt="Email validation suggestions"> 
      </div>
