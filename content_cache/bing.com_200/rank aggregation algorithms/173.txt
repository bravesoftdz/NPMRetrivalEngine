             <td class="empty" colspan="2"> <b>Course contents (annotation):</b><br> 
              <blockquote>
               The course presents an introduction to selected numerical methods in Continuum Mechanics (finite difference method, boundary element method) and, in
               <br> particular, a more detailed discourse of the Finite Element Method. The relation to Ritz method is explained, algorithm of the FEM is presented together with
               <br> the basic theory and terminology (discretisation of continuum, types of elements, shape functions, element and global matrices of stiffness, pre- and
               <br> post-processing). Application of the FEM in different areas of engineering analysis is presented in theory and practice: static linear elasticity, dynamics
               <br> (modal analysis and transient problem), thermal analysis. In the practical part students will learn how to create an appropriate computational model and
               <br> realise the FE analysis using commercial software.
              </blockquote> </td>
