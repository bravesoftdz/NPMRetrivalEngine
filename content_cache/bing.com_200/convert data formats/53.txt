     <h2 class="title">Date Formats</h2> 
     <p class="para"> This page describes the different date formats that the <span class="function"><a href="function.strtotime.php" class="function">strtotime()</a></span>, <a href="class.datetime.php" class="classname">DateTime</a> and <span class="function"><a href="function.date-create.php" class="function">date_create()</a></span> parser understands. </p> 
     <table class="doctable table"> 
      <caption>
       <strong>Used Symbols</strong>
      </caption> 
      <thead> 
       <tr> 
        <th>Description</th> 
        <th>Format</th> 
        <th>Examples</th> 
       </tr> 
      </thead> 
      <tbody class="tbody"> 
       <tr> 
        <td><em>daysuf</em></td> 
        <td>"st" | "nd" | "rd" | "th"</td> 
        <td class="empty">&nbsp;</td> 
       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td><em>dd</em></td> 
        <td>([0-2]?[0-9] | "3"[01]) <em>daysuf</em>?</td> 
        <td>"7th", "22nd", "31"</td> 
       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td><em>DD</em></td> 
        <td>"0" [0-9] | [1-2][0-9] | "3" [01]</td> 
        <td>"07", "31"</td> 
       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td><em>m</em></td> 
        <td>'january' | 'february' | 'march' | 'april' | 'may' | 'june' | 'july' | 'august' | 'september' | 'october' | 'november' | 'december' | 'jan' | 'feb' | 'mar' | 'apr' | 'may' | 'jun' | 'jul' | 'aug' | 'sep' | 'sept' | 'oct' | 'nov' | 'dec' | "I" | "II" | "III" | "IV" | "V" | "VI" | "VII" | "VIII" | "IX" | "X" | "XI" | "XII"</td> 
        <td class="empty">&nbsp;</td> 
       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td><em>M</em></td> 
        <td>'jan' | 'feb' | 'mar' | 'apr' | 'may' | 'jun' | 'jul' | 'aug' | 'sep' | 'sept' | 'oct' | 'nov' | 'dec'</td> 
        <td class="empty">&nbsp;</td> 
       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td><em>mm</em></td> 
        <td>"0"? [0-9] | "1"[0-2]</td> 
        <td>"0", "04", "7", "12"</td> 
       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td><em>MM</em></td> 
        <td>"0" [0-9] | "1"[0-2]</td> 
        <td>"00", "04", "07", "12"</td> 
       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td><em>y</em></td> 
        <td>[0-9]{1,4}</td> 
        <td>"00", "78", "08", "8", "2008"</td> 
       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td><em>yy</em></td> 
        <td>[0-9]{2}</td> 
        <td>"00", "08", "78"</td> 
       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td><em>YY</em></td> 
        <td>[0-9]{4}</td> 
        <td>"2000", "2008", "1978"</td> 
       </tr> 
      </tbody> 
     </table>

       <tr>

       <tr>

       </tr>

       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td>Textual month and four digit year (Day reset to 1)</td> 
        <td><em>m</em> ([ \t.-])* <em>YY</em></td> 
        <td>"June 2008", "DEC1978", "March 1879"</td> 
       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td>Four digit year and textual month (Day reset to 1)</td> 
        <td><em>YY</em> ([ \t.-])* <em>m</em></td> 
        <td>"2008 June", "1978-XII", "1879.MArCH"</td> 
       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td>Textual month, day and year</td> 
        <td><em>m</em> ([ .\t-])* <em>dd</em> [,.stndrh\t ]+ <em>y</em></td> 
        <td>"July 1st, 2008", "April 17, 1790", "May.9,78"</td> 
       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td>Textual month and day</td> 
        <td><em>m</em> ([ .\t-])* <em>dd</em> [,.stndrh\t ]*</td>

       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td>Day and textual month</td>

       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td>Month abbreviation, day and year</td> 
        <td><em>M</em> "-" <em>DD</em> "-" <em>y</em></td>

       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td>Year, month abbreviation and day</td> 
        <td><em>y</em> "-" <em>M</em> "-" <em>DD</em></td> 
        <td>"78-Dec-22", "1814-MAY-17"</td> 
       </tr> 
       <tr> 
        <td>Year (and just the year)</td> 
        <td><em>YY</em></td> 
        <td>"1978", "2008"</td>

     <blockquote class="note">
      <p><strong class="note">Note</strong>: </p>
      <p class="para"> For the <em>y</em> and <em>yy</em> formats, years below 100 are handled in a special way when the <em>y</em> or <em>yy</em> symbol is used. If the year falls in the range 0 (inclusive) to 69 (inclusive), 2000 is added. If the year falls in the range 70 (inclusive) to 99 (inclusive) then 1900 is added. This means that "00-01-01" is interpreted as "2000-01-01". </p> 
      <p></p>
     </blockquote> 
     <blockquote class="note">
      <p><strong class="note">Note</strong>: </p>
      <p class="para"> The "Day, month and two digit year, with dots or tabs" format (<em>dd</em> [.\t] <em>mm</em> "." <em>yy</em>) only works for the year values 61 (inclusive) to 99 (inclusive) - outside those years the <em class="emphasis">time format</em> "<em>HH</em> [.:] <em>MM</em> [.:] <em>SS</em>" has precedence. </p> 
      <p></p>
     </blockquote> 
     <blockquote class="note">
      <p><strong class="note">Note</strong>: </p>
      <p class="para"> The "Year (and just the year)" format only works if a time string has already been found -- otherwise this format is recognised as <em>HH</em> <em>MM</em>. </p> 
      <p></p>
     </blockquote> 
     <blockquote class="note">
      <p><strong class="note">Note</strong>: </p>
      <p class="para"> It is possible to over- and underflow the <em>dd</em> and <em>DD</em> format. Day 0 means the last day of previous month, whereas overflows count into the next month. This makes "2008-08-00" equivalent to "2008-07-31" and "2008-06-31" equivalent to "2008-07-01" (June only has 30 days). </p> 
      <p class="para"> Note that as of PHP 5.1.0 the day range is restricted to 0-31 as indicated by the regular expression above. Thus "2008-06-32" is not a valid date string, for instance. </p> 
      <p class="para"> It is also possible to underflow the <em>mm</em> and <em>MM</em> formats with the value 0. A month value of 0 means December of the previous year. As example "2008-00-22" is equivalent to "2007-12-22". </p> 
      <p class="para"> If you combine the previous two facts and underflow both the day and the month, the following happens: "2008-00-00" first gets converted to "2007-12-00" which then gets converted to "2007-11-30". This also happens with the string "0000-00-00", which gets transformed into "-0001-11-30" (the year -1 in the ISO 8601 calendar, which is 2 BC in the proleptic Gregorian calendar). </p> 
      <p></p>
     </blockquote>
