        <p class="author"><a href="./viewtopic.php?p=234849&amp;sid=49a46dce9787b2725d076d3c6469bcca#p234849"><span class="imageset icon_post_target" title="Post">Post</span></a><span class="responsive-hide">by <strong><a href="./memberlist.php?mode=viewprofile&amp;u=76761&amp;sid=49a46dce9787b2725d076d3c6469bcca" class="username">jak8</a></strong> » </span>Mon Dec 24, 2012 9:15 pm </p> 
        <div class="content">
         I am not sure which Sony models you guys are using but my recent experience with a blu ray S570 may be of interest and assistance. I bought a used DS211 a couple of days ago and have just started to use it. This is my first NAS and thus it is a bit of a learning curve for me. I have a wireless network comprising a 4 year old desk station, lap top, squeezebox Touch, sony bravia TV and the S570 player which is wireless and I use to feed into the TV. 
         <br>
         <br>My primary intention is to use the NAS for backing up and feeding the Touch which I managed to set up yesterday and it works fine. Having completed this I turned my attention to running the NAS as a wider based media server trying to feed into the S570 on DNLA. Although I could get a connection I repeatedly got a flag on the TV saying there was no readable files on the system. I initially thought this was due to the videos being in the wrong format for either the NAS or the S570 and I spent the next day trying various conversions with no success. I eventually solved the problem which I suspect was due a mix of bad filing and lack of media indexing. Once this was resolved I was pleased to discover all of the uploaded videos, which are in a range of different formats were recognised. Some such as the ISO's didn't play and I had not expected them to but contrary to what Sony advise the AVI's play perfectly well over DNLA as do the VOB files and DivX. This didn't particularly surprise me as I have at times used window media player to watch some of the PC loaded videos rather than burn them. What perplexes me is why Sony seem to provide spurious information on their products. I would therefore recommend you try using Synology's media server with the Sony before resorting to converting any videos. If you do need to resort to conversion then MediaCoder a freeware program should assist. You can change frame rates in this program but I found it difficult to download - keeps re-directing to pay progs.. Best to try their forum for direct route. In addition this page has a wealth of data on conversions etc.
