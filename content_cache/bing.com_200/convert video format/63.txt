              <div class="float-left" style="margin:3px 0 0 20px"> 
               <a id="ctl00_ctl00_MC_AMC_Answers_ctl01_A_Accept" title="Mark this solution as an accepted solution." class="button safe" style="display:none;">Accept&nbsp;Solution</a>

             <div class="text">
              Just to add a clarification to a good answer by Simon: AVI and MPEG-4 are the entities of different orders of hierarchy. AVI is a file extension and a format for the media 
              <i>container</i>; but MPEG-4 is more essential thing: this is a method of 
              <i>compression</i> of audio and video. In principle, AVI container can contain different kinds of video+audio streams including MPEG4.
              <br> 
              <br> See for more detail:
              <br> 
              <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Audio_Video_Interleave">http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Audio_Video_Interleave</a>[
              <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Audio_Video_Interleave" target="_blank" title="New Window">^</a>],
              <br> 
              <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MPEG-4">http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MPEG-4</a>[
              <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MPEG-4" target="_blank" title="New Window">^</a>].
              <br> 
              <br> Also, look at the links at the bottom of the first article to see how many different MPEG-4 standards are there. Which one do you need? :-)
              <br>
