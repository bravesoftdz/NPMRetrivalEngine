               <span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot;;">String.format() </span>

Both take minimum two parameters, first of them is <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">formatting instruction</b> and other was actual </span>
               <span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot;;">String</span>
               <span style="font-family: &quot;arial&quot;;"> or anything which needs to be formatted. Java formatting instructions are both powerful and flexible and allow you to generate formatted </span>
               <span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot;;">String</span>
               <span style="font-family: &quot;arial&quot;;"> on many different formats. It's worth to understand the format of "formatting instruction" to take full benefit of </span>
               <span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot;;">String.format() </span>
