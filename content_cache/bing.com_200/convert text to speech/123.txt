There are voice templates to change voice from male to female, but the problem in them all is that the system read each word separately, instead of starting to read a sentence.</p> 
              <p>Is there any advanced program or trick to read a text with a relatively natural voice?</p>

            <td class="answercell"> 
             <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
              <p>I think you mean Text-To-Speech (TTS) since you are talking about a robotic voice. TTS engines take written text and voice it back, whereas speech recognition engines understand human speech and convert it into a machine readable format. <a href="http://www.nuance.com/for-business/customer-service-solutions/loquendo-small-business-bundle/index.htm" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Loquendo</a> has the most natural sounding TTS engine I have heard. They have a version for Linux. They have an <a href="http://www.nuance.com/for-business/by-solution/customer-service-solutions/solutions-services/inbound-solutions/loquendo-small-business-bundle/interactive-tts-demo/index.htm" rel="nofollow noreferrer">interactive demo</a> you can play with to hear how great it sounds. There TTS engine can take special characters in the text to do things like provide emphasis on phrases or even make the voice laugh or cry. Not many TTS engines do this.</p>

            <td class="answercell"> 
             <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
              <p>After weeks of researching the same question I found the voices from <em>Ivona</em> (<a href="http://www.ivona.com/en" rel="nofollow noreferrer">here</a>) and <em>Loquendo</em> (<a href="http://www.loquendo.com/en/demo-center/tts-demo/english" rel="nofollow noreferrer">here</a> and <a href="http://www.loquendo.com/en/demo-center/interactive-tts-demo" rel="nofollow noreferrer">here</a>) to be the best TTS voices available for Windows. Only Ivona lists prices on their website though. To actually use the TTS voices on your Windows PC I recommend <a href="http://www.cross-plus-a.com/balabolka.htm" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><em>Balabolka</em></a> (free), <a href="http://www.ivona.com/en/mini-reader/" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><em>Ivona MiniReader</em></a> (free) or <a href="http://www.ivona.com/en/reader/" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><em>Ivona Reader</em></a>.</p> 
              <p>Next check out <a href="https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/clearly/iooicodkiihhpojmmeghjclgihfjdjhj?hl=en-US" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><em>Clearly</em></a> from Evernote. It is a browser extension currently only available for Google Chrome. It provides TTS for <a href="http://evernote.com/premium" rel="nofollow noreferrer">premium</a> customers only. The (iSpeech powered) TTS voice is not as good as Ivona's or Loquendo's but it gives you a nice reader view on blog posts/articles by stripping away distracting page elements. You can also set it to auto-highlight the currently read word! </p> 
              <p>Check out <a href="http://www.readspeaker.com" rel="nofollow noreferrer">ReadSpeaker</a> too which can be implemented by website owners into their site. Readspeaker provides auto-highlighting of the currently read paragraph/word, auto-scrolling and the ability to change the TTS speed.</p> 
              <p>[Edit:] There are some free useful Google Chrome extensions that are powered by iSpeech as well.</p>

            <td class="answercell"> 
             <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
              <p>When it comes to having a good sound, what you really want to look for is not the text to speech software, but the good voices: they are a separate subject.</p> 
              <p>The same voice will work accross different software. The best one I know of at the moment is named Audrey. A female voice with a british accent. Although I have to change most ' that are on the text with copy-past to one from the keyboard, it is worth it for an almost realistic sounding voice. I also have to place a pausing symbol (.,: or such) at the end of titles so she doesn't keep talking right through them. I still think it's the best voice I have so far. But I wouldn't mind finding the one from that weather channel that was mentioned ealier:</p> 
              <p>"NOAA weather radio broadcasts are EXCELLENT. I would not object to listening to that program read a long text.– Jeanne Pindar"</p> 
              <p>Here is a website which has sample voices on it: including Audrey. I have others, but I have only used her for a long time now.</p> 
              <p>PS: you have to pay for the better voices, but they will work on free text-to-speech software afterwards... at least the one I have. (Free Natural Reader)</p> 
              <p>Voice Sample web page: <a href="http://www.digitalfuturesoft.com/attnaturalvoices.php" rel="nofollow noreferrer">http://www.digitalfuturesoft.com/attnaturalvoices.php</a></p>
