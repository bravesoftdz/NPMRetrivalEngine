                    <div id="SLiFlT"></div>
                    <span class="rendered_qtext"><p class="qtext_para">Cells in Excel cannot contain images.&nbsp; Images are on a different layer and reside above the cells (hiding but not replacing the contents).&nbsp; You can use VBS to walk through the list of images, figure out which cell they are over, delete the image, put something in that cell.&nbsp; Here's a snippet of VBS that replaces all images in the sheet with the word "Hello".&nbsp; Note that this will replace *any* image.&nbsp; You could look at the size of the image or other attributes to decide which ones to replace.</p><pre class="prettyprint linenums">Sub ReplacePictures()
    For Each wShape In ActiveSheet.Shapes
        If (wShape.Type = 13) Then
            wShape.TopLeftCell = "hello"
            wShape.Delete
        End If
    Next wShape
End Sub
</pre></span>

                    <div id="XJCmKC"></div>
                    <span class="rendered_qtext"><p class="qtext_para">By hand.</p><p class="qtext_para">A basic rule when dealing with data is Keep your data separate. Its a pain when all the data is in text and in one cell. For example, a persons name [First M. Last]. Now you cant sort by last name or worse data and be entered in different formats [Last, First M.]. Keeping your data separate you get [First][M.][Last]. Putting the name back together is easy with a formula. YOUR problem is much harder. All the data is together in an image. It is not impossible to get data out, but youll need programming skills that go far beyond the scope of excel or find a specialized tool.</p><p class="qtext_para">If this is a one off thing then get to typing. If its an ongoing thing or a lot of data then you need to get the data another way; before its converted to image format.</p></span>
