   <h1><a name="_Overview:"></a><span style="font-size:16.0pt;font-family:&quot;Trebuchet MS&quot;;
mso-fareast-font-family:Batang">Overview:
     <o:p></o:p></span></h1> 
   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-right:137.25pt"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style="font-family:&quot;Trebuchet MS&quot;;mso-fareast-font-family:Batang;
mso-bidi-font-family:Arial">
      <o:p>
       &nbsp;
      </o:p></span></b></p> 
   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top:0in;margin-right:137.25pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Trebuchet MS&quot;;
mso-fareast-font-family:Batang;mso-bidi-font-family:Arial">The goal of computer vision is to develop the algorithms and representations that will enable a machine to autonomously analyze visual information. <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp;</span></b>As such, <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal">object recognition</b> is a fundamental vision problem: put simply, what’s in the picture, and where?<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp; </span>Recognition remains challenging in large part due to the significant variations exhibited by real-world images.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp; </span>Partial occlusions, viewpoint changes, varying illumination, cluttered backgrounds, and intra-category appearance variations all make it necessary to develop exceedingly robust models of categories.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp; </span>
     <o:p></o:p></span></p> 
   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top:0in;margin-right:137.25pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Trebuchet MS&quot;;
mso-fareast-font-family:Batang;mso-bidi-font-family:Arial">
     <o:p>
      &nbsp;
     </o:p></span></p> 
   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top:0in;margin-right:137.25pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Trebuchet MS&quot;;
mso-fareast-font-family:Batang;mso-bidi-font-family:Arial">In this course we will survey and discuss current computer vision literature on object and category recognition.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp; </span>The goals of the course will be to understand current approaches to some important problems in visual recognition, to actively analyze their strengths and weaknesses, and to begin to identify interesting open questions and possible directions for future research.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp; </span>Topics will include part-based models for recognition, invariant local features, bags of features, local spatial constraints, shape descriptors and matching, learning similarity measures, fast indexing methods, recognition with text and images, the role of context in recognition, and unsupervised category discovery.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp; </span>
     <o:p></o:p></span></p> 
   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top:0in;margin-right:137.25pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Trebuchet MS&quot;;

mso-fareast-font-family:Batang;mso-bidi-font-family:Arial">For each given sub-topic, our discussion and class presentations will center around a few selected relevant research papers.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp; </span>Our study of state-of-the-art topics in recognition will lead up to research-oriented final course projects.
