           <b><font color="000000">Maintenance Note</font></b>
          </center><br> <font color="#444444"> The PNG-supporting applications and toolkits pages are no longer being actively updated; for several years already, it's been safe to assume that virtually any bitmap-capable image app supports PNG. <a href="http://gregroelofs.com/greg_contact.html">Corrections are still welcome</a>, particularly for "repurposed domains" (park-spam), but new applications are no longer being added. </font> </td> 
        </tr> 
       </tbody>
      </table> </td> 
    </tr> 
   </tbody>
  </table> 
  <p> Many of the image-conversion applications listed here are also image viewers and are therefore listed on the <a href="pngapvw.html">PNG-supporting image-viewer page</a> as well. Note also that <a href="pngaped.html">image editors</a> often have the ability to import and export many formats. Finally, not all of these applications convert both to and from the PNG format; the "read-only," "write-only" and "read/write" comments indicate the type of PNG support. </p>
  <p> A special note regarding "png to text" conversion: if you're looking for something to convert embedded, raster text to plain text, you won't find it here--you want OCR (optical character recognition) software instead. The kinds of png-to-text converters sprinkled below (e.g., <b>pngcheck</b> and <b>SNG</b>) tend to fall more along the lines of PNG "dumpers," i.e., programs that describe the internal structure of a PNG file textually. </p>
  <p> As on the other applications pages, links to home WWW sites or to downloadable versions are provided where known, but if a link is broken, check the location and see if an updated version is available (and <a href="http://gregroelofs.com/greg_contact.html">please tell Greg!</a>). Relevant operating systems are printed in (<i>parenthesized italics</i>), where "many" is defined as at least three of Unix, OS/2, Win32, DOS, Mac OS, etc. </p>
  <p> </p>
