            <th style="padding:2px;"> <h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" style="margin:3px; background:#e9caef; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #a3bfb1; text-align:left; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Bitcoin">Bitcoin</span></h2> </th>
           </tr> 
           <tr> 
            <td style="color:#000;"> 
             <div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:2px 5px">
              <div class="floatleft">
               <a href="/wiki/File:BC_Logo_.png" class="image" title="Bitcoin usage worldwide."><img alt="Bitcoin usage worldwide." src="/w/images/en/thumb/2/29/BC_Logo_.png/64px-BC_Logo_.png" width="64" height="64" srcset="/w/images/en/thumb/2/29/BC_Logo_.png/96px-BC_Logo_.png 1.5x, /w/images/en/thumb/2/29/BC_Logo_.png/128px-BC_Logo_.png 2x"></a>
              </div> 
              <p><b><a href="/wiki/Bitcoin" title="Bitcoin">Bitcoin</a></b> is a decentralized <a href="/wiki/Digital_currency" title="Digital currency">digital currency</a> that enables instant payments to anyone, anywhere in the world. Bitcoin uses peer-to-peer technology to operate with no central authority: <a href="/wiki/Transaction" title="Transaction">transaction management</a> and <a href="/wiki/Controlled_supply" title="Controlled supply">money issuance</a> are carried out collectively by the network. </p>
              <p>The original Bitcoin software by <a href="/wiki/Satoshi_Nakamoto" title="Satoshi Nakamoto">Satoshi Nakamoto</a> was released under the MIT license. Most client software, derived or "from scratch", also use open source licensing. </p>
              <p>Bitcoin is the first successful implementation of a <i>distributed crypto-currency</i>, described in part in 1998 by <a href="/wiki/Wei_Dai" title="Wei Dai">Wei Dai</a> on the cypherpunks mailing list. Building upon the notion that money is any object, or any sort of record, accepted as payment for goods and services and repayment of debts in a given country or socio-economic context, Bitcoin is designed around the idea of using cryptography to control the creation and transfer of money, rather than relying on central authorities. </p> 
             </div> </td>
           </tr> 
           <tr> 
            <th style="padding:2px"> <h2 id="mp-dyk-h2" style="margin:3px; background:#e9caef; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #a3bfb1; text-align:left; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Why.3F">Why?</span></h2> </th>
           </tr> 
           <tr> 
            <td style="color:#000;padding:2px 5px 5px"> 
             <div id="mp-dyk"> 
              <ul>
               <li> Bitcoins are sent easily through the Internet, without needing to trust any third party.</li> 
               <li> <a href="/wiki/Transactions" title="Transactions" class="mw-redirect">Transactions</a>: 
                <ul>
                 <li> Are irreversible by design</li> 
                 <li> Are fast. Funds received are available for spending within minutes.</li> 
                 <li> Cost very little, especially compared to other payment networks.</li>
                </ul></li> 
               <li> The supply of bitcoins is regulated by software and the agreement of users of the system and cannot be manipulated by any government, bank, organization or individual. The limited inflation of the Bitcoin system's money supply is distributed evenly (by CPU power) to miners who help secure the network.</li>
              </ul> 
             </div> </td>

            <th style="padding:2px"> <h2 id="mp-otd-h2" style="margin:3px; background:#efc1e2; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #a3b0bf; text-align:left; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em;"><span class="mw-headline" id="FAQ">FAQ</span></h2> </th>
           </tr> 
           <tr> 
            <td style="color:#000;padding:2px 5px 5px"> 
             <div id="mp-otd">
              <b>Q. What is Bitcoin?</b> 
              <p>A. Bitcoin is a peer-to-peer currency. Peer-to-peer means that no central authority issues new money or tracks transactions. These tasks are managed collectively by the <a href="/wiki/Network" title="Network">network</a>. </p>
              <p><b>Q. How does Bitcoin work?</b> </p>
              <p>A. Bitcoin uses <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public-key_cryptography" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Public-key cryptography">public-key cryptography</a>, peer-to-peer networking, and <a href="/wiki/Proof_of_work" title="Proof of work"> proof-of-work</a> to process and verify payments. Bitcoins are sent (or signed over) from one address to another with each user potentially having many, many addresses. Each payment transaction is broadcast to the network and included in the blockchain so that the included bitcoins cannot be spent twice. After an hour or two, each transaction is locked in time by the massive amount of processing power that continues to extend the blockchain. Using these techniques, Bitcoin provides a fast and extremely reliable payment network that anyone can use. </p>
