               <h2>Introduction on Bitcoin</h2> 
               <p>Codeproject fellows, I am glad to release the first article of a serie. [NEW : <a href="http://www.codeproject.com/Articles/775226/NBitcoin-Cryptography-Part">Part 2 Here</a>]<br>I recently <a href="https://github.com/NicolasDorier/NBitcoin">ported a good part</a> of <a href="https://github.com/bitcoin/bitcoin">bitcoin source code</a> from C++ to C#. I imported almost all unit tests. NBitcoin have approximately 70 tests you can use to play and discover.<br>This is a great learning experience that I will share with you. But Bitcoin all the technical aspects of bitcoin are too long to explain in only one article.<br>I will start this intro by looking Bitcoin from different perspectives, talking what led to its birth, and then showing you the code. For the first part I will cover only the offline aspect of bitcoin. In other words, I will omit all protocol details, and talk about the high level cryptographic part.</p> 
               <h3>The Entrepreneur Perspective</h3> 
               <p>Bitcoin grabbed my attention when I got fed up with Paypal happily blocking my fund as they pleased, along with the bureaucratic burden, fees, lack of transparence, and lack of privacy of our banking system to make any monetary transfer.</p> 
               <p>As a <a href="http://iaas.ao-is.com/">startup</a>, integrating a payment provider to the website was a huge burden and cost center. <br>I tried a lot, before using paypal that is now happily taking 5% on each transaction, and reserve the right to block my money when it pleases them. They have lawyers, so they are always right.</p> 
               <p>Why is it so painful ? Why can’t I transfer my money easily without any intermediary ? Just me and the customer without the middle man. Bitcoin makes it possible.<br>This is my view as an entrepreneur. </p> 
               <h3>The Economist Perspective</h3> 
               <p>For the <strong>Keynes</strong> economist, Bitcoin is a money whose central banking (FED or European central bank) can’t control the supply, their poor lizard brains activate and remind them the scares of the Great Depression.<br>By then, transforming dollar into a fiat currency (not backed by gold, but controlled by central banking) was seen as the smartest decision to get out of crisis.<br>From this day, the FED (federal reserve), the bank of the banks, the bank of least resort changed. Before the great depression, the FED was a stock of gold for banks. <br>After, the FED became a large money printer.</p> 
               <p>Abolishing gold standard seemed a good idea, until <a href="http://www.fee.org/the_freeman/detail/the-great-depression-according-to-milton-friedman"><strong>Milton Friedman</strong></a>, properly explained what led to the Great Depression, leading Ben Bernanke to admit in 2002, then a Federal Reserve governor, that the FED was the culprit of the Great Depression. In other words, the problem was not with the dollar being backed by gold, but by the creation of the FED that led banks to take risks by believing they will be rescued. This Great Depression, caused by the FED, ironically caused the FED to be more powerfull, since it became free of the burden of gold. (<a href="http://www.fee.org/the_freeman/detail/the-great-depression-according-to-milton-friedman">Long story here</a>)</p> 
               <p>For Milton Friedman, the creation of the fed, “the bank of the banks” is an error, which lead the big banks to take risks because they know they will be bailout in any case.<br>Our school does not teach this story though.</p> 
               <p>The economist perspective is important.                <h2>Talk is cheap, show me the code</h2> 
               <h3>Getting started</h3> 
               <p>First of all, the code is on <a href="https://github.com/NicolasDorier/NBitcoin">GitHub</a>.<br>Secondly, the binaries are on <a href="https://www.nuget.org/packages/NBitcoin/">Nuget</a>.<br>So to get started, new console project, then add a reference on the nuget package <strong>NBitcoin</strong>.</p> 
               <p><img border="0" src="/KB/cs/768412/image_thumb_1_.png" alt="image" title="image"></p> 
               <p>For the dependencies, <strong>BouncyCastle</strong> is used for the crypto part, bitcoin use <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elliptic_Curve_DSA">ECDSA</a> asymmetric keys under the hood and I don’t want to implement that myself. In the C++ code, OpenSSL is used instead.</p> 
               <p><strong>Mono.NAT</strong> and <strong>SQLite</strong> are used only if you intend to create your own Bitcoin Node (next article). Mono.NAT will use UPNP to open bitcoin ports on your gateway to run your node. <br>SQLite is the embedded database that your node server will use to store transactions, blocks and peers. It works in both x86 and x64. You can remove those if you don’t intend to talk with Bitcoin network.</p> 
               <h3>Bitcoin addresses</h3> 
               <p>As I said, if you want to send me some bitcoins, send it to <strong>15sYbVpRh6dyWycZMwPdxJWD4xbfxReeHe </strong>.<br>But, as a developer what represent this address ? The formal spec is <a href="https://en.bitcoin.it/wiki/Address">here</a>, but let’s explore it with NBitcoin instead.<br>This string is a base58 string encoded byte array. <br>Let’s see that address under its hexadecimal representation.</p> 
               <pre lang="cs"><span class="code-keyword">static</span> <span class="code-keyword">void</span> Main(string[] args)
{
    byte[] byteArray = Encoders.Base58.DecodeData(<span class="code-string">"</span><span class="code-string">15sYbVpRh6dyWycZMwPdxJWD4xbfxReeHe"</span>);
    <span class="code-keyword">string</span> hex = Encoders.Hex.EncodeData(byteArray);
    Console.WriteLine(hex);
}</pre>
               <p>Which gives us :<br><strong>00356facdac5f5bcae995d13e667bb5864fd1e7d59fb69d021<br>00</strong> : Prefix that identify the type of the bitcoin data structure, in this case it is a public key hash (see later)<br><strong>356facdac5f5bcae995d13e667bb5864fd1e7d59</strong> : actual public key hash (20 bytes)<br><strong>fb69d021</strong> : checksum of all the previous data (detect mistyping)</p> 
               <p>In bitcoin, an address belongs to a network, and there is two networks : <strong>Main</strong> and <strong>Test</strong>. You can get bitcoin on the test network for free for your tests.<br>However, each network have a different prefix to identify a public key hash.<br>On the Main Network <strong>00 </strong>is the prefix for the public key hash. But for Test Network it is <strong>6f</strong>.<br>This is done so you can’t send money to an address that belong to another network.</p> 
               <p>You can use the <strong>BitcoinAddress</strong> type to do the conversion for you in several way.</p>
               <pre lang="cs"><span class="code-keyword">static</span> <span class="code-keyword">void</span> Main(string[] args)
{
    BitcoinAddress address = (BitcoinAddress)Network.CreateFromBase58Data(<span class="code-string">"</span><span class="code-string">15sYbVpRh6dyWycZMwPdxJWD4xbfxReeHe"</span>);
    <span class="code-comment">//</span><span class="code-comment">BitcoinAddress address = Network.Main.CreateBitcoinAddress("15sYbVpRh6dyWycZMwPdxJWD4xbfxReeHe");</span>
    <span class="code-comment">//</span><span class="code-comment">BitcoinAddress address = new BitcoinAddress("15sYbVpRh6dyWycZMwPdxJWD4xbfxReeHe", Network.Main);</span>
    Console.WriteLine(address.ID);
    <span class="code-comment">//</span><span class="code-comment">Print 356facdac5f5bcae995d13e667bb5864fd1e7d59</span>
}</pre>
               <p>But where did I got this “<strong>public key hash</strong>” ?<br>When someone send you money in bitcoin, they broadcast a <strong>Transaction</strong> to the network.<br>All transactions contain one or more <strong>TxIn</strong> (Transaction input) and one or more <strong>TxOut. </strong>(Transaction output)<br>Any <strong>TxOut</strong> that contains your <strong>public key hash</strong> is spendable by you.</p> 
               <p>When, in your turn, you will pay someone, you send a new <strong>Transaction </strong>to the network, but this time you will include a <strong>TxIn </strong>with a reference to the <strong>TxOut </strong>you want to spend. (We call such reference an <strong>OutPoint</strong>)<br>However, you will sign the transaction with the <strong>private key </strong>associated to the <strong>public key hash </strong>present in the <strong>TxOut</strong> you want to spend. Thus proving to the network your ownership.</p> 
               <p>Here is the process of creating a new keypair.</p>
               <pre lang="cs"><span class="code-keyword">static</span> <span class="code-keyword">void</span> Main(string[] args)
{
    Key privateKey = <span class="code-keyword">new</span> Key(); <span class="code-comment">//</span><span class="code-comment">Create private key</span>
    BitcoinAddress address = privateKey.PubKey.GetAddress(Network.Main); <span class="code-comment">//</span><span class="code-comment">Get the public key, and derive the address on the Main network</span>
    Console.WriteLine(address); <span class="code-comment">//</span><span class="code-comment">printed in my case 15sYbVpRh6dyWycZMwPdxJWD4xbfxReeHe</span>
}</pre>
               <br>Seemingly, if you want to store the private key,
               <br>
               <pre lang="cs">Key privateKey = <span class="code-keyword">new</span> Key(); <span class="code-comment">//</span><span class="code-comment">Create private key</span>
BitcoinSecret secret = privateKey.GetBitcoinSecret(Network.Main);
Console.Write(secret); <span class="code-comment">//</span><span class="code-comment">Print base58 string</span></pre>
               <p>Here is a small diagram of all classes, you can see that a <strong>BitcoinAddress</strong> is nothing but a <strong>public key hash</strong>, and does not allow you to deduce the <strong>PubKey</strong>.</p> 
               <p><img border="0" src="/KB/cs/768412/image_thumb_12_.png" alt="image" title="image"></p> 
               <h3>Protecting privacy</h3> 
               <p>If every transaction is broadcasted to the network, it means that anyone should be able to track how much I receive, which raise some privacy concern. This is true, you can see <a href="https://blockchain.info/address/15sYbVpRh6dyWycZMwPdxJWD4xbfxReeHe">that someone sent me 0.0026 BTC</a>.</p> 
               <p>You can create as much private key you want. If you want to keep private key, you can break your receive among several addresses. (A set of private key is called a <strong>wallet</strong>)<br>If you are a business and need to automate your payments you have another alternative :<br>Create one different bitcoin address per business transaction.</p> 
               <p>However, there is two problems with that :</p> 
               <ul> 
                <li>You need to maintain a mapping between your business transaction and the private key you used, </li>
                <li>If such database is compromised or private key stolen, you loose money.</li>
               </ul> 
               <p>The solution is called <a href="https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/blob/master/bip-0032.mediawiki">Hierarchical Deterministic Wallet</a>.<br>Thanks to that, you can give your payment server the right to <strong>generate public keys, without giving the private key</strong>. If the payment database is stolen or compromised, you loose nothing.</p> 
               <p>In NBitcoin, this feature is implemented with two classes : <strong>ExtKey </strong>and <strong>ExtPubKey.</strong> <br>ExtKey will generate a <strong>Key</strong> for a corresponding ID,<br>ExtPubKey will generate a <strong>PubKey</strong> for a corresponding ID.</p> 
               <p><img border="0" src="/KB/cs/768412/image_thumb_6_.png" alt="image" title="image"></p> 
               <pre lang="cs">ExtKey privateKey = <span class="code-keyword">new</span> ExtKey();
ExtPubKey pubKey = privateKey.Neuter();
<span class="code-comment">//</span><span class="code-comment">Now, give the pubkey to your payment server</span>
<span class="code-comment">//</span><span class="code-comment">....</span>
<span class="code-comment">//</span><span class="code-comment">The payment server receive an order, note the server does not need the private key to generate the address</span>
<span class="code-keyword">uint</span> orderID = <span class="code-digit">1001</span>;
BitcoinAddress address = pubKey.Derive(orderID).PubKey.GetAddress(Network.Main);
Console.WriteLine(address);

<span class="code-comment">//</span><span class="code-comment">Now on the server that have access to the private key, you get the private key from the orderID</span>
Key key = privateKey.Derive(orderID).Key;
BitcoinSecret secret = key.GetBitcoinSecret(Network.Main);
Console.WriteLine(secret); <span class="code-comment">//</span><span class="code-comment">Print a nice secret key string</span></pre>
               <p>In this example we only use an orderId to generate the Key/Pubkey, but in reality you can use more data, this is why we that a <strong>Hierarchical Wallet.</strong></p> 
               <p>For example</p> 
               <pre lang="cs">pubKey.Derive(departmentID).Derive(orderID).PubKey</pre>
               <p>This way, you can generate a dedicated private key to each department. (And also spend their funds if you have the master <strong>ExtKey</strong>)</p> 
               <h3>Using bitcoin key pairs for identity verification</h3> 
               <p>How can I prove you, dear reader, that I am indeed, the owner of the private key of <strong>15sYbVpRh6dyWycZMwPdxJWD4xbfxReeHe </strong>?<br>Here is the proof : <br>Message : “I am Nicolas Dorier, owner of this address 15sYbVpRh6dyWycZMwPdxJWD4xbfxReeHe”.<br>Signature : “IGkC4NKGXOXJ6CNT8T6Dx0egqaiSb8rAlBdsmanStOhbVfILmY+3p88Z/Fhb/jSkUhHFhsbcxFZydoPrh/2LNY0=”</p> 
               <p>How did I generate this signature ? </p>
               <pre lang="cs">BitcoinSecret secret = <span class="code-keyword">new</span> BitcoinSecret(<span class="code-string">"</span><span class="code-string">base58 private key"</span>, Network.Main);
<span class="code-keyword">string</span> signature = secret.Key.SignMessage(<span class="code-string">"</span><span class="code-string">I am Nicolas Dorier, owner of this address 15sYbVpRh6dyWycZMwPdxJWD4xbfxReeHe"</span>);
Console.WriteLine(signature);</pre>
               <p>On your side, how do you verify ?</p>
               <pre lang="cs"><span class="code-keyword">string</span> message = <span class="code-string">"</span><span class="code-string">I am Nicolas Dorier, owner of this address 15sYbVpRh6dyWycZMwPdxJWD4xbfxReeHe"</span>;
<span class="code-keyword">string</span> signature = <span class="code-string">"</span><span class="code-string">IGkC4NKGXOXJ6CNT8T6Dx0egqaiSb8rAlBdsmanStOhbVfILmY+3p88Z/Fhb/jSkUhHFhsbcxFZydoPrh/2LNY0="</span>;
BitcoinAddress address = <span class="code-keyword">new</span> BitcoinAddress(<span class="code-string">"</span><span class="code-string">15sYbVpRh6dyWycZMwPdxJWD4xbfxReeHe"</span>, Network.Main);
<span class="code-keyword">bool</span> verified = address.VerifyMessage(message, signature);
Console.WriteLine(verified);</pre>
               <h3>Under the hood</h3> 
               <p>To simplify my explanation, I made some shortcut to explain you what is a bitcoin address.<br>I said, a <strong>TxOut</strong> send money to a <strong>bitcoin address</strong>, and when you want to spend it, you add a <strong>TxIn</strong> in your transaction that reference the <strong>TxOut</strong>, and sign that with your private key. </p> 
               <p>But Bitcoin is more flexible than that.<br><strong>TxOut </strong>does not necessarily contains your address. <strong>TxOut</strong> contains a Script that we call the <strong>ScriptPubKey</strong>. This script is like an algorithm that say what you need to do to spend this <strong>TxOut</strong>.<br>Seemingly, <strong>TxIn</strong> have a <strong>Script </strong>called a <strong>ScriptSig</strong> this does what <strong>ScriptPubKey</strong> wants to spend the <strong>TxOut</strong>. These scripts are a sort of <a href="https://en.bitcoin.it/wiki/Script">stack language without any loop</a>.</p> 
               <p>When the <strong>SigPubKey</strong> is concatenated to the <strong>ScriptSig</strong>, the algorithm is executed, its result get push in the stack.</p> 
               <p><img border="0" src="/KB/cs/768412/image_thumb_3_.png" alt="image" title="image"></p> 
               <p>Until now, if you want to pay me, the <strong>ScriptPubKey</strong> will looks like that. (In readable form)</p>
               <pre lang="cs">BitcoinAddress to = <span class="code-keyword">new</span> BitcoinAddress(<span class="code-string">"</span><span class="code-string">15sYbVpRh6dyWycZMwPdxJWD4xbfxReeHe"</span>, Network.Main);
PayToPubkeyHashScriptTemplate template = <span class="code-keyword">new</span> PayToPubkeyHashScriptTemplate();
Script script = template.GenerateOutputScript(to);
Console.WriteLine(script);</pre>
               <p>OP_DUP OP_HASH160 <strong>356facdac5f5bcae995d13e667bb5864fd1e7d59(public key hash)</strong> OP_EQUALVERIFY OP_CHECKSIG</p> 
               <p>On the other hand, if I want to use these funds my script will be</p>
               <pre lang="cs">BitcoinSecret secret = <span class="code-keyword">new</span> BitcoinSecret(<span class="code-string">"</span><span class="code-string">base58 secret key"</span>, Network.Main);
byte[] transactionSignature = secret.Key.Sign(transactionHash);
PayToPubkeyHashScriptTemplate template = <span class="code-keyword">new</span> PayToPubkeyHashScriptTemplate();
Script scriptSig = template.GenerateInputScript(<span class="code-keyword">new</span> TransactionSignature(transactionSignature, SigHash.All), secret.Key.PubKey);
Console.WriteLine(scriptSig);</pre>
               <br>
               <br>The resulting script will push two values on the stack : the signature of the transaction and the public key.
               <br>&lt;signature of transaction&gt; &lt;public key&gt;
               <p>If we concatenate both scripts we get</p> 
               <p>&lt;signature of transaction&gt; &lt;public key&gt; OP_DUP OP_HASH160 &lt;public key hash&gt; OP_EQUALVERIFY OP_CHECKSIG</p> 
               <p>It means<br>Push &lt;signature of transaction&gt;<br>Push &lt;public key (1)&gt;<br>Push &lt;public key (2)&gt; again<br>Hash &lt;public key (2)&gt;, pop &lt;public key (2)&gt;, push &lt;public key hash (1)&gt;<br>Push &lt;public key hash (2)&gt;<br>Verify &lt;public key hash(1)&gt; equals to &lt;public key hash(2)&gt;, pop &lt;public key hash(1)&gt;, pop &lt;public key hash(2)&gt;<br>Check signature &lt;signature of transaction&gt; with Push &lt;public key (1)&gt;, pop &lt;signature of transaction&gt;, pop &lt;public key (1)&gt;, push true if verified or false</p> 
               <h3>Shared ownership</h3> 
               <p>Why the scripting system is such big of a deal ?<br>Because it allows new way of ownership, there is a special script that is called “M of N wallet”, when someone sends funds to a M of N wallet, it means the spender will need to provide N signature of M possible addresses.</p> 
               <p>This way, you can enforce the fact that 2 persons among 3 should agree to spend to enforce a consensus.<br>You can use a 1 person among 2 persons, if you want to share money with someone.<br>The difference between that and sharing a private key is nonrepudiation. When you use a “1 of 2 wallet”, you have the proof of the identity of the spender.<br>Such script can be generated as such</p>
               <pre lang="cs">PayToMultiSigScriptTemplate template = <span class="code-keyword">new</span> PayToMultiSigScriptTemplate();
Script scriptPubKey = template.GenerateOutputScript(<span class="code-digit">1</span>, <span class="code-keyword">new</span> PubKey[] { nicoPubKey, bobPubKey });</pre>
               <br>01 &lt;nico hash pub key&gt; &lt;bob hash pub key&gt; 02 OP_CHECKMULTISIG
