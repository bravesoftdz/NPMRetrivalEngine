      <h2>Quicksort / Insertion Sort&nbsp;Combo</h2> 
      <p><strong>My blog is now located at </strong><strong><a href="http://www.whoissethlong.com">http://www.WhoIsSethLong.com</a></strong></p> 
      <p>The Quicksort is one of the fastest sorting algorithms for sorting large lists of data.<span>&nbsp; </span>The Insertion sort is a fast sorting algorithms for sorting very small lists that are already somewhat sorted.<span>&nbsp; </span>We combine these two algorithms to come up with a very simple and effective method for sorting large lists.</p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal">We start our sort by using a Quicksort.<span>&nbsp; </span>The Quicksort on average runs in O(<em>n </em>log(<em>n</em>)) time but can be as slow as O(<em>n</em><sup>2</sup>) if we try to sort a list that is already sorted and use the left most element as our pivot.<span>&nbsp; </span>If you are unfamiliar with the quicksort here is a Wikipedia article describing the algorithm: <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quicksort">http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quicksort</a></p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal">As the Quicksort works, it breaks down our list into smaller and smaller lists.<span>&nbsp; </span>Once the lists become small enough that an Insertion sort becomes more efficient then the Quicksort we will switch to the Insertion sort to finish off the job.<span>&nbsp; </span>Think of it as grinding away with the Quicksort then polishing it off with the Insertion sort once most of the work has been done.</p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal">Here is a code sample of a combined Quicksort and Insertion sort algorithm in C#.</p>
