       <h2 class="entry-title"><a href="https://trinkerrstuff.wordpress.com/2014/03/14/qdap-1-3-1-release-demoing-dispersion-plots-sentiment-analysis-easy-hash-lookups-boolean-searches-and-more/" rel="bookmark">qdap 1.3.1 Release: Demoing Dispersion Plots, Sentiment Analysis, Easy Hash Lookups, Boolean Searches and&nbsp;More…</a></h2> 
       <div class="entry-meta"> 
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> 
        <a href="https://trinkerrstuff.wordpress.com/2014/03/14/qdap-1-3-1-release-demoing-dispersion-plots-sentiment-analysis-easy-hash-lookups-boolean-searches-and-more/" title="5:59 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">March 14, 2014</span></a> 
        <span class="by-author"><span class="sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="https://trinkerrstuff.wordpress.com/author/tylerrinker/" title="View all posts by tylerrinker" rel="author">tylerrinker</a></span> </span> 
       </div>
       <!-- .entry-meta --> 
       <div class="entry-content"> 
        <p>We’re very pleased to announce the release of <strong><span style="color:blue;">qdap</span></strong> 1.3.1</p> 
        <div class="alignright" style="margin:20px 30px 0;"> 
         <p><a href="http://trinker.github.io/qdap/" target="_blank"><img alt="logo" src="https://dl.dropbox.com/u/61803503/qdap_logo.png" width="225" height="179"></a></p> 
        </div> 
        <p>This is the latest installment of the <a href="http://cran.us.r-project.org/web/packages/qdap/index.html">qdap package available at CRAN</a>. Several important updates have occurred since the 1.1.0 release, most notable the addition of two vignettes and some generic view methods.</p> 
        <p>The new vignettes include:</p> 
        <ol> 
         <li><a href="http://cran.r-project.org/web/packages/qdap/vignettes/qdap_vignette.html">An Introduction to qdap</a></li> 
         <li><a href="http://cran.r-project.org/web/packages/qdap/vignettes/tm_package_compatibility.pdf">qdap-tm Package Compatibility</a></li> 
        </ol> 
        <p>The former is a detailed HTML based guide over viewing the intended use of qdap functions. &nbsp;The second vignette is an explanation of how to move between <strong>qdap</strong> and <a href="http://cran.r-project.org/web/packages/tm/index.html"><strong>tm</strong></a> package forms as qdap moves to be more compatible with this seminal R text mining package.</p> 
        <p>To install use:</p> 
        <p style="text-align:center;"><strong><span style="font-size:large;color:#008000;">install.packages(“qdap”)</span></strong></p> 
        <p><span style="font-size:x-large;"><strong>Some of the <a href="https://github.com/trinker/qdap/blob/master/NEWS.md">changes</a> in versions 1.2.0-1.3.1 include:</strong></span></p> 
        <hr> 
        <p style="text-align:left;"><strong>Generic Methods</strong></p> 
        <ul> 
         <li><code>scores</code>&nbsp;generic method added to view scores from select&nbsp;<a href="https://github.com/trinker/qdap" target="_blank">qdap</a>&nbsp;objects.</li> 
         <li><code>counts</code>&nbsp;generic method added to view counts from select&nbsp;<a href="https://github.com/trinker/qdap" target="_blank">qdap</a>&nbsp;objects.</li> 
         <li><code>proportions</code>&nbsp;generic method added to view proportions from select&nbsp;<a href="https://github.com/trinker/qdap" target="_blank">qdap</a>&nbsp;objects.</li> 
         <li><code>preprocessed</code>&nbsp;generic method added to view preprocessed data from select&nbsp;<a href="https://github.com/trinker/qdap" target="_blank">qdap</a>&nbsp;objects.</li> 
        </ul> 
        <p>These methods allow the user to grab particular parts of qdap objects in a consistent fashion. &nbsp;The majority of these methods also pick up a corresponding plot method as well. &nbsp;This adds to the qdap philosophy that data results should be easy to grab and easy to visualize. For instance:</p> 
        <pre class="brush: r; title: ; notranslate" title="">(x &lt;- question_type(DATA.SPLIT$state, DATA.SPLIT$person))

## methods
scores(x)
plot(scores(x))
counts(x)
plot(counts(x))
proportions(x)
plot(proportions(x))
truncdf(preprocessed(x), 15)
plot(preprocessed(x))</pre> 
        <hr> 
        <h2> Demoing Some of the New Features</h2> 
        <p>We’d like to take the time to highlight some of the development that has happened in qdap in the past several months:</p> 
        <h3>Dispersion Plots</h3> 
        <pre class="brush: r; title: ; notranslate" title=""> wrds &lt;- freq_terms(pres_debates2012$dialogue, stopwords = Top200Words)

        <h3>Simple-Quick Boolean Searches</h3> 
        <p>We’ll be demoing this capability on the qdap data set <strong>DATA</strong>:</p> 
        <pre class="brush: r; title: ; notranslate" title=""> ##        person                                 state
## 1         sam         Computer is fun. Not too fun.
## 2        greg               No it's not, it's dumb.
## 3     teacher                    What should we do?
## 4         sam                  You liar, it stinks!
## 5        greg               I am telling the truth!
## 6       sally                How can we be certain?
## 7        greg                      There is no way.
## 8         sam                       I distrust you.
## 9       sally           What are you talking about?
## 10 researcher         Shall we move on?  Good then.
## 11       greg I'm hungry.  Let's eat.  You already? </pre> 
        <p>First a brief explanation from the documentation:</p> 
        <blockquote>
         <p><strong>terms</strong> – A character string(s) to search for. The terms are arranged in a single string with AND (use AND or &amp;&amp; to connect terms together) and OR (use OR or || to allow for searches of either set of terms. Spaces may be used to control what is searched for. For example using ” I ” on c(“I’m”, “I want”, “in”) will result in FALSE TRUE FALSE whereas “I” will match all three (if case is ignored).</p>
        </blockquote> 
        <p>Let’s see how it works. We’ll start with <strong>” I ORliar&amp;&amp;stinks”</strong>. This will find sentences that contain <strong>” I “</strong> or that contain <strong>“liar”</strong> and the word <strong>“stinks”</strong>.</p> 
        <pre class="brush: r; title: ; notranslate" title=""> boolean_search(DATA$state, " I ORliar&amp;&amp;stinks")

## The following elements meet the criteria:
## [1] 4 5 8

boolean_search(DATA$state, " I &amp;&amp;.", values=TRUE)

## The following elements meet the criteria:
## [1] "I distrust you."

boolean_search(DATA$state, " I OR.", values=TRUE)

## The following elements meet the criteria:
## [1] "Computer is fun. Not too fun."        
## [2] "No it's not, it's dumb."              
## [3] "I am telling the truth!"              
## [4] "There is no way."                     
## [5] "I distrust you."                      
## [6] "Shall we move on?  Good then."        
## [7] "I'm hungry.  Let's eat.  You already?"

boolean_search(DATA$state, " I &amp;&amp;.")

## The following elements meet the criteria:
## [1] 8 </pre> 
        <h4> Exclusion as Well </h4> 
        <pre class="brush: r; title: ; notranslate" title="">boolean_search(DATA$state, " I ||.", values=TRUE)

## The following elements meet the criteria:
