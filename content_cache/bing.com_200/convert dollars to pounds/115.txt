    <h4>How <i>pound</i>, the English unit of weight, got its name</h4> 
    <span><img src="images/scale.jpg" alt="Grocer's scale" width="90" height="88" align="left" class="fourpixwrap">In many parts of the world, the English system of measurements is common in everyday life. If you visit a supermarket or grocery store, for instance, you may see prices or costs of fresh produce in pounds or lbs. If you're not familiar with the English unit of weight, here's a brief introduction to the English unit of weight - pound. Pound stands for pound weight or <i>libra pondo</i>. In Latin, <i>libra</i> means weight or balance scale. Thus, pound derived its name from <i>pondo</i>, and the lb notation for pound is an abbreviation of <i>libra</i>. </span>
