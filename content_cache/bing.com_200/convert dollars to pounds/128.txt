       </table><br>PayPal lets you choose what currencies to accept and how you would like to accept them. When a buyer sends a payment in a currency you hold, the funds will automatically appear in your account in the balance of the given currency. When a buyer sends a payment in a currency you do not hold, you will be asked to Accept or Deny the payment as shown below:<br><br><img align="center" src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/en_US/i/demo/demo_mc_3.gif" border="0" alt=""><br><br>
       <ul>
        <li>Accept the payment and open a new currency balance</li>
        <li>Accept the payment and convert it to your primary currency</li>
        <li>Block the payment</li>
       </ul>You may also set up your Payment Receiving Preferences to indicate how you would like to handle incoming currency payments.<br><br><a href="https://www.paypal.com/us/cgi-bin/webscr?cmd=_display-receiving-fees-outside">Receiving fees</a> will be assessed in the currency in which the funds were sent. Payments converted to your primary currency will be converted at a competitive exchange rate. <a href="https://www.paypal.com/us/cgi-bin/webscr?cmd=p/sell/mc/mc_convert-outside">Learn more</a><br><br>To learn more about receiving money in Multiple Currencies, see the <a href="https://www.paypal.com/us/cgi-bin/webscr?cmd=_help-ext&amp;exloc=mcrec&amp;source_page=p%2fsell%2fmc%2fmc%5freceive%2doutside">Multiple Currencies FAQ's</a> in our Help Center.<br><br>If you use Instant Payment Notification (IPN), the <a href="https://www.paypal.com/us/cgi-bin/webscr?cmd=p/acc/ipn-info-outside">IPN page</a> has been updated with information about the new Multiple Currencies variables.<br><br>
