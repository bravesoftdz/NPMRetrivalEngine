         <p><span>One currency can be purchased by another currency through banking institutions or on the open market. The volumes of currencies traded are increased and decreased&nbsp;depending on the attractiveness&nbsp;of any particular currency, which depends on a multitude of factors such as political stability, economic strength, government debt and fiscal policy among others.</span></p> 
         <p>Government<span>&nbsp;central banks also have the ability to set a currency at a constant price through a method called pegging, which essentially tethers the value of one currency to another.
