     <h1 class="titre"><a style="color:#333333" href="http://blog.outwit.com/?p=55" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link: Create your First Web Scraper to Extract Data from a Web Page">Create your First Web Scraper to Extract Data from a Web Page</a></h1> 
     <div class="entry"> 
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;"><em><strong>Important Note:</strong> The tutorials you will find on this blog may become outdated with new versions of the program. We have now added a series of built-in tutorials in the application which are accessible from the Help menu.<br>You should run these to discover the Hub.</em></p> 
      <p></p> 
      <h4 style="text-align: center; color:red;">Find a simple but more up-to-date version of this tutorial <a href="https://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/sample_list.html">here</a></h4> 
      <p><em>This tutorial was created using version 0.8.2. The Scraper Editor interface has changed a long time ago. Many more features were included and some controls now have a new name. The following can still be a good complement to get acquainted with scrapers. The Sraper Editor can now be found in the ‘Scrapers’ view instead of ‘Source’ but the principle remains funamentally the same.</em></p> 
      <p>In many cases the automatic data extraction functions: tables, lists, guess, will be enough and you will manage to extract and export the data in just a few clicks.</p> 
      <p>If, however, the page is too complex, or if your needs are more specific there is a way to extract data manually: Create your own scraper.</p> 
      <p>Scrapers will be saved to your personal database and you will be able to re-apply them on the same URL or on other URLs starting, for instance, with the same domain name.</p> 
      <p>A scraper can even be applied to whole lists of URLs.</p> 
      <p>You can also export your scrapers and share them with other users.</p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal">Let’s get acquainted with this feature by creating a simple one.</p> 
      <p><span id="more-55"></span><br> <strong><span style="font-size: 18pt;">1. </span><a title="http://blog.outwit.com/?p=52#installation" href="http://blog.outwit.com/?p=52#installation" target="_blank">Launch OutWit Hub</a></strong></p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: left;"> </p>
      <p><strong><span style="font-size: 18pt;">2. </span>Choose the Web Page to Scrape </strong></p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: left;">Let’s use this example of an HTML list: <a title="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/GrabDataExample1.html" href="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/GrabDataExample1.html" target="_blank">http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/GrabDataExample1.html</a></p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;"><a title="http://blog.outwit.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/tutorial6_world.png" href="http://blog.outwit.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/tutorial6_world.png" target="_blank"><img class="size-medium wp-image-101 aligncenter" title="tutorial6_world" src="http://blog.outwit.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/tutorial6_world-300x213.png" alt="" width="400" height="284" srcset="http://blog.outwit.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/tutorial6_world-300x213.png 300w, http://blog.outwit.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/tutorial6_world.png 610w" sizes="(max-width: 400px) 100vw, 400px"></a></p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: left;">Type the URL in the address bar.</p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: left;"><a title="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_addressBar2.png" href="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_addressBar2.png" target="_blank"><img src="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_addressBar1.png" alt="" width="452"></a></p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal"> </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal"> </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: left;">In our present example, the data could be extracted&nbsp;simply using&nbsp;the ‘List’ view&nbsp;in the data&nbsp;section.</p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal"> </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p><em>If you don’t see anything in the list view,</em> <em> </em></p> 
       <p><em>reload <img src="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial5_reload.png" alt="" align="middle"> the page.</em></p>
      </blockquote> 
      <blockquote>
       <p><em>In the ‘Lists’ view, like in most other views, right-clicking on selected rows gives you access to a wealth of features to edit and clean the data.</em></p>
      </blockquote> 
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: left;"> </p>
      <p>If the data, as extracted in the list view, is not structured enough for your needs you will have to create a customized scraper for this page.</p> 
      <p>The Scraper Editor is on the right side of the ‘Source’ view, with the colorized HTML source of the page.</p> 
      <p style="text-align: center;"><a title="http://blog.outwit.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/10/image-33.png" href="http://blog.outwit.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/10/image-33.png" target="_blank"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-447" title="image-33" src="http://blog.outwit.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/10/image-33.png" alt="" width="500" height="204" srcset="http://blog.outwit.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/10/image-33.png 1055w, http://blog.outwit.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/10/image-33-300x122.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 500px) 100vw, 500px"></a></p> 
      <p>The text in black is the content actually displayed on the page. This colorization makes it very easy to identify the data you are interested in.</p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal"> </p>
      <p>Building a scraper is simply telling the program what comes immediately before and after the data you want to extract and/or its format.</p> 
      <p>So let’s create a scraper for this list.</p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal"> </p>
      <p>Click on ‘New,’ type in the URL of the page and a name for your new scraper.</p> 
      <p style="text-align: center;"><a title="http://blog.outwit.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/scraper-h.jpg" href="http://blog.outwit.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/scraper-h.jpg" target="_blank"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-722" title="scraper-l" src="http://blog.outwit.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/scraper-l.jpg" alt="" width="450" height="130" srcset="http://blog.outwit.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/scraper-l.jpg 450w, http://blog.outwit.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/scraper-l-300x86.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 450px) 100vw, 450px"></a></p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal"> </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal">Fill the cells with the most logical markers you find around the different pieces of data (don’t look below for the solution… your computer is watching and you would loose ten points.)</p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal"> </p>
      <p>Your first version should logically look like this:</p> 
      <p style="text-align: center;"><a title="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_firstScraper1.png" href="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_firstScraper1.png" target="_blank"><img class="aligncenter" src="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_firstScraper1.png" alt="" width="421" height="207"></a></p> 
      <p>Hit ‘Save,’ and that’s it! You are ready to run your first scraper.</p> 
      <p>If you now go to the ‘Scraper’ view and hit&nbsp;<img src="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial5_reload.png" alt="" align="middle"> refresh, the results are there.</p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal"> </p>
      <p>They are not bad… but not totally satisfying:</p> 
      <p style="text-align: center;"><a title="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_firstScrapResult2.png" href="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_firstScrapResult2.png" target="_blank"><img class="aligncenter" src="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_firstScrapResult1.png" alt="" width="452" height="246"></a></p> 
      <p>The first row contains text instead of the Coordinates, and the City is missing.</p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal"> </p>
      <p>Another look at the source code explains it. The parenthesis ( which is used as the Marker Before Coordinates, appears in a comment hidden in the source code:</p> 
      <p style="text-align: center;"><a title="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_sourceCodeFirst1.png" href="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_sourceCodeFirst1.png" target="_blank"><img class="aligncenter" src="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_sourceCodeFirst1.png" alt="" width="452"></a></p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal"> </p>
      <p>You must, therefore, be a little more precise and define the format of the first character that must be found after the marker.</p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal"> </p>
      <p>Here, a good way is to use the Regular Expression syntax in the Format field. RegExps can become pretty tricky if you need to find complex patterns, but here, what you want to say is simple: “a string that starts with a digit”.</p> 
      <p>For this, you need to type \d.+ (a digit \d, followed by a series of one or more characters .+)</p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;"><a title="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_2ndScraper1.png" href="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_2ndScraper1.png" target="_blank"><img class="aligncenter" src="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_2ndScraper1.png" alt="" width="421" height="207"></a></p> 
      <p>Hit Save.</p> 
      <p>Back to the scraper view, the new result is pretty good.</p> 
      <blockquote>
       <p><em>Reload <img src="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial5_reload.png" alt="" align="middle"> to see the updates.</em></p>
      </blockquote> 
      <p class="MsoNormal"> </p>
      <p>One last problem, though, the first city took its continent along with it…</p> 
      <p style="text-align: center;"><a title="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_2ndResult2.png" href="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_2ndResult2.png" target="_blank"><img class="aligncenter" src="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_2ndResult1.png" alt=""></a></p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal"> </p>
      <p>Let’s have a look at the source code one last time.</p> 
      <p>&lt;li&gt;, our Marker Before City, also appears before the continent.</p> 
      <p style="text-align: center;"><a title="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_sourceCode2nd2.png" href="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_sourceCode2nd2.png" target="_blank"><img class="aligncenter" src="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_sourceCode2nd1.png" alt="" width="452"></a></p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal"> </p>
      <p>A simple way, here, is to select all the characters between the beginning of the line and the city name, and copy them into the scraper editor. It makes the marker more specific, and it will keep working because all cities are at the same indentation level:</p> 
      <p><a title="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_sourceCode3rd.png" href="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_sourceCode3rd.png" target="_blank"><img class="alignnone" src="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_sourceCode3rd.png" alt="" width="170" height="92"></a></p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal"> </p>
      <p>Our final scraper looks like this:</p> 
      <p style="text-align: center;"><a title="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_3rdScraper.png" href="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_3rdScraper.png" target="_blank"><img class="aligncenter" src="http://www.outwit.com/support/help/hub/tutorials/blog_images/tutorial6_3rdScraper.png" alt="" width="421" height="207"></a></p> 
      <blockquote>
       <p><em>Don’t forget to hit ‘Save’ for indeed we did it!</em></p>
      </blockquote> 
      <p class="MsoNormal">OK, the present example is not all that exciting and the figures are already out of date. It would almost be faster to do the 15 rows manually.</p> 
      <p>But, what if the data filled 20 pages and we decided to update the population figures tomorrow?</p> 
      <p>Better: what if the data was changing every morning, like job ads, sport results, or stock market indices?… No problem, you would simply re-apply your new scraper.</p> 
      <p class="MsoNormal"> </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p><em>If you want some tips concerning Scrapers and Regular Expressions, you can refer to the “Help” menu.</em></p>
      </blockquote>
