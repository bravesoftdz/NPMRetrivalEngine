        <a class="big-red-button" href="#seg_337"> <span class="line1">FREE WEATHER GUIDE</span><br> <span class="line2">Click Here to Download</span> </a> 
       </div> 
       <div class="fitVid bottom-24px bound-uls">
        <p>Sri Lanka is a year-round destination with two monsoon seasons so there is a lot of confusion around when is the best time to visit.&nbsp;<span style="line-height: 1.6em;"><span style="line-height: 1.6em;">The weather doesn’t run like clockwork here, and we can always be surprised by the weather in Sri Lanka! However, there will rarely be weather so dramatic that it will stand in the way of a brilliant holiday with plenty of warm weather and sunshine. This is our guide as to when you might like to visit Sri Lanka. For a more in depth breakdown of the best times to travel to Sri Lanka, download our <a href="../../../../weather/when-to-go-sri-lanka/#seg_337">free weather guide</a>.&nbsp;</span></span></p>
       </div> 
       <div class="c-map group"> 
        <img src="/static_files/rspv/imgs/maps/sri-lanka-watercolour.jpg" alt="watercolour map of Sri Lanka"> 
        <div class="c-map-info"> 
         <h2>When to visit <i>(Quick Summary)</i></h2> 
         <h3>Best Time</h3> 
         <p class="group">January - April <i>(Entire Country)</i> <br>Mid-July - September <i>(Entire Country)</i> <br>February - September <i>North and East)</i> </p> 
         <hr> 
         <h3>Monsoon Season</h3> 
         <p class="group">May - July <i>(South-West Monsoon)</i> <br>October - January <i>(North-East Monsoon)</i></p> 
         <hr> 
         <h3>Beach Season</h3> 
         <p class="group">January - April <i>(South)</i> <br>May - July <i>(East)</i></p> 
         <hr> 
         <h3>Wildlife</h3> 
         <p class="group">December - April <i>(Bundala National Park - birds)</i> <br><i>(South coast - whales)</i> <br>November - April <i>(Kalpitya - dolphins)</i> <br> July - September <i>(The Cultural Triangle</i><br><i>- The Gathering of the Elephants)</i> <br><i>(Yala/Wilpattu - leopards)</i> </p> 
         <hr> 
        </div> 
       </div> 
       <div class="grid-3-text group"> 
        <div class="wrapper"> 
         <h2>January - March</h2> 
         <p>A fantastic time to travel across the whole country, Sri Lanka is generally basking in sunshine and relatively dry all over throughout these months. Temperatures will be lovely and warm, although a touch cooler up in the Hill Country. Beaches on the south and west coasts are dry, hot and sunny. </p> 
        </div> 
        <div class="wrapper"> 
         <h2>April</h2> 
         <p>Arguably the driest and hottest month of the year, April starts to get very hot - especially in the central and Cultural Triangle regions with temperatures at least in the mid-30°Cs. It’s a great month to head to the beach, with clear skies and long, sunny days. Make sure to avoid the hottest hours of the day (11am-3pm) when sightseeing during April. </p> 
        </div> 
        <div class="wrapper"> 
         <h2>May - June</h2> 
         <p>The south-west monsoon blows through May and June, but as the name suggests it only affects the south-west quarter of the country such as Colombo and Galle. It is also the more gentle of the two monsoons that Sri Lanka experiences, with mainly short and sharp showers that tend to pass quite quickly.</p> 
        </div> 
       </div> 
       <div class="grid-3-text group"> 
        <div class="wrapper"> 
         <h2>July - September</h2> 
         <p>This is what is known as "inter-monsoon" season, meaning that spare the odd shower, the weather is great at this time of year. Temperatures remain high across the country at around 29°C average and the beaches are hot and dry. All in all, a great period to travel.</p> 
        </div> 
        <div class="wrapper"> 
         <h2>October - November</h2> 
         <p>The north-east monsoon blows in October and November, and it is the heavier of the two monsoons that Sri Lanka experiences. Whilst the name may lend itself to the north-east only, this monsoon does in fact affect the whole country. This monsoon can be unpredictable – it can be stronger, lighter or more sporadic than past years, and sometimes it never comes at all! </p> 
        </div> 
        <div class="wrapper"> 
         <h2>December</h2> 
         <p>December is usually a great month to travel if the monsoon winds down, with nice, hot and dry weather across the island. However, there have been instances in recent years of monsoon rains lasting into December. The rains will also have helped thicken out the lush green landscapes, making it an exceptionally beautiful time to enjoy the rural parts of Sri Lanka. </p> 
        </div>
