             <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
              <p>As follow up to my <a href="https://dsp.stackexchange.com/questions/1522/simplest-way-of-detecting-where-audio-envelopes-start-and-stop">previous question</a> I was wondering if there are any speech detection libraries in existence. By speech detection I mean passing in an audio buffer and getting back an index of where speech starts and stops. So if I have 10 seconds of audio sampling at 44kHz, I would expect an array of numbers such as:</p> 
              <pre><code>44000
88000
123000
190334
...
</code></pre> 
              <p>This would indicate for example that speech starts one second in and then finishes at the two second point, etc.</p> 
              <p>What I'm <strong>not</strong> looking for is speech recognition which writes out text from spoken word. This unfortunately is what I see a lot of when I google 'speech detection'.</p> 
              <p>It would be great if the library was in C, C++ or even Objective-C as I'm writing an app for the iPhone.</p> 
              <p>Thanks!</p>

             <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
              <p>In my <a href="https://dsp.stackexchange.com/a/1543/440">answer</a> to your that question, I had mentioned that Voice Activity Detection is a standard feature for codecs like G.729 and such others. </p> 
              <p>You should look for reference encoders and decoders for algorithms that applies this.</p> 
              <p>One such example is - <a href="http://www.voiceage.com/openinit_g729.php" rel="nofollow noreferrer">http://www.voiceage.com/openinit_g729.php</a></p> 
              <p>Another possible source is Speex codec. Which implements <a href="https://speex.org/docs/manual/speex-manual/node4.html" rel="nofollow noreferrer">VAD</a></p> 
              <p>BTW: You should google "Voice Activity Detection" or "Talk Spurt" rather than "Speech Detection".</p>

              <p>Here you can have a algorithm which is <a href="http://citeseerx.ist.psu.edu/viewdoc/download?doi=10.1.1.176.6740&amp;rep=rep1&amp;type=pdf" rel="nofollow">Adaptive Energy based</a> </p> 
              <p>Small addition to above algorithm when you are calculating for very first time go for taking Mean of Energy and mark as Emin </p> 
              <p>in this the frame you pass is divided in to sub-frames and further you can examine each sub-frame for its activity </p> 
              <p>you can overcome further problems with overlapped frames </p>
